Philosophical Thinking and Peace

This chapter connects Indian philosophical traditions with practical peace-building for elementary schools.

Key Points

  • Philosophy does not only mean abstract theory; for D.El.Ed study it means disciplined thinking about values and conduct.
  • Indian traditions such as Vedanta, Buddhism, Jainism, Bhakti, Sufi thought, and Gandhian philosophy all stress self-control and compassion.
  • Peace begins with internal discipline but becomes meaningful when it appears in family, school, community, and national life.
  • Non-violence is not weakness; it is active courage to resist injustice without hatred.
  • Truthfulness helps children build trust, accurate self-expression, and responsibility for their actions.
  • A teacher can convert philosophy into classroom habits through stories, reflective questions, cooperative work, and service activities.

Indian Philosophical Bases Of Peace

  • Ahimsa teaches respect for life and avoidance of physical, verbal, and emotional harm.
  • Satya teaches honesty in speech, work, assessment, and relationships.
  • Karuna and maitri develop empathy towards classmates, animals, elders, and people in difficulty.
  • Aparigraha and simplicity reduce greed, comparison, and unnecessary competition.
  • Sarva dharma sambhava encourages respect for all faiths and ways of worship.

Gandhiji And Peace Education

  • Gandhiji joined personal character with social action: truth must be practised, not merely recited.
  • Satyagraha means insistence on truth through non-violent methods.
  • Basic education valued productive work, self-reliance, cooperation, and dignity of labour.
  • For school life, Gandhian ideas mean dialogue, cleanliness, craft work, community service, and peaceful problem-solving.

Concepts And Differences

IdeaMeaningExample

Negative peaceOnly absence of open conflict.Students stay silent out of fear.
Positive peacePresence of fairness, empathy, safety, and participation.Students disagree politely and help each other.

Classroom Connection

Use stories, morning meetings, role play, silent reflection, group work, and class duties to make philosophical values visible in everyday behaviour.

  • Positive peace requires justice and cooperation, not merely absence of quarrel.
