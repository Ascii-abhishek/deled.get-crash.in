Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write a short note on Violence.

Violence means Use of force or harm against self, person, group or nature. Example: bullying. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

2. Write a short note on Structural violence.

Structural violence means Unfair system causing deprivation. Example: discrimination. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

3. Write a short note on Media influence.

Media influence means Messages that can normalise aggression or support peace. Example: violent content. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

4. Write a short note on Conflict resolution.

Conflict resolution means Process of solving disagreement peacefully. Example: dialogue and mediation. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

5. Write a short note on Prevention.

Prevention means Reducing causes and creating safe conditions. Example: counselling, rules, empathy. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Define Violence with example.
  • Violence-Causes and Prevention should be studied as a practical chapter for school and society, not as isolated theory.
  • Violence gives the base idea of the chapter and should be linked with examples.
  • Structural violence and Media influence are common short-answer areas.
  • Definitions must be written in simple words with one classroom example.
2. Write short notes on Structural violence and Media influence.
  • Violence gives the base idea of the chapter and should be linked with examples.
  • Structural violence and Media influence are common short-answer areas.
  • Definitions must be written in simple words with one classroom example.
  • For D.El.Ed answers, add child-centred teaching use and value-based application.
3. Differentiate Violence and Structural violence.
  • Violence: Use of force or harm against self, person, group or nature (bullying)
  • Structural violence: Unfair system causing deprivation (discrimination)
  • Media influence: Messages that can normalise aggression or support peace (violent content)
4. Explain classroom use of Violence-Causes and Prevention.
  • Begin with a familiar example from the learner’s home, school or local community.
  • Use pair work, oral practice, chart work and short written reflection.
  • Close the lesson with one application question so learners connect concept with behaviour.
  • End with an evaluation question and quick revision.
