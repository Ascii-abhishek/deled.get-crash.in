Key Points

  • Violence-Causes and Prevention should be studied as a practical chapter for school and society, not as isolated theory.
  • Violence gives the base idea of the chapter and should be linked with examples.
  • Structural violence and Media influence are common short-answer areas.
  • Definitions must be written in simple words with one classroom example.
  • For D.El.Ed answers, add child-centred teaching use and value-based application.
  • Use tables to compare similar terms and avoid confusion in exams.

  • Begin with a familiar example from the learner’s home, school or local community.

One-minute Revision

  • Violence: Use of force or harm against self, person, group or nature.
  • Structural violence: Unfair system causing deprivation.
  • Media influence: Messages that can normalise aggression or support peace.
  • Conflict resolution: Process of solving disagreement peacefully.
  • Prevention: Reducing causes and creating safe conditions.
