Sustainable Development and Environment

This chapter explains sustainable development, environmental balance, resource degradation, and conservation efforts for school practice.

Exam Snapshot

TermLast-minute meaningClassroom or exam example
EnvironmentThe natural and human-made surroundings that affect life.Air, water, soil, plants, animals, school building, and community.
Sustainable developmentDevelopment that meets present needs without harming the ability of future generations to meet their needs.Using water carefully so future students also have safe water.
Resource degradationDecline in quality or quantity of natural resources due to misuse.Soil erosion, polluted water, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity.
ConservationProtection, wise use, and restoration of resources.Tree care, waste segregation, rainwater harvesting, and energy saving.

Key Points

  • Development and environment must be balanced; progress that destroys basic resources becomes harmful.
  • Major environmental problems include pollution, deforestation, soil erosion, climate change, biodiversity loss, and waste mismanagement.
  • Sustainable development includes economic, social, and environmental dimensions.
  • Local communities play a major role in water, forest, soil, sanitation, and energy conservation.
  • International efforts such as environmental conferences, agreements, and Sustainable Development Goals promote shared responsibility.
  • School education can develop habits of reduce, reuse, recycle, repair, respect, and restore.

Dimensions Of Sustainability

  • Environmental: protect air, water, soil, forests, animals, and climate balance.
  • Social: ensure equality, health, education, safety, gender dignity, and participation.
  • Economic: use resources efficiently and support livelihoods without exploitation.
  • Cultural: respect local knowledge, traditional conservation practices, and community cooperation.

School-Level Sustainable Practices

  • Keep a water audit: identify leaking taps and avoid wastage.
  • Make compost from biodegradable waste where possible.
  • Use local plants for biodiversity corners and observation activities.
  • Link lessons with sanitation, nutrition gardens, energy-saving, and community surveys.

Concepts And Differences

IdeaMeaningExample
DevelopmentImprovement in life, services, income, knowledge, and infrastructure.New school building.
Sustainable developmentImprovement with care for environment and future needs.Building with ventilation, water saving, trees, and safe waste disposal.
Renewable resourceResource that can replenish if used wisely.Solar energy and groundwater with recharge.
Non-renewable resourceResource available in limited stock.Coal, petroleum, and many minerals.

Classroom Connection

Use environmental projects: waste mapping, leaf collection, school garden, weather chart, water-saving pledge, community cleanliness walk, and discussion on local environmental problems.

Memory Hook

  • Sustainability asks three questions: Is it useful now? Is it fair to people? Is it safe for nature and the future?
  • Reduce, reuse, recycle, repair, respect, restore.

One-minute Revision

  • Environment: The natural and human-made surroundings that affect life.
  • Sustainable development: Development that meets present needs without harming the ability of future generations to meet their needs.
  • Resource degradation: Decline in quality or quantity of natural resources due to misuse.

Common Exam Lines

  • Sustainable development balances present needs, future generations, social justice, and environmental protection.
  • Resource degradation occurs due to overuse, pollution, deforestation, careless mining, and wasteful consumption.
  • Teachers can promote sustainability through projects, habits, local examples, and community participation.
