Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write a short note on Interpersonal relationship.

Interpersonal relationship means Meaningful relation between two or more persons. Example: peer friendship. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

2. Write a short note on Empathy.

Empathy means Understanding another person’s feelings. Example: listening to a hurt classmate. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

3. Write a short note on Character building.

Character building means Development of honesty, responsibility and self-control. Example: keeping promises. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

4. Write a short note on Moral education.

Moral education means Learning right conduct through values and reflection. Example: fairness. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

5. Write a short note on Pro-social behaviour.

Pro-social behaviour means Helpful and cooperative behaviour. Example: sharing materials. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

3. Differentiate Interpersonal relationship and Empathy.
  • Interpersonal relationship: Meaningful relation between two or more persons (peer friendship)
  • Empathy: Understanding another person’s feelings (listening to a hurt classmate)
  • Character building: Development of honesty, responsibility and self-control (keeping promises)
4. Explain classroom use of Interpersonal Understanding of Relationship.
  • Begin with a familiar example from the learner’s home, school or local community.
  • Use pair work, oral practice, chart work and short written reflection.
  • Close the lesson with one application question so learners connect concept with behaviour.
  • End with an evaluation question and quick revision.
