1. Write the short meaning of Educational Administration.

6. Write one difference related to Administration and Management.

Administration focuses on policy implementation; management focuses on efficient use of resources for goals.

7. Write one difference related to Financial management and Educational management.

Budget estimates future income and expenditure; account records actual transactions.

8. Write one difference related to School record and Stock register.

Record keeping preserves evidence; reporting communicates selected evidence for action.

9. Write one difference related to NCERT and NCTE.

NCERT gives academic support; NCTE regulates teacher education standards.

10. Write one difference related to DIET and BEO.

DIET gives academic support; district offices handle administration and monitoring.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the main concepts of Educational Administration with examples.
  • Educational administration organises people, resources, plans, rules, and community support to achieve school goals.
  • Good administration makes school work purposeful, transparent, timely, and child-centred.
  • Institutional planning should use local needs, enrolment, learning levels, resources, and community support.
  • The head teacher coordinates academic work, records, safety, finance, meetings, and public communication.
  • Classroom example: Prepare a class improvement plan using attendance, reading level, available TLM, and parent meeting dates.
2. Explain the main concepts of Financial, Educational and Time Management with examples.
  • School quality depends on careful financial records, academic planning, timetable design, and balanced co-curricular work.
  • Funds should be used according to rules, school needs, transparency, and child benefit.
  • Financial records need bills, vouchers, stock entries, signatures, and regular review.
  • Academic management includes annual plan, unit plan, remedial teaching, assessment, and follow-up.
  • Classroom example: Use the timetable to place language practice early in the day and remedial mathematics after diagnostic assessment.
3. Explain the main concepts of Records and Disaster Management with examples.
  • School records ensure continuity and accountability, while disaster management keeps children safe before, during, and after emergencies.
  • Important records include admission, attendance, assessment, stock, cash, meeting, inspection, and transfer records.
  • Records should be accurate, updated, secure, accessible to authorised persons, and easy to audit.
  • Disaster management includes risk identification, prevention, preparedness, response, recovery, and review.
  • Classroom example: Practise a silent evacuation drill and then let children draw the safe route from classroom to assembly point.
4. Explain the main concepts of Agencies Working at National and State Level with examples.
  • National and state agencies support curriculum, teacher education, policy, research, distance learning, and school quality.
  • NCERT supports national curriculum, textbooks, research, surveys, and teacher resources.
  • NCTE frames norms and standards for teacher education institutions and programmes.
  • NIEPA works on educational planning, management, leadership, and administration.
  • Classroom example: Use NCERT learning outcomes and SCERT textbook units together while planning monthly class targets.
5. Explain the main concepts of Agencies Working at District and Local Level with examples.
  • District and local agencies connect state policy with actual school support, supervision, training, community participation, and grievance response.
  • DIET supports pre-service training, in-service training, action research, resource development, and academic guidance.
  • District and block education offices monitor enrolment, attendance, grants, inspections, and learning programmes.
  • Local bodies and SMCs help school development planning, community mobilisation, and accountability.
  • Classroom example: Discuss a school attendance problem with SMC members, then plan home visits and child-friendly morning activities.
