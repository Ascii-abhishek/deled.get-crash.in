Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Records and Disaster Management.

School records ensure continuity and accountability, while disaster management keeps children safe before, during, and after emergencies. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to School record and Stock register.

Record keeping preserves evidence; reporting communicates selected evidence for action.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

Important records include admission, attendance, assessment, stock, cash, meeting, inspection, and transfer records. Records should be accurate, updated, secure, accessible to authorised persons, and easy to audit.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Practise a silent evacuation drill and then let children draw the safe route from classroom to assembly point.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Records and Disaster Management with examples.
  • School records ensure continuity and accountability, while disaster management keeps children safe before, during, and after emergencies.
  • Important records include admission, attendance, assessment, stock, cash, meeting, inspection, and transfer records.
  • Records should be accurate, updated, secure, accessible to authorised persons, and easy to audit.
  • Disaster management includes risk identification, prevention, preparedness, response, recovery, and review.
  • Classroom example: Practise a silent evacuation drill and then let children draw the safe route from classroom to assembly point.
2. Explain School record, Stock register, and Disaster.
  • School record: Written or digital evidence of school activity and decisions. Example: Admission register
  • Stock register: Record of materials received, used, and available. Example: Library books and TLM
  • Disaster: Event causing serious disruption, loss, or danger. Example: Fire, flood, earthquake
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Practise a silent evacuation drill and then let children draw the safe route from classroom to assembly point.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Record keeping preserves evidence; reporting communicates selected evidence for action.
  • Preparedness happens before disaster; response happens during and immediately after disaster.
