Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Agencies Working at District and Local Level.

District and local agencies connect state policy with actual school support, supervision, training, community participation, and grievance response. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to DIET and BEO.

DIET gives academic support; district offices handle administration and monitoring.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

DIET supports pre-service training, in-service training, action research, resource development, and academic guidance. District and block education offices monitor enrolment, attendance, grants, inspections, and learning programmes.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Discuss a school attendance problem with SMC members, then plan home visits and child-friendly morning activities.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

An example connects the abstract concept with child experience and makes the answer clear.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Agencies Working at District and Local Level with examples.
  • District and local agencies connect state policy with actual school support, supervision, training, community participation, and grievance response.
  • DIET supports pre-service training, in-service training, action research, resource development, and academic guidance.
  • District and block education offices monitor enrolment, attendance, grants, inspections, and learning programmes.
  • Local bodies and SMCs help school development planning, community mobilisation, and accountability.
  • Classroom example: Discuss a school attendance problem with SMC members, then plan home visits and child-friendly morning activities.
2. Explain DIET, BEO, and SMC.
  • DIET: District Institute of Education and Training for elementary teacher education and academic support. Example: In-service teacher training
  • BEO: Block Education Officer supervising schools at block level. Example: School inspection and support
  • SMC: School Management Committee involving parents and community. Example: School development plan
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Discuss a school attendance problem with SMC members, then plan home visits and child-friendly morning activities.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • DIET gives academic support; district offices handle administration and monitoring.
  • SMC participation is community ownership, not interference in daily teaching.
