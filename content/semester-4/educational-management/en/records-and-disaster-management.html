Records and Disaster Management

School records ensure continuity and accountability, while disaster management keeps children safe before, during, and after emergencies.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
School record Written or digital evidence of school activity and decisions. Admission register
Stock register Record of materials received, used, and available. Library books and TLM
Disaster Event causing serious disruption, loss, or danger. Fire, flood, earthquake
Preparedness Advance planning, training, and resources for safety. Mock drill and exit map
Mitigation Steps that reduce disaster risk and damage. Safe wiring and clear exits

Key Points

  • Important records include admission, attendance, assessment, stock, cash, meeting, inspection, and transfer records.
  • Records should be accurate, updated, secure, accessible to authorised persons, and easy to audit.
  • Disaster management includes risk identification, prevention, preparedness, response, recovery, and review.
  • Schools need evacuation routes, emergency contacts, first-aid box, mock drills, and child-friendly instructions.
  • Teachers must protect children from panic, rumours, crowding, unsafe buildings, and missing-person confusion.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Record keeping preserves evidence; reporting communicates selected evidence for action.
Point 2 Preparedness happens before disaster; response happens during and immediately after disaster.
