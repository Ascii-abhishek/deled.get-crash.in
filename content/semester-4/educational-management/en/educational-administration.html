Educational Administration

Educational administration organises people, resources, plans, rules, and community support to achieve school goals.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Administration Implementation and coordination of policies, rules, and school work. Running admission, records, timetable
Management Planning, organising, directing, and controlling resources. Using teachers and rooms effectively
Institutional planning School-level plan based on needs, resources, and targets. Annual improvement plan
Human resources Teachers, head teacher, staff, students, parents, and community helpers. School management committee
Physical resources Buildings, rooms, furniture, library, lab, playground, and equipment. Safe classroom and drinking water

Key Points

  • Good administration makes school work purposeful, transparent, timely, and child-centred.
  • Institutional planning should use local needs, enrolment, learning levels, resources, and community support.
  • The head teacher coordinates academic work, records, safety, finance, meetings, and public communication.
  • Teachers contribute through class management, records, assessment, child protection, and parent contact.
  • Community participation improves enrolment, attendance, resources, accountability, and trust.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Administration focuses on policy implementation; management focuses on efficient use of resources for goals.
Point 2 Centralised control is uniform but slow; decentralised school planning is local and responsive.
