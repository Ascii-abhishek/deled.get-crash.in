Agencies Working at District and Local Level

District and local agencies connect state policy with actual school support, supervision, training, community participation, and grievance response.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
DIET District Institute of Education and Training for elementary teacher education and academic support. In-service teacher training
BEO Block Education Officer supervising schools at block level. School inspection and support
SMC School Management Committee involving parents and community. School development plan
Basic Education Council Body connected with elementary education administration in the state. Rules and school governance
RTI Right to Information for transparency in public authorities. Asking for public record information

Key Points

  • DIET supports pre-service training, in-service training, action research, resource development, and academic guidance.
  • District and block education offices monitor enrolment, attendance, grants, inspections, and learning programmes.
  • Local bodies and SMCs help school development planning, community mobilisation, and accountability.
  • Basic education rules, service manuals, TET norms, and RTI connect teachers with rights and responsibilities.
  • Effective local coordination reduces dropout, improves attendance, and solves school-level problems faster.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 DIET gives academic support; district offices handle administration and monitoring.
Point 2 SMC participation is community ownership, not interference in daily teaching.
