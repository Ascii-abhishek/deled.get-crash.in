Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Educational Administration.

Educational administration organises people, resources, plans, rules, and community support to achieve school goals. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Administration and Management.

Administration focuses on policy implementation; management focuses on efficient use of resources for goals.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

Good administration makes school work purposeful, transparent, timely, and child-centred. Institutional planning should use local needs, enrolment, learning levels, resources, and community support.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Prepare a class improvement plan using attendance, reading level, available TLM, and parent meeting dates.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

An example connects the abstract concept with child experience and makes the answer clear.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Educational Administration with examples.
  • Educational administration organises people, resources, plans, rules, and community support to achieve school goals.
  • Good administration makes school work purposeful, transparent, timely, and child-centred.
  • Institutional planning should use local needs, enrolment, learning levels, resources, and community support.
  • The head teacher coordinates academic work, records, safety, finance, meetings, and public communication.
  • Classroom example: Prepare a class improvement plan using attendance, reading level, available TLM, and parent meeting dates.
2. Explain Administration, Management, and Institutional planning.
  • Administration: Implementation and coordination of policies, rules, and school work. Example: Running admission, records, timetable
  • Management: Planning, organising, directing, and controlling resources. Example: Using teachers and rooms effectively
  • Institutional planning: School-level plan based on needs, resources, and targets. Example: Annual improvement plan
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Prepare a class improvement plan using attendance, reading level, available TLM, and parent meeting dates.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Administration focuses on policy implementation; management focuses on efficient use of resources for goals.
  • Centralised control is uniform but slow; decentralised school planning is local and responsive.
