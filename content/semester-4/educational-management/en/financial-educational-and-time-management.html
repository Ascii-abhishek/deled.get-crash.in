Financial, Educational and Time Management

School quality depends on careful financial records, academic planning, timetable design, and balanced co-curricular work.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Financial management Planning, using, recording, and auditing school funds. Grant register and vouchers
Educational management Organisation of curriculum, teaching, assessment, and support. Monthly academic plan
Time management Using school time according to priorities and learning needs. Balanced timetable
Co-curricular activities Activities supporting all-round development beyond textbook teaching. Sports, debate, art
Monitoring Regular observation and review for improvement. Checking lesson completion and attendance

Key Points

  • Funds should be used according to rules, school needs, transparency, and child benefit.
  • Financial records need bills, vouchers, stock entries, signatures, and regular review.
  • Academic management includes annual plan, unit plan, remedial teaching, assessment, and follow-up.
  • Timetable should balance subjects, teacher workload, age level, local conditions, and activity time.
  • Co-curricular activities should support values, health, creativity, citizenship, and confidence.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
