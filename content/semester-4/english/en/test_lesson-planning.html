Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write a short note on Lesson plan.

Lesson plan means Teacher prepared outline of teaching-learning steps. Example: Prose lesson plan. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

2. Write a short note on Learning objective.

Learning objective means Expected student behaviour after lesson. Example: Students will identify nouns. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

3. Write a short note on Teaching aids.

Teaching aids means Materials supporting teaching. Example: flash cards, chart. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

4. Write a short note on Evaluation.

Evaluation means Checking learning during and after lesson. Example: oral questions. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

5. Write a short note on Blackboard work.

Blackboard work means Planned board summary and examples. Example: new words and structure. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Define Lesson plan with example.
  • Lesson Planning should be studied as a practical chapter for school and society, not as isolated theory.
  • Lesson plan gives the base idea of the chapter and should be linked with examples.
  • Learning objective and Teaching aids are common short-answer areas.
  • Definitions must be written in simple words with one classroom example.
2. Write short notes on Learning objective and Teaching aids.
  • Lesson plan gives the base idea of the chapter and should be linked with examples.
  • Learning objective and Teaching aids are common short-answer areas.
  • Definitions must be written in simple words with one classroom example.
  • For D.El.Ed answers, add child-centred teaching use and value-based application.
3. Differentiate Lesson plan and Learning objective.
  • Lesson plan: Teacher prepared outline of teaching-learning steps (Prose lesson plan)
  • Learning objective: Expected student behaviour after lesson (Students will identify nouns)
  • Teaching aids: Materials supporting teaching (flash cards, chart)
4. Explain classroom use of Lesson Planning.
  • Begin with a familiar example from the learner’s home, school or local community.
  • Use pair work, oral practice, chart work and short written reflection.
  • Close the lesson with one application question so learners connect concept with behaviour.
  • End with an evaluation question and quick revision.
