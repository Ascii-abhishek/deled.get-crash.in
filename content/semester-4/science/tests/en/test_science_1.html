Science: Test 1

Mixed subject-level practice from Evolution and Ecosystem plus Minerals and Metals.

Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. Which sequence correctly shows energy flow in a simple food chain?

2. Which one is an example of an alloy?

3. What happens to number and energy at higher levels of an ecological pyramid?

Section 1 Answers
  1. A: "Sun -> grass -> grasshopper -> frog" Energy enters through sunlight, is trapped by producers, and then passes to consumers.
  2. D: "Brass" Brass is an alloy, commonly made from copper and zinc.
  3. C: "They generally decrease" Energy is lost at each transfer, so higher trophic levels usually have less energy and fewer organisms.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Why are producers placed at the base of an ecological pyramid?

Producers are placed at the base because they make food by photosynthesis and support all consumer levels above them. They are usually greater in number and energy than higher levels.

2. Why is concentration important in metallurgy?

Concentration removes unwanted impurities or gangue from ore. This makes later extraction steps easier and more efficient.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Compare the role of decomposers in ecosystem with the role of refining in metallurgy.

Decomposers clean and recycle matter in an ecosystem by breaking dead organisms and returning nutrients to soil. Refining purifies crude metal by removing unwanted impurities after extraction. Both processes improve usefulness: decomposers maintain nutrient cycling for living systems, while refining makes metal suitable for tools, wires, utensils, and other uses.
