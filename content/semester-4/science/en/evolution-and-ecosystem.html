Evolution: Remember in 5 Points

  • Evolution means gradual change in organisms over a long time.
  • It happens generation after generation, not in one lifetime.
  • Helpful features are called adaptations.
  • Organisms with useful adaptations survive and reproduce better.
  • Evolution explains diversity of life: bacteria, plants, animals, and humans are different because life changed over time.

   Biotic components          Abiotic components
   living things              non-living things
        |                           |
 Plants, animals, microbes    air, water, soil, sunlight

Components of Ecosystem

Component Role Easy clue
Producers Make food by photosynthesis Green plants
Primary consumers Eat producers Goat, deer, grasshopper
Secondary consumers Eat primary consumers Frog, small fish
Tertiary consumers Top-level consumers Snake, eagle
Decomposers Break dead matter and return nutrients Bacteria, fungi

Food Chain

  • A food chain shows who eats whom.
  • Energy starts mainly from the Sun.
  • Green plants trap solar energy and make food.
  • Energy passes from producer to consumer.
  • At every step, some energy is lost as heat.
Sun -> Grass -> Grasshopper -> Frog -> Snake -> Eagle
       Producer   Primary      Secondary  Tertiary
                  consumer     consumer   consumer

Food Web

  • A food web is a network of food chains.
  • It is more realistic than a single food chain.
  • One animal may eat many things and may also be eaten by many animals.
  • If one organism disappears, the whole web may be disturbed.
Grass -> Grasshopper -> Frog -> Snake
  |           |            |
  v           v            v
Goat       Small bird -> Eagle

Ecological Pyramid

        Top consumers
             /\

     Primary consumers
       /            \
 Producers: plants and algae
  • Base is broad because producers are maximum.
  • Number and energy usually decrease at higher levels.
  • This is why top carnivores are fewer than herbivores.

Real-world Classroom Example

  • Take a school garden.
  • Producers: grass, neem plant, flowering plants.
  • Primary consumers: ants, butterflies, goats.
  • Secondary consumers: lizards, birds.
  • Decomposers: fungi and bacteria in soil.
  • Ask children to draw arrows from food to eater.

One-minute Revision

  • Evolution = slow change in living things over generations.
  • Ecosystem = biotic + abiotic interaction.
  • Producers make food; consumers eat; decomposers recycle nutrients.
  • Food chain is linear; food web is interconnected.
  • Ecological pyramid shows decreasing number or energy at higher trophic levels.

Common Exam Lines

  • An ecosystem is a functional unit of nature where living and non-living components interact.
  • Food chain represents the transfer of food energy from producer to consumers.
  • Decomposers maintain nutrient cycling in the ecosystem.
