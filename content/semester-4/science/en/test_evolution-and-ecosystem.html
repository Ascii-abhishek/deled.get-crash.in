Test Yourself: Evolution and Ecosystem

Test 1 covers definitions, ecosystem components, food chains, food webs, and ecological pyramids.

Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. Which pair correctly shows biotic and abiotic components?

2. In a food chain, energy usually starts from which source?

3. Which group returns nutrients to the soil by breaking dead matter?

Section 1 Answers
  1. B: "Plants and sunlight" Plants are living, so they are biotic. Sunlight is non-living, so it is abiotic.
  2. A: "Sun" Green plants trap solar energy and make food. This energy then moves to consumers.
  3. D: "Decomposers" Bacteria and fungi break dead matter and return nutrients to the ecosystem.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the meaning of ecosystem.

An ecosystem is a functional unit where living components such as plants, animals, and microbes interact with non-living components such as air, water, soil, sunlight, and temperature.

2. Give one difference between food chain and food web.

A food chain shows a single path of energy transfer, while a food web shows many connected food chains and is closer to real nature.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the components of an ecosystem with a school garden example.

An ecosystem has biotic and abiotic components. In a school garden, grass and flowering plants are producers, insects and goats may be primary consumers, birds and lizards may be secondary consumers, and bacteria and fungi act as decomposers. Abiotic components include sunlight, air, water, soil, and temperature. Together these components exchange energy and matter.
