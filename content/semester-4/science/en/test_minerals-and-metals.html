Test Yourself: Minerals and Metals

Test 1 covers minerals, ores, gangue, metallurgy, extraction steps, and properties of metals and non-metals.

Section 1: Multiple Choice

1. Which statement best defines an ore?

2. What is gangue?

3. Which property means a metal can be drawn into wires?

Section 1 Answers
  1. C: "A mineral from which metal can be extracted profitably" All ores are minerals, but only useful and economical minerals are called ores.
  2. B: "Unwanted earthy impurities in ore" Gangue includes unwanted sand, clay, or rocky material mixed with ore.
  3. A: "Ductility" Ductility is the property by which metals can be drawn into wires, such as copper wires.

Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write two differences between minerals and ores.

A mineral is a naturally occurring substance in the earth's crust, while an ore is a mineral from which metal can be extracted profitably. All ores are minerals, but all minerals are not ores.

2. Name the main steps in extraction of metals.

The main steps are mining, crushing and grinding, concentration to remove gangue, reduction to obtain crude metal, and refining to purify the metal.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the major physical properties of metals and non-metals.

Metals are usually shiny, good conductors of heat and electricity, malleable, ductile, and sonorous. Examples are iron, copper, and aluminium. Non-metals are usually dull, poor conductors, brittle when solid, non-malleable, non-ductile, and non-sonorous. Examples are carbon, sulphur, and oxygen. Important exceptions should be remembered: graphite conducts electricity, iodine is lustrous, and mercury is a liquid metal.
