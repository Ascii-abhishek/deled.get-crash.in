Minerals and Metals

This chapter is scoring because definitions and differences repeat often. Learn the flow: mineral -> ore -> extraction -> metal use.

Exam Snapshot

Term Fast meaning Example
Mineral Naturally occurring substance found in earth Bauxite, hematite, limestone
Ore Mineral from which metal can be extracted profitably Hematite for iron, bauxite for aluminium
Metallurgy Process of extracting metals from ores Crushing, concentration, reduction, refining
Gangue Unwanted earthy impurities in ore Sand, clay, rocky material
Alloy Mixture of metal with metal or non-metal Steel, brass, bronze

Mineral vs Ore

Mineral Ore
Natural substance in earth crust Mineral useful for extracting metal
May or may not contain enough metal Contains enough metal for profitable extraction
All minerals are not ores All ores are minerals
Example: clay, quartz, bauxite Bauxite is ore of aluminium

Steps in Simple Words

  • Mining: ore is taken out from earth.
  • Crushing: large pieces are broken into small pieces.
  • Concentration: impurities like soil and sand are removed.
  • Reduction: oxygen or other combined elements are removed to get metal.
  • Refining: crude metal is purified for use.

Metals and Non-metals

Property Metals Non-metals
Lustre Usually shiny Usually dull
Conductivity Good conductor of heat and electricity Poor conductor, except graphite
Malleability Can be beaten into sheets Brittle, not malleable
Ductility Can be drawn into wires Not ductile
Sound Sonorous Non-sonorous
Common state Mostly solid Solid, liquid, or gas
Examples Iron, copper, aluminium Carbon, sulphur, oxygen

Remember These Exceptions

  • Mercury is a liquid metal.
  • Graphite is a non-metal but conducts electricity.
  • Iodine is a lustrous non-metal.
  • Sodium and potassium are soft metals.

Real-world Classroom Example

  • Show an iron nail, copper wire, aluminium foil, pencil graphite, and a piece of chalk.
  • Ask students to sort them into metal and non-metal.
  • Let them check shine, sound, bending, and use.
  • Connect the activity with properties instead of memorising only names.

Quick Difference: Physical and Chemical Clues

  • Physical clues: shine, hardness, sound, bending into sheets, drawing into wires.
  • Chemical clue: metals usually form basic oxides; many non-metals form acidic oxides.
  • For D.El.Ed level, focus on observable properties first.

One-minute Revision

  • Ore is a useful mineral from which metal is extracted economically.
  • Gangue means unwanted impurities.
  • Extraction usually follows mining, crushing, concentration, reduction, and refining.
  • Metals are usually lustrous, malleable, ductile, sonorous, and good conductors.
  • Non-metals usually lack these metallic properties, with a few exceptions.

Common Exam Lines

  • All ores are minerals, but all minerals are not ores.
  • The removal of gangue from ore is called concentration of ore.
  • Metals are used widely because they are strong, conductive, and can be shaped.
