Study of Sound

Sound study builds listening, phonemic awareness, pronunciation, rhythm, and expressive oral language.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Sound Smallest audible unit used in speech. The sound /क/ in कमल
Pronunciation Correct production of speech sounds. Clear difference between श and स
Rhythm Pattern of stress, pause, and flow in speech or poem. Clapping with a rhyme
Phonemic awareness Ability to hear and manipulate sounds in words. Finding words that start with म
Listening discrimination Identifying similarity and difference between sounds. ब and व distinction

Key Points

  • Listening comes before formal reading and supports correct pronunciation.
  • Poems, rhymes, folk songs, and stories develop sound patterns naturally.
  • Children need repeated but joyful practice for difficult sounds and blends.
  • Teacher modelling, mirror practice, clapping, and oral games improve speech clarity.
  • Regional pronunciation should be respected while standard pronunciation is gently introduced.
