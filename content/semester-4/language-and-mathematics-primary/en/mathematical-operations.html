Mathematical Operations

Operations grow from concrete actions: joining, separating, repeated addition, sharing, grouping, zero, and place value.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Addition Combining quantities to find total. 3 mangoes and 2 mangoes make 5
Subtraction Taking away or comparing to find difference. 7 pencils minus 3 pencils
Multiplication Repeated addition or equal groups. 4 groups of 3
Division Equal sharing or grouping. 12 sweets shared among 4 children
Place value Value of a digit according to its place. 5 in 52 means 5 tens

Key Points

  • Begin with concrete material, then pictures, then symbols.
  • Addition and subtraction should include joining, taking away, comparison, and missing-number situations.
  • Multiplication should begin with equal groups, arrays, skip counting, and repeated addition.
  • Division should include equal sharing and equal grouping.
  • Place value with ones, tens, and hundreds is essential for carrying and borrowing.
