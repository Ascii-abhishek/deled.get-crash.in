Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Study of Sound.

Sound study builds listening, phonemic awareness, pronunciation, rhythm, and expressive oral language. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Sound and Pronunciation.

Hearing is physical reception of sound; listening is attentive understanding.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

Listening comes before formal reading and supports correct pronunciation. Poems, rhymes, folk songs, and stories develop sound patterns naturally.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Play a sound hunt: children find classroom words beginning with क, clap syllables, and recite a short rhyme.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

An example connects the abstract concept with child experience and makes the answer clear.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Study of Sound with examples.
  • Sound study builds listening, phonemic awareness, pronunciation, rhythm, and expressive oral language.
  • Listening comes before formal reading and supports correct pronunciation.
  • Poems, rhymes, folk songs, and stories develop sound patterns naturally.
  • Children need repeated but joyful practice for difficult sounds and blends.
  • Classroom example: Play a sound hunt: children find classroom words beginning with क, clap syllables, and recite a short rhyme.
2. Explain Sound, Pronunciation, and Rhythm.
  • Sound: Smallest audible unit used in speech. Example: The sound /क/ in कमल
  • Pronunciation: Correct production of speech sounds. Example: Clear difference between श and स
  • Rhythm: Pattern of stress, pause, and flow in speech or poem. Example: Clapping with a rhyme
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Play a sound hunt: children find classroom words beginning with क, clap syllables, and recite a short rhyme.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Hearing is physical reception of sound; listening is attentive understanding.
  • Pronunciation accuracy is clarity, not artificial accent.
