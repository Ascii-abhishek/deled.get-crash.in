Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Reading and Writing in the Pure Form of Script.

Script learning includes letter recognition, matras, punctuation, spacing, directionality, nasal sounds, and meaningful writing practice. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Script and Punctuation.

Copying trains form; independent writing trains thought and expression.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

Children need large-to-small movement practice before neat notebook writing. Letter shape, direction, matra placement, spacing, and punctuation should be taught together with meaning.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Create a class word wall from children's names, local objects, and story words; use it for reading and sentence writing.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

An example connects the abstract concept with child experience and makes the answer clear.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Reading and Writing in the Pure Form of Script with examples.
  • Script learning includes letter recognition, matras, punctuation, spacing, directionality, nasal sounds, and meaningful writing practice.
  • Children need large-to-small movement practice before neat notebook writing.
  • Letter shape, direction, matra placement, spacing, and punctuation should be taught together with meaning.
  • Reading and writing should use familiar words, labels, names, and short messages.
  • Classroom example: Create a class word wall from children's names, local objects, and story words; use it for reading and sentence writing.
2. Explain Script, Punctuation, and Matra.
  • Script: System of written symbols used to represent language. Example: Devanagari
  • Punctuation: Marks that show pause, question, emotion, or structure. Example: Full stop and question mark
  • Matra: Vowel sign attached to consonants in Devanagari. Example: कि, की, कु
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Create a class word wall from children's names, local objects, and story words; use it for reading and sentence writing.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Copying trains form; independent writing trains thought and expression.
  • Punctuation guides meaning, not only decoration of sentences.
