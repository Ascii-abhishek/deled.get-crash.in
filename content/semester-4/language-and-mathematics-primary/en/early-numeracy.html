Early Numeracy

Early numeracy builds number sense through counting, one-to-one correspondence, comparison, ordering, grouping, and symbols.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Number sense Understanding quantity, order, and relation of numbers. Knowing 8 is more than 5
One-to-one correspondence Matching one count word with one object. Counting five seeds one by one
Cardinality Understanding the last count tells total quantity. One, two, three: there are three balls
Sequencing Arranging objects or numbers in order. 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
Conservation of number Understanding quantity stays same when arrangement changes. Five sticks spread out are still five

Key Points

  • Children first build quantity ideas through objects, movement, stories, and games.
  • Rote counting is not enough; learners must connect number words, objects, and symbols.
  • Numbers 1 to 9 should be taught through grouping, comparison, and local examples.
  • Zero should be introduced as none, empty set, and a placeholder in later place value.
  • Assessment should use activities: give, match, compare, arrange, and explain.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Counting is saying number names; number sense is understanding quantity and relation.
Point 2 More/less compares quantity; before/after compares order.
