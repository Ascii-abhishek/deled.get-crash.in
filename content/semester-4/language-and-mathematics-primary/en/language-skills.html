Language Skills

Language learning develops listening, speaking, reading, and writing through meaningful use of sounds, words, sentences, and texts.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Listening Understanding spoken language with attention and meaning. Following a story or instruction
Speaking Expressing thoughts orally with clarity and confidence. Narrating a local festival
Reading Making meaning from written symbols and text. Reading a short poem
Writing Expressing ideas through script, words, and sentences. Writing two lines about a picture
Vocabulary Words known and used by a learner. Names of classroom objects

Key Points

  • The four skills develop together; oral language supports reading and writing.
  • Children learn language best through stories, songs, conversation, play, and purposeful print.
  • Letters should be linked with sounds and meaningful words, not taught as isolated marks only.
  • Errors are clues about learning and should guide support instead of punishment.
  • Multilingual children need respect for home language while gradually building school language.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Decoding means reading symbols aloud; comprehension means understanding the message.
Point 2 Handwriting is form; writing skill is meaningful expression.
