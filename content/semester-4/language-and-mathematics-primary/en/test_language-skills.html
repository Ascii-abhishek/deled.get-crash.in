Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Language Skills.

Language learning develops listening, speaking, reading, and writing through meaningful use of sounds, words, sentences, and texts. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Listening and Speaking.

Decoding means reading symbols aloud; comprehension means understanding the message.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

The four skills develop together; oral language supports reading and writing. Children learn language best through stories, songs, conversation, play, and purposeful print.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Use a picture of a market: children listen to a story, name objects, read labels, and write two sentences.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Language Skills with examples.
  • Language learning develops listening, speaking, reading, and writing through meaningful use of sounds, words, sentences, and texts.
  • The four skills develop together; oral language supports reading and writing.
  • Children learn language best through stories, songs, conversation, play, and purposeful print.
  • Letters should be linked with sounds and meaningful words, not taught as isolated marks only.
  • Classroom example: Use a picture of a market: children listen to a story, name objects, read labels, and write two sentences.
2. Explain Listening, Speaking, and Reading.
  • Listening: Understanding spoken language with attention and meaning. Example: Following a story or instruction
  • Speaking: Expressing thoughts orally with clarity and confidence. Example: Narrating a local festival
  • Reading: Making meaning from written symbols and text. Example: Reading a short poem
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Use a picture of a market: children listen to a story, name objects, read labels, and write two sentences.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Decoding means reading symbols aloud; comprehension means understanding the message.
  • Handwriting is form; writing skill is meaningful expression.
