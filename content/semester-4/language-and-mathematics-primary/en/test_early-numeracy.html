Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Early Numeracy.

Early numeracy builds number sense through counting, one-to-one correspondence, comparison, ordering, grouping, and symbols. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Number sense and One-to-one correspondence.

Counting is saying number names; number sense is understanding quantity and relation.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

Children first build quantity ideas through objects, movement, stories, and games. Rote counting is not enough; learners must connect number words, objects, and symbols.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Use seeds, bottle caps, and attendance sticks for counting, matching, making groups, and comparing numbers.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

An example connects the abstract concept with child experience and makes the answer clear.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Early Numeracy with examples.
  • Early numeracy builds number sense through counting, one-to-one correspondence, comparison, ordering, grouping, and symbols.
  • Children first build quantity ideas through objects, movement, stories, and games.
  • Rote counting is not enough; learners must connect number words, objects, and symbols.
  • Numbers 1 to 9 should be taught through grouping, comparison, and local examples.
  • Classroom example: Use seeds, bottle caps, and attendance sticks for counting, matching, making groups, and comparing numbers.
2. Explain Number sense, One-to-one correspondence, and Cardinality.
  • Number sense: Understanding quantity, order, and relation of numbers. Example: Knowing 8 is more than 5
  • One-to-one correspondence: Matching one count word with one object. Example: Counting five seeds one by one
  • Cardinality: Understanding the last count tells total quantity. Example: One, two, three: there are three balls
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Use seeds, bottle caps, and attendance sticks for counting, matching, making groups, and comparing numbers.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Counting is saying number names; number sense is understanding quantity and relation.
  • More/less compares quantity; before/after compares order.
