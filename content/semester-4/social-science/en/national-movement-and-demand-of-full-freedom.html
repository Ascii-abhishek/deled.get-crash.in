National Movement and Demand of Full Freedom

The Indian national movement moved from early constitutional politics to mass movements and the demand for complete independence.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Indian National Congress Political organisation founded in 1885 that became a major platform of nationalism. Annual sessions and resolutions
Swadeshi Movement Movement encouraging boycott of foreign goods and use of Indian goods. After Bengal partition
Rowlatt Act Repressive law allowing detention without trial. Opposed by Gandhi
Non-Cooperation Mass movement withdrawing cooperation from British institutions. Boycott of titles and schools
Purna Swaraj Complete independence declared as national goal in 1929. Lahore session

Key Points

  • Early nationalists used petitions, speeches, resolutions, and constitutional methods.
  • Bengal partition, Swadeshi, revolutionary activities, and press spread stronger nationalism.
  • Gandhian movements used satyagraha, non-cooperation, boycott, civil disobedience, and constructive work.
  • Events such as Jallianwala Bagh, Khilafat, Chauri Chaura, Kakori, Simon Commission, Bardoli, and Nehru Report shaped the movement.
  • The demand for Purna Swaraj gave the movement a clear goal of complete independence.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Moderates relied on constitutional requests; extremists emphasised boycott, swadeshi, and stronger agitation.
Point 2 Swaraj could mean self-government; Purna Swaraj meant complete independence.
