Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write a short note on Food security.

Food security means Availability, access and absorption of food for all. Example: Enough nutritious food. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

2. Write a short note on PDS.

PDS means Public Distribution System for subsidised food grains. Example: Ration shops. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

3. Write a short note on FCI.

FCI means Agency for procurement, storage and distribution of food grains. Example: Buffer stock. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

4. Write a short note on ICDS.

ICDS means Integrated Child Development Services. Example: Anganwadi nutrition. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

5. Write a short note on Globalisation.

Globalisation means Integration of national economy with world economy. Example: Trade, technology, investment. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Define food security and its pillars.
  • Food security needs food production, purchasing power, fair distribution and nutrition awareness.
  • PDS provides grains to eligible households through ration shops.
  • FCI procures grains at support prices, stores buffer stock and supplies PDS.
  • ICDS and mid-day meal support child nutrition and school participation.
2. Explain the role of FCI.
  • PDS provides grains to eligible households through ration shops.
  • FCI procures grains at support prices, stores buffer stock and supplies PDS.
  • ICDS and mid-day meal support child nutrition and school participation.
  • The food security law gives legal entitlement to subsidised grains for eligible people.
3. Write a note on PDS.
  • Availability: Food exists in the country or region (Production, imports, buffer stock)
  • Access: People can obtain food (Income, prices, PDS)
  • Absorption: Body can use nutrition (Health, sanitation, clean water)
4. How do ICDS and mid-day meal help children?
  • Use a food plate activity to discuss cereals, pulses, milk, fruits and vegetables.
  • Link school mid-day meal with attendance, nutrition and equality.
  • Compare local market food with packaged global products to discuss globalisation.
  • End with an evaluation question and quick revision.
