Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Freedom Movement of 1857 and Reform Movements with examples.
  • The revolt of 1857 and nineteenth-century reform movements shaped anti-colonial consciousness and social change in India.
  • Causes of 1857 included political annexation, economic exploitation, military discrimination, religious fear, and immediate cartridge issue.
  • Important centres included Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur, Lucknow, Jhansi, Bareilly, and parts of Bihar.
  • Effects included end of Company rule, transfer to the Crown, army reorganisation, and cautious British policy toward princes.
  • Classroom example: Use a timeline with reformers and revolt centres, then ask children to locate Jhansi, Delhi, and Lucknow on a map.
2. Explain Revolt of 1857, Doctrine of Lapse, and Social reform.
  • Revolt of 1857: Widespread uprising against British rule involving soldiers, rulers, peasants, and citizens. Example: Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur, Jhansi
  • Doctrine of Lapse: British annexation policy affecting Indian states without accepted heirs. Example: Jhansi grievance
  • Social reform: Effort to remove social evils and promote equality, education, and rational thinking. Example: Campaign against sati
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Use a timeline with reformers and revolt centres, then ask children to locate Jhansi, Delhi, and Lucknow on a map.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • 1857 was mainly an armed revolt; reform movements were social, religious, and educational efforts.
  • Political causes challenged British power; social reform challenged internal social evils.
