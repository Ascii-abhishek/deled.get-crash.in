Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Environmental Pollution and United Nations Organisation with examples.
  • Environmental pollution harms air, water, soil, sound balance, and health, while the United Nations supports international peace and cooperation.
  • Pollution may be air, water, soil, noise, thermal, radioactive, or solid-waste related.
  • Causes include vehicles, industries, burning waste, sewage, chemicals, plastics, and careless resource use.
  • Effects include disease, biodiversity loss, climate problems, crop damage, dirty water, and reduced quality of life.
  • Classroom example: Conduct a school waste audit, classify waste, plan reduction steps, and connect it with global responsibility.
2. Explain Pollution, Air pollution, and Water pollution.
  • Pollution: Harmful change in environment due to unwanted substances or energy. Example: Smoke in air
  • Air pollution: Contamination of air by smoke, dust, gases, or particles. Example: Vehicle emissions
  • Water pollution: Contamination of water bodies by waste or chemicals. Example: Untreated sewage in river
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Conduct a school waste audit, classify waste, plan reduction steps, and connect it with global responsibility.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Pollutant is the harmful substance; pollution is the harmful condition created by it.
  • UNO is a global organisation; its specialised agencies work in specific fields such as health, education, and children.
