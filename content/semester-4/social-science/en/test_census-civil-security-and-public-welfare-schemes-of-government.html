Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write a short note on Census.

Census means Official count of population and its social-economic features. Example: Age, literacy, occupation. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

2. Write a short note on Civil security.

Civil security means Protection of citizens during war, disaster or emergency. Example: Mock drill and shelter plan. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

3. Write a short note on Welfare scheme.

Welfare scheme means Government support for public well-being. Example: PDS, MGNREGA, pensions. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

4. Write a short note on NGO.

NGO means Non-government voluntary organisation. Example: Health camp, awareness drive. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

5. Write a short note on Social audit.

Social audit means Public checking of scheme use. Example: Gram Sabha review. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Define census and write its uses.
  • Census data helps planning for schools, roads, hospitals, ration shops and employment programmes.
  • Civil security includes warning, rescue, first aid, evacuation and rehabilitation.
  • Welfare schemes try to reduce poverty, hunger, unemployment and social exclusion.
  • Public distribution, mid-day meal, ICDS, pensions and scholarships are common welfare examples.
2. Explain civil security with examples.
  • Civil security includes warning, rescue, first aid, evacuation and rehabilitation.
  • Welfare schemes try to reduce poverty, hunger, unemployment and social exclusion.
  • Public distribution, mid-day meal, ICDS, pensions and scholarships are common welfare examples.
  • NGOs support government work through awareness, training, surveys and community mobilisation.
3. Write notes on major welfare schemes.
  • Census: Collects facts about people (Planning and resource allocation)
  • Civil security: Protects life and property (Disaster and emergency preparedness)
  • Welfare scheme: Delivers benefits (Social justice and basic needs)
