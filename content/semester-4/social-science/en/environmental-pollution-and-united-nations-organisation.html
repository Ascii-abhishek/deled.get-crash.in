Environmental Pollution and United Nations Organisation

Environmental pollution harms air, water, soil, sound balance, and health, while the United Nations supports international peace and cooperation.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Pollution Harmful change in environment due to unwanted substances or energy. Smoke in air
Air pollution Contamination of air by smoke, dust, gases, or particles. Vehicle emissions
Water pollution Contamination of water bodies by waste or chemicals. Untreated sewage in river
Noise pollution Unwanted or excessive sound harmful to health. Loudspeakers near school
United Nations International organisation formed to promote peace, security, cooperation, and human welfare. UN agencies and programmes

Key Points

  • Pollution may be air, water, soil, noise, thermal, radioactive, or solid-waste related.
  • Causes include vehicles, industries, burning waste, sewage, chemicals, plastics, and careless resource use.
  • Effects include disease, biodiversity loss, climate problems, crop damage, dirty water, and reduced quality of life.
  • Control needs reduce-reuse-recycle, clean energy, public transport, sanitation, tree plantation, law, and citizen action.
  • The United Nations works through agencies and programmes for peace, health, education, children, environment, and development.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Pollutant is the harmful substance; pollution is the harmful condition created by it.
Point 2 UNO is a global organisation; its specialised agencies work in specific fields such as health, education, and children.
