Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of National Movement and Demand of Full Freedom.

The Indian national movement moved from early constitutional politics to mass movements and the demand for complete independence. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Indian National Congress and Swadeshi Movement.

Moderates relied on constitutional requests; extremists emphasised boycott, swadeshi, and stronger agitation.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

Early nationalists used petitions, speeches, resolutions, and constitutional methods. Bengal partition, Swadeshi, revolutionary activities, and press spread stronger nationalism.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of National Movement and Demand of Full Freedom with examples.
  • The Indian national movement moved from early constitutional politics to mass movements and the demand for complete independence.
  • Early nationalists used petitions, speeches, resolutions, and constitutional methods.
  • Bengal partition, Swadeshi, revolutionary activities, and press spread stronger nationalism.
  • Gandhian movements used satyagraha, non-cooperation, boycott, civil disobedience, and constructive work.
  • Classroom example: Let groups create event cards from 1885 to 1930 and arrange them as a human timeline.
2. Explain Indian National Congress, Swadeshi Movement, and Rowlatt Act.
  • Indian National Congress: Political organisation founded in 1885 that became a major platform of nationalism. Example: Annual sessions and resolutions
  • Swadeshi Movement: Movement encouraging boycott of foreign goods and use of Indian goods. Example: After Bengal partition
  • Rowlatt Act: Repressive law allowing detention without trial. Example: Opposed by Gandhi
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.

  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Moderates relied on constitutional requests; extremists emphasised boycott, swadeshi, and stronger agitation.
  • Swaraj could mean self-government; Purna Swaraj meant complete independence.
