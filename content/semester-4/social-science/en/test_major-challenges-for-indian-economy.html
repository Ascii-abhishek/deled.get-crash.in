Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write a short note on Poverty.

Poverty means Inability to meet minimum needs. Example: Food, shelter, health, education. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

2. Write a short note on Unemployment.

Unemployment means Willing workers not getting work. Example: Educated unemployment. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

3. Write a short note on Population pressure.

Population pressure means Rapid population growth increasing demand on resources. Example: Pressure on land and jobs. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

4. Write a short note on Literacy.

Literacy means Ability to read and write with understanding. Example: Basis of human development. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

5. Write a short note on Demographic dividend.

Demographic dividend means Large working-age population. Example: Opportunity if skilled and employed. In exams, write its definition, use, and one classroom connection.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain major economic challenges of India.
  • Poverty is both income deprivation and lack of access to health, education, housing and dignity.
  • Unemployment may be open, disguised, seasonal, educated or underemployment.
  • Population growth affects land, water, housing, jobs, environment and public services.
  • Low literacy reduces productivity, health awareness and democratic participation.
2. What is disguised unemployment?
  • Unemployment may be open, disguised, seasonal, educated or underemployment.
  • Population growth affects land, water, housing, jobs, environment and public services.
  • Low literacy reduces productivity, health awareness and democratic participation.
  • Skill development, education, health and entrepreneurship convert population into human resource.
3. How does literacy help economic development?
  • Poverty: Lack of minimum living standard (Measured through income and multidimensional indicators)
  • Unemployment: Lack of suitable work (Measured through labour-force data)
  • Illiteracy: Lack of basic learning (Improved by universal quality education)

  • Let learners compare seasonal work and regular work using village examples.
