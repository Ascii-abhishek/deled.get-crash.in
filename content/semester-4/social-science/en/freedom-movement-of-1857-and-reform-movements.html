Freedom Movement of 1857 and Reform Movements

The revolt of 1857 and nineteenth-century reform movements shaped anti-colonial consciousness and social change in India.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Revolt of 1857 Widespread uprising against British rule involving soldiers, rulers, peasants, and citizens. Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur, Jhansi
Doctrine of Lapse British annexation policy affecting Indian states without accepted heirs. Jhansi grievance
Social reform Effort to remove social evils and promote equality, education, and rational thinking. Campaign against sati
Brahmo Samaj Reform movement associated with Raja Rammohan Roy. Monotheism and social reform
Arya Samaj Reform movement founded by Swami Dayanand Saraswati. Vedic reform and education

Key Points

  • Causes of 1857 included political annexation, economic exploitation, military discrimination, religious fear, and immediate cartridge issue.
  • Important centres included Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur, Lucknow, Jhansi, Bareilly, and parts of Bihar.
  • Effects included end of Company rule, transfer to the Crown, army reorganisation, and cautious British policy toward princes.
  • Reform movements worked for women education, widow remarriage, abolition of harmful practices, religious reform, and modern education.
  • These movements prepared educated public opinion and later nationalist consciousness.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 1857 was mainly an armed revolt; reform movements were social, religious, and educational efforts.
Point 2 Political causes challenged British power; social reform challenged internal social evils.
