Major Challenges for Indian Economy

This crash-course chapter brings together the core definitions, comparisons, classroom uses, and likely exam lines for D.El.Ed revision.

Exam Snapshot

TermExam meaningExample
PovertyInability to meet minimum needsFood, shelter, health, education
UnemploymentWilling workers not getting workEducated unemployment
Population pressureRapid population growth increasing demand on resourcesPressure on land and jobs
LiteracyAbility to read and write with understandingBasis of human development
Demographic dividendLarge working-age populationOpportunity if skilled and employed

Key Points

  • Poverty is both income deprivation and lack of access to health, education, housing and dignity.
  • Unemployment may be open, disguised, seasonal, educated or underemployment.
  • Population growth affects land, water, housing, jobs, environment and public services.
  • Low literacy reduces productivity, health awareness and democratic participation.
  • Skill development, education, health and entrepreneurship convert population into human resource.
  • Inclusive growth means benefits reach weaker sections, women, rural areas and informal workers.

Classroom Connection

  • Use a local occupation chart to connect economy with family livelihoods.
  • Discuss how education, health and skill training change life chances.
  • Let learners compare seasonal work and regular work using village examples.

One-minute Revision

  • Poverty, unemployment, population pressure and illiteracy are linked challenges.
  • Human capital is created through education, health and skills.
  • Disguised unemployment is common in agriculture.
  • Population can become an asset only with productive employment.
  • Inclusive growth is the goal of democratic planning.

Common Exam Lines

  • Explain major economic challenges of India.
  • What is disguised unemployment?
  • How does literacy help economic development?
  • Write measures to reduce poverty.
  • Explain demographic dividend.
