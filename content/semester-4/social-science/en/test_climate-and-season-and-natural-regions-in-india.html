Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Climate and Season and Natural Regions in India with examples.
  • India's climate, seasons, monsoon pattern, and natural regions influence life, agriculture, settlement, and culture.
  • Weather changes daily; climate is studied over many years.
  • India has broad seasons: cold weather, hot weather, south-west monsoon, and retreating monsoon.
  • The Himalayas, northern plains, desert, plateau, coastal plains, islands, and forests show regional diversity.
  • Classroom example: Make a weekly weather chart and connect observations with season, clothing, food, and school attendance.
2. Explain Weather, Climate, and Monsoon.
  • Weather: Short-term condition of atmosphere at a place. Example: Today's temperature and rain
  • Climate: Long-term average weather pattern of a region. Example: Hot desert climate
  • Monsoon: Seasonal wind system bringing most rainfall to India. Example: South-west monsoon
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.

  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Weather is immediate and local; climate is long-term and regional.
  • Season is a time period; natural region is a geographic area.
