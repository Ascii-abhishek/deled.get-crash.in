Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the main concepts of Freedom Movement of 1857 and Reform Movements with examples.
  • The revolt of 1857 and nineteenth-century reform movements shaped anti-colonial consciousness and social change in India.
  • Causes of 1857 included political annexation, economic exploitation, military discrimination, religious fear, and immediate cartridge issue.
  • Important centres included Meerut, Delhi, Kanpur, Lucknow, Jhansi, Bareilly, and parts of Bihar.
  • Effects included end of Company rule, transfer to the Crown, army reorganisation, and cautious British policy toward princes.
  • Classroom example: Use a timeline with reformers and revolt centres, then ask children to locate Jhansi, Delhi, and Lucknow on a map.
2. Explain the main concepts of National Movement and Demand of Full Freedom with examples.
  • The Indian national movement moved from early constitutional politics to mass movements and the demand for complete independence.
  • Early nationalists used petitions, speeches, resolutions, and constitutional methods.
  • Bengal partition, Swadeshi, revolutionary activities, and press spread stronger nationalism.
  • Gandhian movements used satyagraha, non-cooperation, boycott, civil disobedience, and constructive work.
  • Classroom example: Let groups create event cards from 1885 to 1930 and arrange them as a human timeline.
3. Explain the main concepts of Climate and Season and Natural Regions in India with examples.
  • India's climate, seasons, monsoon pattern, and natural regions influence life, agriculture, settlement, and culture.
  • Weather changes daily; climate is studied over many years.
  • India has broad seasons: cold weather, hot weather, south-west monsoon, and retreating monsoon.
  • The Himalayas, northern plains, desert, plateau, coastal plains, islands, and forests show regional diversity.
  • Classroom example: Make a weekly weather chart and connect observations with season, clothing, food, and school attendance.
4. Explain the main concepts of Uttar Pradesh-A Holistic View with examples.
  • A holistic view of Uttar Pradesh covers location, natural regions, rivers, agriculture, minerals, energy, trade, culture, and heritage.
  • Uttar Pradesh lies in the northern plains and has strong links with the Ganga-Yamuna river system.
  • Major regions include western plains, central plains, eastern plains, Bundelkhand, and Terai areas.
  • Agriculture is important; wheat, rice, sugarcane, pulses, oilseeds, and vegetables are significant.
  • Classroom example: Ask children to map their district, nearest river, main crop, local craft, and one heritage place.
5. Explain the main concepts of Environmental Pollution and United Nations Organisation with examples.
  • Environmental pollution harms air, water, soil, sound balance, and health, while the United Nations supports international peace and cooperation.
  • Pollution may be air, water, soil, noise, thermal, radioactive, or solid-waste related.
  • Causes include vehicles, industries, burning waste, sewage, chemicals, plastics, and careless resource use.
  • Effects include disease, biodiversity loss, climate problems, crop damage, dirty water, and reduced quality of life.
  • Classroom example: Conduct a school waste audit, classify waste, plan reduction steps, and connect it with global responsibility.
