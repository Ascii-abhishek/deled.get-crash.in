Education and Value

Revise the meaning of value education, human values, national and international harmony, democratic attitude, scientific temper, and responsible use of technology.

Exam Snapshot

TermFast meaningExample
Value educationEducation that develops good judgement, right conduct, empathy, and social responsibility.Morning assembly, classroom rules, group work
Human valuesQualities that make life dignified and cooperative.Truth, non-violence, compassion, honesty
National harmonyRespect for unity, diversity, constitution, and common citizenship.Celebrating languages and festivals respectfully
International understandingFeeling of world friendship and peace beyond national boundaries.UN Day, global environmental issues
Democratic attitudeRespecting equality, participation, discussion, and rights with duties.Class monitor elected fairly

Technological attitudeUsing tools and digital media ethically and purposefully.Using videos, not copying blindly

Key Points

  • Value education is not a separate moral lecture only; it should appear in language, EVS, mathematics, games, school routines, and teacher behaviour.
  • The main sources of values are family, school, community, religion, constitution, media, peer group, and life experience.
  • A primary teacher teaches values through story, role play, group task, class responsibility, inclusive seating, cleanliness work, and fair discipline.
  • National values include secularism, democracy, equality, justice, liberty, fraternity, dignity of labour, and respect for public property.
  • Scientific temper protects children from superstition by encouraging observation, experiment, evidence, and open questioning.
  • Technology has value only when it supports learning, honesty, creativity, inclusion, and safety.

Concepts And Differences

First ideaSecond ideaMain difference
Moral instructionValue educationMoral instruction often gives direct advice; value education builds habits through experience and reflection.
NationalismInternationalismNationalism builds responsible citizenship; internationalism builds world peace and cooperation.
Scientific temperBlind beliefScientific temper asks for evidence; blind belief accepts without checking.
Technology useTechnology misuseUse supports learning and access; misuse spreads copying, distraction, or misinformation.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin from learners own experience and then place the key vocabulary in a short board summary.
  • Use pictures, local material, story, role play, measurement, observation, or group discussion as suitable.
  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
