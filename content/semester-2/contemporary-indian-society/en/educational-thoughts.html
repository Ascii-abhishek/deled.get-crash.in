Educational Thoughts

Revise idealism, naturalism, and pragmatism with their aims, curriculum, discipline, teacher role, and classroom meaning.

Exam Snapshot

Key Points

  • Idealism sees education as the development of soul, moral values, self-realisation, and cultural heritage.
  • For idealism, the teacher is a model and guide; discipline is inner self-control.
  • Naturalism says the child is central and education should follow natural interests, stages, and direct experiences.
  • For naturalism, excessive punishment and artificial bookish learning should be avoided.
  • Pragmatism connects education with life, democracy, activity, experimentation, and social problem solving.
  • For pragmatism, curriculum is flexible and based on projects, activities, and real situations.
  • No single philosophy solves every classroom need; teachers combine values, child nature, and practical activity.
  • In primary school, moral stories, nature walks, and project work can balance all three philosophies.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with learners own experience, then place the key vocabulary on the board in a small table.
  • Use pair or group activity so children learn by doing and explaining.
  • Connect the topic with family, community, school management, festivals, water saving, or current news.
  • Support weak readers with pictures, oral instructions, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute oral recap and one short written question.
