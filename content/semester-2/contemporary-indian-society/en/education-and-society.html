Education and Society

Revise the relationship between education and society, agencies of education, social change, and school-community connection.

Key Points

  • Education is created by society and also reconstructs society by changing knowledge, values, skills, and attitudes.
  • Family gives early language, habits, affection, culture, and discipline.
  • School gives planned learning, peer interaction, citizenship, equality, and structured knowledge.
  • State provides policy, law, finance, curriculum, teacher training, and welfare schemes.
  • Mass media informs, entertains, influences opinions, and can support learning if used critically.
  • Social change is caused by technology, urbanisation, industrialisation, democracy, education, media, migration, and reform movements.
  • A school becomes a community centre when it supports health, awareness, meetings, local culture, and child welfare.
  • Teachers should respect local culture but also challenge discrimination, superstition, and exclusion.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with learners own experience, then place the key vocabulary on the board in a small table.
  • Use pair or group activity so children learn by doing and explaining.
  • Connect the topic with family, community, school management, festivals, water saving, or current news.
  • Support weak readers with pictures, oral instructions, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute oral recap and one short written question.
