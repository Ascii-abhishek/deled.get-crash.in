Educational Thoughts of Western Thinkers

Revise the educational ideas of Plato, Rousseau, Dewey, Froebel, and Montessori for primary teaching.

Key Points

  • Plato linked education with moral formation and preparation for responsible citizenship.
  • Rousseau criticized bookish and forced education; he wanted education according to child nature and developmental stage.
  • Dewey made school a miniature society where children learn by doing, discussing, experimenting, and solving problems.
  • Froebel viewed children like plants in a garden and emphasized play, songs, movement, gifts, and occupations.
  • Montessori designed a prepared environment where children choose materials, work independently, and develop senses and order.
  • Western thinkers strongly shaped modern child-centred and activity-based pedagogy.
  • A primary teacher can use Dewey in projects, Froebel in play-way teaching, and Montessori in self-correcting materials.
  • Exam answers should avoid long biography and focus on principles, aims, methods, discipline, and teacher role.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with learners own experience, then place the key vocabulary on the board in a small table.
  • Use pair or group activity so children learn by doing and explaining.
  • Connect the topic with family, community, school management, festivals, water saving, or current news.
  • Support weak readers with pictures, oral instructions, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute oral recap and one short written question.
