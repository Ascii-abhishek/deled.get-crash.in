Major Issues of Emerging Society 2

Revise environmental protection, water conservation, global warming, globalisation, gender inequality, and mass media in education.

Key Points

  • Environmental education should connect knowledge with daily habits: cleanliness, plantation, waste segregation, saving electricity, and local biodiversity.
  • Water conservation can be taught through school water audit, leaky tap repair, rain gauge, and community awareness.
  • Global warming affects seasons, rainfall, crops, health, disasters, and poor communities more severely.
  • Globalisation brings knowledge exchange, technology, jobs, and exposure, but also cultural pressure, inequality, consumerism, and language challenges.
  • Gender inequality appears in household work division, school participation, subject choice, safety, nutrition, and leadership roles.
  • Mass media can spread awareness quickly but also misinformation, stereotypes, distraction, and consumer culture.
  • Critical media literacy means checking source, purpose, evidence, bias, and effect before believing or sharing.
  • The school must build sustainable habits, gender sensitivity, scientific temper, and responsible digital citizenship.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with learners own experience, then place the key vocabulary on the board in a small table.
  • Use pair or group activity so children learn by doing and explaining.
  • Connect the topic with family, community, school management, festivals, water saving, or current news.
  • Support weak readers with pictures, oral instructions, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute oral recap and one short written question.
