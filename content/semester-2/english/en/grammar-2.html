Key Points

  • Grammar II is application-focused: learners must use forms in sentences.
  • Pronouns and possessives help avoid repetition and show relation.
  • Adjective degrees compare two or more persons or things.
  • Tense practice should connect time words with verb forms.
  • Sentence transformation and voice should be taught through meaning, subject and object movement.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Give a base sentence: The girl reads a book. Change pronoun, tense, negative form and passive form step by step.

Memory Hook

Term -> Example -> Reason/steps -> Classroom activity -> Revision

One-minute Revision

  • Noun: Name word
  • Pronoun: Word used instead of noun
  • Adjective: Word describing noun
  • Degree of comparison: Positive, comparative, superlative forms
  • Verb: Action or state word
  • Tense: Time of verb

Common Exam Lines

  • In Grammar II, define the term, add one example, and connect it with classroom use.
  • For difference questions, write basis, feature, and example in separate lines.
  • For primary teaching, add local examples, pictures, cards, activity and oral practice.
