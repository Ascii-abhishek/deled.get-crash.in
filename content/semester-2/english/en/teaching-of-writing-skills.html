Key Points

  • Writing moves from strokes and letters to words, sentences and composition.
  • Good handwriting needs posture, pencil grip, spacing, letter size and regular practice.
  • Spelling improves through sound-letter relation, word families and repeated meaningful use.
  • Dictation tests listening, memory, spelling and attention.
  • Correction should be selective; too many red marks reduce confidence.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Use a three-line board model: trace the letter in air, copy a word, write a sentence, then exchange notebooks for one friendly correction.
