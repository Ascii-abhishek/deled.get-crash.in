Key Points

  • Grammar I should be taught through simple classroom sentences.
  • Children must identify subject and predicate before writing longer sentences.
  • Kinds of sentences are linked with punctuation: full stop, question mark and exclamation mark.
  • Articles are determiners: use a before consonant sound, an before vowel sound, the for specific nouns.
  • Grammar practice should include correction, substitution and sentence-making.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Give mixed sentence strips. Learners sort them into statement, question and command, then add the correct punctuation mark.

Memory Hook

Term -> Example -> Reason/steps -> Classroom activity -> Revision

One-minute Revision

  • Grammar: Rules for meaningful and correct language
  • Sentence: Group of words with complete meaning
  • Subject: Who or what the sentence is about
  • Predicate: What is said about subject
  • Assertive sentence: Statement sentence
  • Interrogative sentence: Question sentence

Common Exam Lines

  • In Grammar I, define the term, add one example, and connect it with classroom use.
  • For difference questions, write basis, feature, and example in separate lines.
  • For primary teaching, add local examples, pictures, cards, activity and oral practice.
