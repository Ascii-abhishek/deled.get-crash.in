Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define Phoneme and write one classroom or exam point.

Phoneme: Smallest sound unit that changes meaning, such as /p/ and /b/. Teach through listening, imitation, and minimal pairs.

2. Define Vowel sound and write one classroom or exam point.

Vowel sound: Open sound made without full blockage of air. English has more vowel sounds than vowel letters.

3. Define Consonant sound and write one classroom or exam point.

Consonant sound: Sound made with partial or complete obstruction of air. Use mouth position and examples: pin, tin, fan.

4. Define Stress and write one classroom or exam point.

Stress: Extra force on a syllable or word. Wrong stress can make speech unclear.

5. Define Intonation and write one classroom or exam point.

Intonation: Rise and fall of voice in sentences. Questions, commands, and feelings need different tone patterns.
