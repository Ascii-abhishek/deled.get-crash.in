Key Points

  • Conversation practice should use real classroom needs: greeting, permission, help, apology and thanks.
  • Instructions should be short, clear and supported by gestures at first.
  • Self-introduction can be practised through sentence frames and pair interviews.
  • Repeated routines make English natural: Good morning, May I come in, I have finished.
  • Teacher should encourage attempts and correct gently after communication.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Build an instruction bank on the wall. Use total physical response: teacher says, children act, then children give instructions to peers.

Memory Hook

Term -> Example -> Reason/steps -> Classroom activity -> Revision

One-minute Revision

  • Conversation: Exchange of spoken ideas
  • Classroom instruction: Teacher direction in class
  • Self-introduction: Telling basic information about oneself
  • Polite expression: Respectful phrase
  • Command: Instruction to do something
  • Request: Polite asking

Common Exam Lines

  • In Conversational Classroom Instructions, define the term, add one example, and connect it with classroom use.
  • For difference questions, write basis, feature, and example in separate lines.
  • For primary teaching, add local examples, pictures, cards, activity and oral practice.
