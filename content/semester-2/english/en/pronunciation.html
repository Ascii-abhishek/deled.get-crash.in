Key Points

  • Pronunciation teaching should connect listening, imitation and meaningful use.
  • English has sounds that may not exist in children's home language; give mouth-position practice.
  • Minimal pairs help learners hear and produce small sound differences.
  • Syllable and stress practice improves natural rhythm of speech.
  • Reading simple sentences should include pause, stress and intonation, not only word reading.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Use listen-point-repeat: play or say two minimal-pair words, children point to picture, repeat, then use one word in a sentence.

Memory Hook

Term -> Example -> Reason/steps -> Classroom activity -> Revision

One-minute Revision

  • Pronunciation: Way a word is spoken
  • Phoneme: Smallest meaningful sound unit
  • Vowel: Sound made without major blockage
  • Consonant: Sound made with some blockage
  • Syllable: Beat of a word
  • Stress: Extra force on syllable or word

Common Exam Lines

  • In Pronunciation, define the term, add one example, and connect it with classroom use.
  • For difference questions, write basis, feature, and example in separate lines.
  • For primary teaching, add local examples, pictures, cards, activity and oral practice.
