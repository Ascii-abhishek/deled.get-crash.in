Key Points

  • Content specifications help the teacher plan what to teach, practise and assess.
  • Oral tasks such as viva, self-introduction and extempore build confidence.
  • Description and narration develop observation, sequence and vocabulary.
  • Story teaching needs preparation, presentation, comprehension, practice and evaluation.
  • Vocabulary should be taught through theme, picture, use and recycling.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Use one picture for three tasks: describe the picture, narrate what happened before and after, and ask two viva questions.

Common Exam Lines

  • In Content Specifications, define the term, add one example, and connect it with classroom use.
  • For difference questions, write basis, feature, and example in separate lines.
  • For primary teaching, add local examples, pictures, cards, activity and oral practice.
