LCM and HCF of Three Digit Numbers

This chapter prepares students to find common factors and common multiples of three-digit numbers by factor listing, prime factorisation, and division method, then use them in word problems.

Exam Snapshot

Key Points

  • For HCF, multiply only the common prime factors with the smallest powers.
  • For LCM, multiply all prime factors with the greatest powers.
  • If two numbers are co-prime, their HCF is 1 and LCM is their product.
  • In distribution problems asking maximum equal size, use HCF.
  • In repeated event problems asking when events meet again, use LCM.
  • Always check: HCF should not be greater than the smallest number; LCM should be at least as large as the greatest number.
