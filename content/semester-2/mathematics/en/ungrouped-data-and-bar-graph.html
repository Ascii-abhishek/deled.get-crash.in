Key Points

  • Data can be collected by observation, question, measurement, or record.
  • Tally marks reduce counting errors in repeated data.
  • Bars must have equal width and equal gap.
  • The scale must be suitable and written clearly.
  • Bar height represents frequency, not width.
  • A good graph has title, labelled axes, scale, and neat bars.
