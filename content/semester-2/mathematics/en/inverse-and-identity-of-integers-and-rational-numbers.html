Inverse and Identity of Integers and Rational Numbers

Revise integer and rational-number operations, additive identity, multiplicative identity, additive inverse, reciprocal, and number-line meaning.

Key Points

  • The additive inverse of a is -a; their sum is zero.
  • The reciprocal of p/q is q/p, but p must not be zero.
  • Negative numbers decrease as we move left on number line.
  • For rational numbers, signs can be written in numerator, denominator, or before the fraction, but only one negative sign means negative value.
  • Identity and inverse ideas help solve equations.
  • Do not confuse additive inverse with reciprocal.
