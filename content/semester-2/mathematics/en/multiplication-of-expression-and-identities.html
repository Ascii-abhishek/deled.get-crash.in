Multiplication of Expression and Identities

Revise multiplication of monomials and polynomials, distributive law, standard identities, expansion, and verification through substitution or area models.

Exam Snapshot

IdeaMeaningExample
MonomialExpression with one term.3x
BinomialExpression with two terms.a+b
PolynomialExpression with one or more algebraic terms.x²+3x+2
Distributive lawMultiply each term inside bracket.a(b+c)=ab+ac
Product of powersAdd powers for same base.x² x x³ = x⁵
IdentityTrue for all values.(a+b)²=a²+2ab+b²
Difference of squares(a+b)(a-b).a²-b²
ExpansionRemoving brackets by multiplication.(x+2)(x+3)=x²+5x+6
VerificationCheck by values or area model.Put x=1
Common mistakeForgetting middle term.(a+b)² not a²+b²

Key Points

  • Multiply coefficients and then multiply variable parts.
  • For same base, add exponents during multiplication.
  • Use distributive property for bracket multiplication.
  • Remember identities: (a+b)², (a-b)², (a+b)(a-b).
  • Area model helps children visualise algebraic multiplication.
  • Verify identities by substituting simple values.

Working Method And Exam Use

  • Write the definition or formula clearly.
  • Give one suitable example.
  • Put difference, reason, or steps in a table or points.
  • End with classroom use or life connection.
