Equation and Identity

Revise mathematical statements, equations, identities, solving, checking, equivalent forms, and common confusion between equation and identity.

Exam Snapshot

IdeaMeaningExample
StatementSentence that may be true or false.7+3=10
Open statementContains a variable and truth depends on value.x+2=5
EquationOpen statement with equality sign.2x+1=9
Solution/rootValue making equation true.x=4
IdentityTrue for all allowed values.(a+b)²=a²+2ab+b²
Equivalent equationSame solution after legal operation.x+3=8 -> x=5
CheckingSubstitute value in original equation.2(4)+1=9
ContradictionNo value satisfies it.x+1=x+2
FormulaIdentity-like relation used in applications.Area = l x b
Balance ideaSame operation on both sides keeps equality.Add 3 both sides

Key Points

  • An equation is true for particular value(s); an identity is true for all allowed values.
  • Solving means finding the value of the variable.
  • Every step must keep both sides balanced.
  • Checking in the original equation is compulsory in exam answers.
  • Do not call every equation an identity.
  • Word problems are converted into equations by identifying unknown and relation.
