Key Points

  • Area is two-dimensional; volume is three-dimensional.
  • Capacity is used for hollow containers and liquids.
  • Area units are square units; volume units are cubic units.
  • Do not write linear units for area or volume.
  • Use grids, unit squares, cubes, bottles, and measuring jars in class.
  • Children understand units better when they cover, fill, and compare real objects.
