Key Points

  • Congruence requires both same shape and same size.
  • Similarity requires same shape; size may be enlarged or reduced.
  • For congruent triangles, use SSS, SAS, ASA, or RHS criteria.
  • For similar triangles, compare angles and proportional sides.
  • CPCT is used after proving triangles congruent.
  • Cutting and overlaying paper shapes helps children see congruence.
