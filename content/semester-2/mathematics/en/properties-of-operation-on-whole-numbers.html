Properties of Operation on Whole Numbers

Revise closure, commutative, associative, distributive, identity, and zero properties for operations on whole numbers.

Exam Snapshot

PropertyMeaningExample
ClosureResult remains in the set.5+7=12 whole number
Commutative additionChanging order does not change sum.4+9=9+4
Commutative multiplicationChanging order does not change product.3x8=8x3
Subtraction not commutativeOrder changes result.9-4 != 4-9
Division not commutativeOrder changes result.12/3 != 3/12
Associative additionGrouping does not change sum.(2+3)+4=2+(3+4)
Associative multiplicationGrouping does not change product.(2x3)x4=2x(3x4)
Distributive propertyMultiplication over addition/subtraction.6(10+2)=60+12
Additive identityAdding 0 changes nothing.18+0=18
Multiplicative identityMultiplying by 1 changes nothing.18x1=18

Key Points

  • Whole numbers are closed under addition and multiplication, but not under subtraction and division.
  • Commutative and associative properties apply to addition and multiplication only.
  • Distributive property is very useful for mental calculation.
  • Zero is the identity for addition but not for multiplication.
  • One is the identity for multiplication.
  • Division by zero is not defined.
