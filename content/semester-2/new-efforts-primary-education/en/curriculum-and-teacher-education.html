Curriculum and Teacher Education

Revise curriculum, syllabus, textbook, NCF 2005, NCFTE 2009, teacher preparation, reflective practice, and child-centred pedagogy.

Key Points

  • Curriculum is wider than syllabus; it includes experiences, values, activities, assessment, and school culture.
  • NCF 2005 asks teachers to connect knowledge with children’s life, avoid rote learning, and use dialogue and activity.
  • Teacher education should combine theory, practice, school internship, observation, reflection, and community understanding.
  • A primary teacher needs subject knowledge, child psychology, language sensitivity, assessment skill, and inclusive attitude.
  • Textbook should be used flexibly with local examples, TLM, stories, field visits, and children’s questions.
  • Assessment should support learning through feedback, not only rank children.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin from learners own experience and then place the key vocabulary in a short board summary.
  • Use pictures, local material, story, role play, measurement, observation, or group discussion as suitable.
  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
