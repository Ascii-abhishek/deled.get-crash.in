Commissions and Committees for Elementary Education

Revise major pre-independence commissions and reports that shaped elementary education: Charter Act, Macaulay, Wood, Hunter, Gokhale, Wardha, Sargent and related ideas.

Exam Snapshot

TermFast meaningExample
Charter Act 1813First official provision of funds for education by the Company.One lakh rupees for education
Macaulay Minute 1835Promoted English education and western knowledge.Downward filtration idea
Wood Despatch 1854Called the Magna Carta of English education in India.Department of Public Instruction
Hunter Commission 1882Gave attention to primary education and local bodies.Primary education through local management

Wardha Scheme 1937Basic education through productive craft and mother tongue.Learning by doing
Sargent Plan 1944Proposed free compulsory education for 6-14 age group in stages.Long-term national education plan

Key Points

  • Pre-independence reports slowly moved education from elite access to broader public concern.
  • Wood Despatch supported a graded system from primary to university and teacher training.
  • Hunter Commission recommended more attention to primary education, indigenous schools, grants, and local bodies.
  • Gokhale’s effort is important because it raised compulsory elementary education as a legislative issue.
  • Wardha Basic Education linked craft, dignity of labour, mother tongue, self-reliance, and child activity.
  • Sargent Plan gave a broad blueprint but required large funds and time.

Concepts And Differences

First ideaSecond ideaMain difference
Wood DespatchHunter CommissionWood gave a system-wide plan; Hunter focused strongly on primary education and local management.
MacaulayWardhaMacaulay favoured English and western learning; Wardha favoured mother tongue, craft, and productive work.
Gokhale BillSargent PlanGokhale demanded compulsion; Sargent proposed a staged national plan.
Elite educationMass educationElite education served a few; mass education aimed to reach common children.
