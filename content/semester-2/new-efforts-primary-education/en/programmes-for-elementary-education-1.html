Programmes for Elementary Education 1

Revise Operation Blackboard, teacher orientation, Bihar Education Project, DPEP, school readiness, literacy programmes, and early access measures.

Key Points

  • Programmes before SSA created the ground for universal elementary education.
  • Operation Blackboard showed that quality needs basic physical and learning resources.
  • DPEP used district-level planning, local needs, community involvement, and focus on disadvantaged groups.
  • School readiness prevents early failure by preparing language, social, emotional, and motor abilities.
  • Teacher orientation links policy with actual classroom practice.
  • Literacy and bridge programmes connect school education with community development.

Concepts And Differences

First ideaSecond ideaMain difference
Operation BlackboardDPEPOperation Blackboard focused on minimum school facilities; DPEP focused on district planning and quality improvement.
School readinessRemedial teachingReadiness prepares before formal learning; remedial teaching supports after learning difficulty appears.
Teacher orientationRegular teachingOrientation upgrades teacher understanding; regular teaching applies it daily.
LiteracyElementary educationLiteracy gives basic reading-writing; elementary education gives full school learning and development.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin from learners own experience and then place the key vocabulary in a short board summary.
  • Use pictures, local material, story, role play, measurement, observation, or group discussion as suitable.
  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
