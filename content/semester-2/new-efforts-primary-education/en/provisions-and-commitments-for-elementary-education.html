Provisions and Commitments for Elementary Education

Revise constitutional provisions, child rights, equality, RTE commitments, and the duties of school and teacher in elementary education.

Key Points

  • The Constitution makes education a public responsibility, not charity.
  • RTE 2009 strengthened access, admission, retention, child-friendly learning, infrastructure norms, and accountability.
  • Free education means no direct cost should stop a child from completing elementary schooling.
  • Compulsory education means the State must ensure admission, attendance, and completion.
  • Teachers must avoid physical punishment, mental harassment, discrimination, private tuition pressure, and exclusion.
  • Commitment to elementary education includes universal access, equity, quality, inclusion, and community participation.
