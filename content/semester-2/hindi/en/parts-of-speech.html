Key Points

  • Parts of speech are best understood through sentence use, not isolated lists.
  • Noun and pronoun reduce repetition and make writing smoother.
  • Verb, tense and agreement are central to sentence correctness.
  • Gender and number errors are common in children's writing; correction should be example-based.
  • Classroom practice should move from identification to substitution and sentence creation.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Write one sentence on the board and ask children to colour noun, pronoun, verb and adjective with different chalk colours.

Memory Hook

Term -> Example -> Reason/steps -> Classroom activity -> Revision

One-minute Revision

  • Noun: Name of person, place, thing or feeling
  • Pronoun: Word used in place of noun
  • Verb: Word showing action or state
  • Adverb: Word modifying verb
  • Adjective: Word qualifying noun
  • Gender: Masculine or feminine form

Common Exam Lines

  • In Parts of Speech, define the term, add one example, and connect it with classroom use.
  • For difference questions, write basis, feature, and example in separate lines.
  • For primary teaching, add local examples, pictures, cards, activity and oral practice.
