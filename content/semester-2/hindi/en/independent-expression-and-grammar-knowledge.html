Key Points

  • Expression improves when children get meaningful situations, not only grammar drills.
  • Grammar should support speaking and writing; it should not stop fluency in early practice.
  • Punctuation helps the reader understand pause, question, emotion and completion.
  • Similar sound practice improves spelling, pronunciation and careful listening.
  • Teacher should move from oral talk to keywords, then sentences, then paragraph.

Answer Writing Frame

  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Show a picture, collect ten words from children, make three sentences orally, then write a short paragraph with punctuation.
