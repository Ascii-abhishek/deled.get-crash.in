Key Points

  • Oral reading checks pronunciation and fluency; silent reading checks comprehension and self-learning.
  • Sentence teaching begins with meaningful oral sentences from children's experience.
  • Children should arrange jumbled words, complete sentences and write short answers.
  • Reading comprehension must include factual, inferential and value-based questions.
  • Teacher should avoid mechanical reading; every reading task should have a purpose.

Answer Writing Frame

  1. Write a clear definition of the key term.
  2. Add one local or textbook example.
  3. Put reason, steps or difference in points/table.
  4. End with one short primary-class use.

Classroom Connection

Give five jumbled words from a familiar sentence, ask children to arrange, read aloud, copy, and make one new sentence.
