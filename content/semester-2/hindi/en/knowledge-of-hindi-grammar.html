Key Points

  • Grammar should be taught through examples from children's reading and writing.
  • Prefix and suffix help in vocabulary expansion and meaning-making.
  • Sandhi explains sound joining; samas explains compact expression.
  • Children learn better when they break and make words rather than memorise definitions only.
  • Grammar exercises should include identification, formation and sentence use.

Answer Writing Frame

Classroom Connection

Give root words on cards and ask groups to add prefixes or suffixes, then use the new words in meaningful sentences.
