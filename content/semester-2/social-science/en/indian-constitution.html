Key Points

  • The Constitution fixes the powers of legislature, executive and judiciary.
  • It protects citizens from arbitrary rule and gives a framework for equality and justice.
  • Rights and duties must be studied together; rights become meaningful when citizens act responsibly.
  • Directive Principles are not directly enforceable in court, but they guide welfare policies.
  • Consumer awareness connects civics with daily life: bill, quality mark, complaint and fair price.
