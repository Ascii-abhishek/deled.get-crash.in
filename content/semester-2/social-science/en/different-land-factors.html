Different Land Factors

Revise internal and external landform-making forces: earthquake, volcano, weathering, erosion, deposition, river, glacier, wind, and sea waves.

Exam Snapshot

TermFast meaningExample
Internal forceForce from inside Earth.Earthquake, volcano
External forceForce acting on surface.River, wind, glacier
WeatheringBreaking of rocks in place.Heat, water, roots
ErosionRemoval of weathered material.River cuts valley
DepositionLaying down transported material.Delta formation
EarthquakeSudden shaking of Earth crust.Fault movement
VolcanoOpening through which lava and gases come out.Volcanic cone
River workErosion, transport, deposition by running water.Valley, floodplain, delta
Glacier workMoving ice erodes and deposits.U-shaped valley
Wind workErosion and deposition by wind.Sand dunes

Key Points

  • Use timeline and map together; time and place make the answer precise.
  • Connect each event or process with cause, effect, and evidence.
  • Write names, terms, and examples accurately; avoid vague statements.
  • For history, use sources such as inscriptions, coins, texts, and travel accounts.
  • For geography, connect natural process with landform and human life.
  • In primary classes, use stories, pictures, maps, models, and local observation.

Working Method And Exam Use

  • Write the definition or formula clearly.
  • Give one suitable example.
  • Put difference, reason, or steps in a table or points.
  • End with classroom use or life connection.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with a local example and then connect it with textbook vocabulary.
  • Give a pair or group task: solve, classify, map, or find examples.
  • Support weaker learners with cues, pictures, steps, and oral practice.
  • Close with a quick recap and one exam-style written question.
