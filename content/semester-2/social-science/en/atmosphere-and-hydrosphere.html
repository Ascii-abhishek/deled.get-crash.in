Key Points

  • Atmosphere is divided into layers; the troposphere is most important for weather and human life.
  • Unequal heating of Earth creates pressure differences; air moves from high pressure to low pressure.
  • Cyclones bring strong winds, rainfall and damage; anticyclones usually bring clear and stable weather.
  • Hydrosphere includes oceans, seas, rivers, lakes, groundwater, glaciers and water vapour.
  • Waves, currents and tides influence fishing, ports, navigation and coastal climate.

Classroom Connection

Use a newspaper weather map, a bowl of water and a torch to show pressure, wind, waves and evaporation. Ask children to connect monsoon with crops and local water sources.
