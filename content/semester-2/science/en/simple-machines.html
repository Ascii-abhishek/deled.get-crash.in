Simple Machines

Revise simple machines, lever, pulley, wheel and axle, inclined plane, screw, wedge, mechanical advantage, and daily-life uses.

Key Points

  • Simple machines do not reduce total work magically; they make work easier by increasing distance or changing force direction.
  • Levers are of three classes depending on position of fulcrum, load, and effort.
  • First-class lever: fulcrum between load and effort; second-class: load in between; third-class: effort in between.
  • Pulley can change direction of effort and in combinations can reduce effort.
  • Inclined plane, wedge, and screw are related: wedge is two inclined planes; screw is an inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder.
  • Children can identify simple machines in school: scissors, stapler, ramp, door handle, bicycle, flag pulley.

Concepts And Differences

Classroom Connection

  • Begin from learners own experience and then place the key vocabulary in a short board summary.
  • Use pictures, local material, story, role play, measurement, observation, or group discussion as suitable.
  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
