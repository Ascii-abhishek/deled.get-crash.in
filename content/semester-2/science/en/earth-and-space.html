Earth and Space

Revise the solar system, stars, celestial bodies, eclipses, life on earth, satellites, and space exploration in primary science language.

Key Points

  • The Sun is the nearest star to Earth and the main source of light and heat.
  • Earth supports life because it has suitable temperature, water, air, soil, gravity, and protective atmosphere.
  • The Moon is Earth’s natural satellite and shows phases because of changing visible sunlight.
  • Rotation causes day and night; revolution with tilted axis causes seasons.
  • A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon comes between the Sun and Earth; a lunar eclipse occurs when Earth comes between the Sun and Moon.
  • Artificial satellites help in weather forecast, communication, navigation, education, and disaster warning.
