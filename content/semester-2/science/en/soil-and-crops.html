Soil and Crops

Revise soil formation, soil types, crops, agricultural practices, manure, fertilisers, insecticides, erosion, and soil conservation.

Exam Snapshot

HumusDark organic matter formed from dead plants and animals.Improves fertility
CropPlants grown on a large scale for food or other use.Wheat, rice, sugarcane

Key Points

  • Soil is formed slowly by weathering of rocks and mixing of organic matter.
  • Sandy soil drains quickly; clayey soil holds water; loamy soil is best for many crops because it balances air, water, and humus.
  • Main agricultural practices include preparation of soil, sowing, adding manure/fertiliser, irrigation, weeding, crop protection, harvesting, threshing, and storage.
  • Manure improves soil structure; chemical fertilisers add specific nutrients but excess use harms soil and water.
  • Pesticides and insecticides should be used carefully because they may kill useful organisms and pollute food and water.
  • Soil conservation needs vegetation cover, terrace farming, contour ploughing, crop rotation, check dams, and controlled grazing.

Concepts And Differences

First ideaSecond ideaMain difference
ManureFertiliserManure is organic and improves soil texture; fertiliser is chemical and supplies specific nutrients quickly.
KharifRabiKharif grows in monsoon; rabi grows in winter.
Sandy soilClayey soilSandy soil has large particles and drains fast; clayey soil has fine particles and holds water.
ErosionConservationErosion removes soil; conservation protects soil.

  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
