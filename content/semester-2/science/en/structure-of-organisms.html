Structure of Organisms

Revise cell, cell organelles, tissues, organs, organ systems, unicellular and multicellular organisms, and microscope-based understanding.

Key Points

  • All living organisms are made of cells, but the number and type of cells differ.
  • Unicellular organisms have one cell performing all life functions; multicellular organisms have specialised cells.
  • Plant cells generally have cell wall, chloroplasts, and large vacuole; animal cells do not have cell wall or chloroplasts.
  • Levels of organisation: cell -> tissue -> organ -> organ system -> organism.
  • Microscope helps us see cells and microorganisms that are invisible to naked eye.
  • Structure and function are connected: shape of red blood cells helps transport oxygen; root hairs help absorption.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin from learners own experience and then place the key vocabulary in a short board summary.
  • Use pictures, local material, story, role play, measurement, observation, or group discussion as suitable.
  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
