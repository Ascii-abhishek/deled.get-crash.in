Activities of Life

Revise nutrition, respiration, excretion, sensitivity, photosynthesis, growth, reproduction, transpiration, and life processes.

Key Points

  • Life processes maintain living organisms and distinguish them from non-living things.
  • Autotrophs make their own food; heterotrophs depend on others for food.
  • Photosynthesis produces food and oxygen; it needs sunlight, chlorophyll, carbon dioxide, and water.
  • Respiration releases energy from food and occurs in plants and animals both.
  • Plants also respond to stimuli, though slowly: roots grow toward water and shoots toward light.
  • Transpiration helps water movement in plants and cools leaves, but excess water loss can cause wilting.

Concepts And Differences

PhotosynthesisRespirationPhotosynthesis makes food and stores energy; respiration breaks food and releases energy.
Autotrophic nutritionHeterotrophic nutritionAutotrophs make food; heterotrophs obtain food from others.
ExcretionEgestionExcretion removes metabolic waste; egestion removes undigested food.
GrowthReproductionGrowth increases size or cells; reproduction forms new organisms.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin from learners own experience and then place the key vocabulary in a short board summary.
  • Use pictures, local material, story, role play, measurement, observation, or group discussion as suitable.
  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
