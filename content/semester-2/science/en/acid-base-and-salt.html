Acid, Base and Salt

Revise acids, bases, salts, indicators, neutralisation, pH idea, common properties, safety, and classroom activities using household substances.

Exam Snapshot

TermFast meaningExample
AcidSubstance that gives hydrogen ions in water and turns blue litmus red.Lemon juice, vinegar, HCl
BaseSubstance that gives hydroxide ions in water and turns red litmus blue.Soap solution, lime water, NaOH
AlkaliBase soluble in water.Sodium hydroxide, potassium hydroxide
SaltSubstance formed when acid reacts with base.Sodium chloride
IndicatorSubstance that shows acidic or basic nature by colour change.Litmus, turmeric, China rose
NeutralisationReaction of acid and base forming salt and water.Antacid reduces acidity
pHScale showing acidity or basicity from 0 to 14.7 neutral; below 7 acidic; above 7 basic

Key Points

  • Acids are sour, corrosive in strong form, and react with metals to release hydrogen gas.
  • Bases are bitter, soapy to touch, and also corrosive in strong form.
  • Litmus is the most common school indicator; turmeric turns reddish brown in a base.
  • Neutralisation is used in antacid tablets, soil treatment, and first aid for insect stings.
  • Salts may be neutral, acidic, or basic depending on the strength of reacting acid/base.
  • Children must not taste unknown chemicals; use weak household samples and teacher demonstration.

Classroom Connection

  • Ask children to collect examples from home, kitchen, field, road, and school.
  • Use small-group observation, classification, reason-giving, and safety writing.
  • Clarify concepts through low-cost material, pictures, tables, and oral discussion.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one written answer on a local example.
