Environmental Pollution

Revise pollution, air, water, soil, noise pollution, disasters, disaster management, natural calamities, and school-level environmental action.

Key Points

  • Pollution affects health, crops, climate, biodiversity, and quality of life.
  • Air pollution may cause cough, asthma, eye irritation, and reduced visibility.
  • Water pollution spreads diarrhoea, cholera, typhoid, and skin diseases.
  • Soil pollution reduces fertility and may enter the food chain.
  • Noise pollution affects concentration, hearing, sleep, and classroom learning.
  • Disaster management begins with risk awareness, safe behaviour, emergency contacts, and practice drills.

Important Steps And Applications

Classroom Connection

  • Ask children to collect examples from home, kitchen, field, road, and school.
  • Use small-group observation, classification, reason-giving, and safety writing.
  • Clarify concepts through low-cost material, pictures, tables, and oral discussion.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one written answer on a local example.
