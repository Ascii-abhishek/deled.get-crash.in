Organs and Functions of Human Body

Revise major organs, skeletal system, muscles, teeth, digestion, breathing, circulation, sense organs, and healthy habits.

Exam Snapshot

TermFast meaningExample

SkeletonFramework of bones giving shape, support, and protection.Skull protects brain
MuscleTissue that contracts and relaxes to produce movement.Arm muscles
DigestionBreaking food into simpler soluble substances.Stomach and intestine
CirculationTransport of blood through body by heart and blood vessels.Oxygen and nutrients carried
RespirationExchange of gases and release of energy from food.Lungs and cells
Sense organsOrgans that receive information from surroundings.Eyes, ears, nose, tongue, skin

Key Points

  • Human body works through organ systems; no organ works fully alone.
  • Skeleton gives shape, protects soft organs, helps movement with muscles, and produces blood cells in bone marrow.
  • Teeth help biting and chewing; types include incisors, canines, premolars, and molars.
  • Digestive system changes food into absorbable nutrients and removes undigested waste.
  • Circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste materials.
  • Healthy habits include balanced diet, clean water, exercise, sleep, hygiene, correct posture, and vaccination.

Concepts And Differences

BoneMuscleBone gives support and protection; muscle contracts to create movement.
DigestionCirculationDigestion prepares nutrients; circulation transports them.
Milk teethPermanent teethMilk teeth appear early and fall; permanent teeth replace them.
BreathingCellular respirationBreathing is air movement; cellular respiration releases energy in cells.

Classroom Connection

  • Use pictures, local material, story, role play, measurement, observation, or group discussion as suitable.
  • Support weak readers with oral cues, peer help, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute recap and one short written question.
