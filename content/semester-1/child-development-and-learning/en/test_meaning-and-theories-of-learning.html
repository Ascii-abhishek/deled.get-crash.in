Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write the meaning of learning.

Learning is a relatively permanent change in behavior, knowledge, skill, attitude, or understanding that comes through experience, practice, interaction, and reflection.

2. Mention four factors affecting learning.

Readiness, motivation, maturity, health, attention, interest, previous knowledge, teaching method, learning material, classroom environment, and individual differences affect learning.

3. State Thorndike's three main laws of learning.

The three main laws are the law of readiness, the law of exercise, and the law of effect. They mean that learning improves when the learner is ready, practices meaningfully, and receives satisfying results.

4. What is reinforcement?

Reinforcement is a consequence that strengthens desired behavior or learning. In class it may be praise, feedback, a token, success experience, or recognition of effort.

5. What is learning plateau?

A learning plateau is a temporary stage where progress appears stopped despite practice. It may be caused by fatigue, boredom, poor method, lack of feedback, or low motivation.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and characteristics of learning.

Learning is a relatively permanent change in behavior or capacity due to experience and practice.

  • It is continuous and life-long.
  • It is purposeful and goal-directed.
  • It is active and based on experience.
  • It is individual as well as social.
  • It is affected by readiness, motivation, health, method, and environment.
  • It includes knowledge, skill, attitude, habit, understanding, and values.
2. Discuss the factors affecting learning with classroom examples.
  • Readiness: A child learns subtraction better after understanding counting and grouping.
  • Motivation and interest: Story, game, and praise increase attention and effort.
  • Health: Hunger, fatigue, illness, or poor eyesight reduces learning.
  • Previous knowledge: Old learning helps when connected properly.
  • Method and material: Concrete material, TLM, demonstration, and feedback improve learning.
  • Environment: Respectful, safe, and participatory classrooms support learning.
3. Explain Thorndike's laws of learning and their educational importance.

ReadinessLearning is satisfying when the learner is ready.Prepare interest and check previous knowledge.

These laws help teachers plan preparation, practice, and positive feedback.

4. Compare major learning theories and their classroom use.
  • Thorndike: Trial and error; use practice and correction.
  • Pavlov: Classical conditioning; build positive associations with school and learning.
  • Skinner: Operant conditioning; reinforce desired behavior through feedback and praise.
  • Kohler: Insight; give puzzles and problem situations.
  • Piaget: Cognitive construction; use concrete activities suited to the child's stage.
  • Vygotsky: Social interaction and scaffolding; use peer work, dialogue, and guided help.
  • Bandura: Observation; model good behavior, reading, cooperation, and problem solving.
5. Explain transfer of learning and learning plateau.

Transfer of learning means the effect of previous learning on new learning.

  • Positive transfer: Previous learning helps new learning, such as counting helping addition.
  • Negative transfer: Previous learning interferes, such as old pronunciation habits affecting a new language.
  • Zero transfer: Previous learning has little effect.

Learning plateau is a temporary pause in visible progress. It can be reduced by rest, method change, feedback, step-by-step work, and motivation.
