Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define personality in educational psychology.

Personality is the integrated and organized pattern of a child's behavior, emotions, habits, attitudes, adjustment, and social relations. It shows how the child thinks, feels, behaves, and relates with the environment.

2. Write any three factors affecting personality development.

Important factors are heredity, family environment, school climate, peer group, society and culture, media, health, nutrition, and emotional security. These factors shape confidence, habits, values, and social behavior.

3. What is adjustment?

Adjustment is the continuous process by which a child maintains a balanced relation between personal needs and the demands of home, school, peers, and society. A well-adjusted child participates, cooperates, follows rules, and handles failure with support.

4. Mention four areas of individual differences.

Individual differences may be physical, mental, emotional, linguistic, social, creative, and interest-based. Children may differ in health, intelligence, readiness, language, pace, confidence, family background, and aptitude.

5. Why should personality assessment not be used for labeling?

Personality is dynamic and changes with experience, guidance, and environment. Labeling can reduce confidence and opportunity. Assessment should be used to understand causes, provide support, and guide the child's development.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning, features, and types of personality.

Personality is the organized pattern of the whole individual. It includes behavior, emotions, habits, attitudes, values, adjustment, and social relations.

  • Features: It is unique, integrated, dynamic, influenced by heredity and environment, and expressed through adjustment and social behavior.
  • Introvert: quiet, reflective, may prefer individual or small-group work.
  • Extrovert: active, expressive, sociable, and group-oriented.
  • Ambivert: shows both introvert and extrovert tendencies according to situation.
2. Discuss the factors affecting personality development.
  • Heredity: body structure, temperament, basic capacity, and energy level.
  • Family: affection, discipline, language, values, parenting style, and security.
  • School: teacher behavior, peer acceptance, activities, praise, failure, and discipline.
  • Peer group: friendship, cooperation, rejection, leadership, and competition.
  • Society and culture: customs, gender expectations, community values, and local experiences.
  • Health and media: nutrition, illness, fatigue, screen exposure, stories, and role models.

A teacher should create a safe, encouraging, fair, and inclusive environment because school experiences strongly influence personality.

4. What are individual differences? Explain their causes and educational importance.

Individual differences are natural differences among children in ability, interest, pace, language, health, emotion, creativity, family background, and learning readiness.

  • Causes: heredity, environment, age, maturity, health, nutrition, socio-economic background, language, gender socialization, motivation, and interest.
  • Importance: They help the teacher plan child-centered methods, remedial teaching, enrichment, flexible grouping, inclusive education, and guidance.
  • Classroom link: Equal dignity must be given to all learners, but the method, pace, practice, and support may differ according to need.
5. Suggest classroom strategies for handling personality and individual differences.
  • Observe children in different situations before judging them.
  • Use differentiated tasks, flexible groups, peer tutoring, and multisensory teaching.
  • Give quiet children preparation time and active children responsible leadership roles.
  • Provide remedial support for learning gaps and enrichment for advanced learners.
  • Use positive discipline, fair rules, private correction, and praise for effort.
  • Build emotional security through affection, guidance, success experiences, and parent contact.
  • Never use personality type or individual difference as a fixed label.
