Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define educational statistics.

Educational statistics is the use of statistical methods to collect, organize, present, analyze, and interpret educational data such as marks, attendance, progress, and survey responses.

2. What is frequency?

Frequency is the number of times a score, value, category, or class interval occurs in a set of data.

3. Write the formula for mean.

Mean = Sum of all scores divided by number of scores. For example, if scores are 5, 7, 8, then mean = 20 / 3.

4. Differentiate median and mode in one line.

Median is the middle value after arranging scores, while mode is the most frequently occurring value.

5. Name two educational uses of statistics.

Statistics helps identify weak areas, plan remedial teaching, prepare progress reports, compare performance, study attendance, and conduct action research.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and importance of educational statistics.

Educational statistics applies statistical methods to educational data. It helps teachers collect, organize, present, analyze, and interpret marks, attendance, progress, and classroom survey data.

  • It makes large data simple and meaningful.
  • It supports evaluation and progress records.
  • It helps identify learning gaps and plan remedial teaching.
  • It helps compare performance before and after teaching intervention.
  • It supports school records, parent communication, and action research.
2. Describe tabulation and graphical representation of educational data.

Tabulation arranges data in rows and columns. Frequency distribution shows how often each value or class occurs.

  • Bar graph: compares separate categories.
  • Line graph: shows change over time.
  • Histogram: shows grouped continuous data.
  • Pie chart: shows parts of a whole.
  • Pictograph: uses symbols for simple visual comparison.

Graphs should have a title, labels, scale, and clear units.

3. Explain mean, median, and mode with examples.
  • Mean: Sum of scores / number of scores. For 8, 10, 12, mean = 30 / 3 = 10.
  • Median: Middle value after arranging. For 5, 8, 12, median = 8.
  • Mode: Most frequent value. For 4, 6, 6, 7, mode = 6.

Mean uses all scores, median uses position, and mode uses frequency.

5. What precautions should a teacher take while using statistics?
  • Collect accurate and honest data.
  • Do not use numbers to label children permanently.
  • Interpret mean carefully when extreme scores are present.
  • Use clear graph titles, labels, scale, and units.
  • Interpret data with classroom context such as health, attendance, language, and teaching method.
  • Use statistics to plan support, not to punish or shame learners.
