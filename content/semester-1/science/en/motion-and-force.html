Motion and Force

This chapter explains motion, speed and common forces so that primary children can connect playground experiences with scientific language.

Exam Snapshot

TermLast-minute meaningExample
MotionChange in position with time.A rolling ball.
RestNo change in position with respect to a chosen reference point.A book on a table.
SpeedDistance covered per unit time; speed = distance/time.60 km in 2 h = 30 km/h.
ForcePush or pull that can change rest, motion, shape or direction.Kicking a football.

Types Of Motion

  • Linear motion is motion in a straight line, such as a child running on a track.
  • Circular motion is motion around a fixed centre, such as a stone tied to a string.
  • Rotational motion is spinning about an axis, such as a fan or wheel.
  • Oscillatory motion is repeated to-and-fro motion, such as a swing.

Speed And Simple Calculation

  • Speed tells how fast an object moves.
  • Formula: speed = distance / time.
  • Units: metre per second (m/s) and kilometre per hour (km/h).
  • If a child walks 100 m in 50 s, speed = 2 m/s.

Laws And Effects Of Force

  • Force can start motion, stop motion, change speed, change direction or change shape.
  • First law idea: an object tends to remain at rest or uniform motion unless an external force acts.
  • Second law idea: greater force produces greater change in motion for the same mass.
  • Third law idea: every action has an equal and opposite reaction, such as walking by pushing the ground backward.

Common Types Of Force

  • Muscular force is produced by muscles, like lifting a bag.
  • Gravitational force pulls objects towards Earth.
  • Magnetic force acts between magnets and magnetic materials.
  • Electric force acts between charged bodies.
  • Friction acts opposite to motion and can be useful or harmful.

Concepts And Differences

ConceptKey difference
Distance vs displacementDistance is total path length; displacement is shortest change from start to end.
Speed vs velocitySpeed has magnitude only; velocity has magnitude and direction.
Balanced vs unbalanced forceBalanced forces do not change motion; unbalanced force changes motion.
Friction useful vs harmfulFriction helps walking and writing; it causes wear and energy loss in machines.

Classroom Connection

Use toy cars, balls, magnets, rubber bands and rough/smooth surfaces. Ask children to predict, push, observe and record what changed: speed, direction, distance or shape.

One-minute Revision

  • Motion is change in position with time.
  • Speed = distance / time.
  • Force is a push or pull.
  • Linear, circular, rotational and oscillatory motions are common examples.
  • Friction opposes motion but is necessary for walking, writing and braking.

Common Exam Lines

  • A body is said to be in motion when its position changes with time relative to a reference point.
  • Force can change the state of rest or motion of a body.
  • Primary science should teach motion and force through play, observation and safe experiments.
