Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define measurement.

Measurement is the comparison of an unknown physical quantity with a fixed standard unit and writing it as number plus unit.

2. Write two needs of standard units.

Standard units make measurements uniform and comparable, and they avoid confusion caused by body-based units such as handspan or footstep.

3. Name four measuring instruments.

Ruler or metre scale for length, balance for mass, stopwatch for time and thermometer for temperature.

4. What is parallax error?

Parallax error is the wrong reading obtained when the eye is not kept vertically above the scale mark.

5. Why should units be written with numbers?

A number alone does not tell what has been measured. Units give meaning, such as 5 m, 5 kg or 5 s.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the need and importance of physical measurement.
  • Measurement changes vague statements into exact facts.
  • It is used in trade, construction, medicine, weather, experiments and classroom activities.
  • It helps comparison and fair recording.
  • It develops scientific attitude because children observe, estimate, measure and verify.
2. Describe SI units and common measuring instruments.
  • SI is the international system of units used in science.
  • Length is measured in metre, mass in kilogram, time in second and temperature in kelvin or degree Celsius for classroom use.
  • Common instruments include scale, measuring tape, balance, stopwatch, thermometer, measuring cylinder and rain gauge.
  • Correct choice of instrument improves accuracy.
3. Write precautions while measuring length with a scale.
  • Place the scale along the object without tilt.
  • Start from zero or subtract if starting from another mark.
  • Keep the eye exactly above the mark.
  • Use a suitable scale and write the unit.
  • Repeat reading if needed.
4. How will you teach measurement in a primary classroom?
  • Begin with non-standard units such as handspan and footsteps.
  • Let groups compare results and find why answers differ.
  • Introduce metre scale and standard units.
  • Use station work for length, mass, time and temperature.
  • Ask children to record number, unit and observation.
