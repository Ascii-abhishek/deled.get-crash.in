Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define model in science teaching.

A model is a simplified representation of an object, idea or process used to make a scientific concept clear.

2. Write four qualities of a good model.

A good model is scientifically correct, safe, low-cost, clearly labelled and useful for child participation.

3. Name three possible science model themes.

Rainwater harvesting, laws of motion, electromagnetic doorbell, states of matter and types of force are suitable themes.

4. What should be written in a model report?

Aim, materials, procedure, principle, precautions, labelled diagram and classroom use should be written.

5. Why are low-cost materials preferred?

Low-cost local materials make the model practical for ordinary schools and encourage creativity without waste.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the steps of preparing a science model.
  • Select a concept and learning objective.
  • Collect safe and low-cost materials.
  • Draw a rough labelled plan.
  • Prepare, test and improve the model.
  • Write principle, precautions and classroom use.
2. Describe a rainwater harvesting model.
  • Show a sloping roof that collects rainwater.
  • Attach pipe to carry water.
  • Add filter layer using cloth, sand or gravel representation.
  • Collect water in a tank and show recharge pit.
  • Use it to discuss water conservation.
3. Differentiate between working and static models.
  • Working model shows movement or a process.
  • Static model shows structure or arrangement.
  • Electric bell is a working model.
  • A labelled chart of water cycle may be static.
  • Both are useful when linked with explanation.
4. How does model preparation help trainee teachers?
  • It develops planning and creativity.
  • It connects theory with classroom practice.
  • It encourages low-cost TLM preparation.
  • It improves presentation and questioning skill.
  • It makes science activity-based.
