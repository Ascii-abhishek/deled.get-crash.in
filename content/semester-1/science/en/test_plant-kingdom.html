Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define plant kingdom.

The plant body has root and shoot systems, and each part performs a specific function. Plant parts: root, stem, leaf, flower, fruit, seed.

2. Write two important terms from this chapter.

Photosynthesis is the process by which green leaves prepare food. Leaves make food; roots absorb; stems support and transport.

3. Give two classroom examples related to plant kingdom.

Modification of plant parts is an adaptation for special functions such as storage, support, protection, or reproduction. Herbs, shrubs, trees, climbers, and creepers are common groups.

4. Why is observation important in this chapter?

5. Write one common misconception and its correction.

Photosynthesis is the process by which green leaves prepare food. Plants and animals are interdependent.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the main concepts of Plant Kingdom with examples.

Plant Kingdom: Plant kingdom at the primary level means knowing common plants, their parts, functions, usefulness, and simple modifications. A teacher should connect plant study with gardens, farms, food, health, environment, and local occupations.

  • Herbs are small soft-stemmed plants such as coriander and spinach.
  • Shrubs are medium-sized bushy plants such as rose and hibiscus.
  • Trees are tall woody plants such as neem, mango, and peepal.
  • Climbers need support to grow upward, for example pea and money plant.

Make a school herbarium with fallen leaves only. Children group leaves by shape, margin, venation, smell, and use. They label local names and discuss why plucking flowers or uprooting small plants should be avoided.

2. Describe how you will teach this chapter through activities in an elementary classroom.

Plant Kingdom: Plant kingdom at the primary level means knowing common plants, their parts, functions, usefulness, and simple modifications. A teacher should connect plant study with gardens, farms, food, health, environment, and local occupations.

  • Roots absorb water and minerals and hold soil, reducing erosion.
  • Stem bears branches, leaves, flowers, and fruits; it conducts water and food.

  • Flowers help reproduction by forming fruits and seeds.

Plant Kingdom: Plant kingdom at the primary level means knowing common plants, their parts, functions, usefulness, and simple modifications. A teacher should connect plant study with gardens, farms, food, health, environment, and local occupations.
