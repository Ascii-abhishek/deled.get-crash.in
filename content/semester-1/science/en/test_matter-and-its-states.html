Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define matter.

Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space, such as air, water, soil, milk and stone.

2. Write two properties of solids.

Solids have fixed shape and fixed volume. Their particles are closely packed.

3. What is a solution?

A solution is a homogeneous mixture formed when a solute dissolves in a solvent, such as salt in water.

4. Name four separation methods.

Handpicking, sieving, filtration, evaporation, magnetic separation and decantation are common methods.

5. What is condensation?

Condensation is the change of gas or vapour into liquid, such as droplets forming on a cold glass.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the three states of matter.
  • Solid has fixed shape and volume because particles are closely packed.
  • Liquid has fixed volume but takes the shape of container because particles can slide.
  • Gas has no fixed shape or volume because particles are far apart.
  • Examples: ice, water and steam.
2. Describe changes of state with examples.
  • Melting changes solid to liquid.
  • Freezing changes liquid to solid.
  • Evaporation or boiling changes liquid to gas.
  • Condensation changes gas to liquid.
  • Sublimation changes some solids directly to gas.
3. Explain mixtures and methods of separation.
  • A mixture contains two or more substances physically combined.
  • Components keep their properties and can often be separated physically.
  • Sand and water can be separated by sedimentation, decantation or filtration.
  • Salt solution can be separated by evaporation.
  • Iron filings can be separated by magnet.
4. How will you teach states of matter in primary class?
  • Show ice, water and steam or evaporation safely.
  • Let children observe shape and flow in different containers.
  • Use beads to model particle arrangement.
  • Use salt, sand and water for mixture activities.
  • Ask children to draw and explain observations.
