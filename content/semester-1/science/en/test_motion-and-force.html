Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define motion.

Motion is the change in position of an object with time relative to a reference point.

2. Write the formula and unit of speed.

Speed = distance/time. Common units are metre per second and kilometre per hour.

3. Name four types of motion.

Linear, circular, rotational and oscillatory motion are four common types.

4. Write two effects of force.

Force can start or stop motion, change speed, change direction or change the shape of an object.

5. Why is friction useful?

Friction helps us walk, write, hold objects and stop vehicles using brakes.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain types of motion with examples.
  • Linear motion occurs in a straight line, such as a runner on a track.
  • Circular motion occurs around a centre, such as a stone tied to a string.
  • Rotational motion is spinning about an axis, such as a wheel.
  • Oscillatory motion is repeated to-and-fro motion, such as a swing.
2. Explain force and its effects.
  • Force is a push or pull.
  • It can start or stop motion.
  • It can change speed or direction.
  • It can change the shape of an object.
  • Examples include kicking a ball, pulling a door and stretching a rubber band.
3. Differentiate between balanced and unbalanced force.
  • Balanced forces are equal and opposite and do not change motion.
  • Unbalanced force changes rest, speed, direction or shape.
  • A book resting on a table has balanced forces.
  • A pushed toy car moves because of unbalanced force.
4. How will you teach motion and force through activities?
  • Use balls, toy cars, ramps, magnets and rough surfaces.
  • Ask children to predict and then test.
  • Let them measure distance and time where possible.
  • Discuss what changed: speed, direction or shape.
  • Record observations in a simple table.
