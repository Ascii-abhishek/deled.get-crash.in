Matter and Its States

This chapter gives a quick revision of matter, its three common states, properties, dissolving, mixtures and simple methods of separating mixtures.

Exam Snapshot

TermLast-minute meaningExample
MatterAnything that has mass and occupies space.Air, water, stone, milk.
SolidFixed shape and fixed volume.Book, ice, chalk.
LiquidFixed volume but no fixed shape.Water, oil, milk.
GasNo fixed shape and no fixed volume.Air, steam, LPG.
MixtureTwo or more substances physically combined.Salt water, soil, air.

Particle Idea

  • Matter is made of very small particles.
  • Particles have spaces between them and attract one another.
  • In solids particles are closely packed; in liquids they can slide; in gases they are far apart.
  • Heating increases particle movement, so state may change.

States And Change Of State

  • Melting: solid changes into liquid, such as ice to water.
  • Freezing: liquid changes into solid, such as water to ice.
  • Evaporation or vaporisation: liquid changes into gas.
  • Condensation: gas changes into liquid, such as water drops on a cold glass.
  • Sublimation: some solids change directly into gas, such as camphor.

Dissolving And Solutions

  • A solute dissolves in a solvent to form a solution.
  • Salt is solute, water is solvent, salt water is solution.
  • Solubility depends on nature of substance, temperature and stirring.
  • A saturated solution cannot dissolve more solute at that temperature.

Mixtures And Separation

  • Mixtures may be homogeneous, like sugar solution, or heterogeneous, like sand in water.
  • Handpicking separates large visible impurities.
  • Sieving separates particles of different sizes.
  • Sedimentation and decantation separate insoluble solid from liquid.
  • Filtration, evaporation, magnetic separation and winnowing are common methods.

Concepts And Differences

PairDifference
Solid, liquid, gasSolid has fixed shape; liquid takes container shape; gas fills the whole container.
Pure substance vs mixturePure substance has fixed composition; mixture has variable composition.
Soluble vs insolubleSoluble substance dissolves in a solvent; insoluble substance does not.
Evaporation vs boilingEvaporation occurs from surface at any temperature; boiling occurs throughout liquid at fixed boiling point.

Quick Diagram / Memory Hook

Solid: particles close
Liquid: particles close but sliding
Gas: particles far apart
Heat usually moves matter: solid -> liquid -> gas

Classroom Connection

Let children observe ice melting, salt dissolving, sand settling in water and iron filings moving with a magnet. Ask them to draw what they saw before writing definitions.

One-minute Revision

  • Matter has mass and occupies space.
  • Solids, liquids and gases differ in shape, volume and particle arrangement.
  • Heating and cooling can change state.
  • A solution has solute and solvent.
  • Mixtures can be separated by physical methods.

Common Exam Lines

  • Matter is made up of tiny particles which have spaces and attraction between them.
  • The method of separation depends on the properties of mixture components.
  • Activity-based teaching makes states of matter visible to primary learners.
