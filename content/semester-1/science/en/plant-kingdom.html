Plant Kingdom

Plant kingdom at the primary level means knowing common plants, their parts, functions, usefulness, and simple modifications. A teacher should connect plant study with gardens, farms, food, health, environment, and local occupations.

Groups Of Common Plants

  • Herbs are small soft-stemmed plants such as coriander and spinach.
  • Shrubs are medium-sized bushy plants such as rose and hibiscus.
  • Trees are tall woody plants such as neem, mango, and peepal.
  • Climbers need support to grow upward, for example pea and money plant.
  • Creepers spread on the ground, for example pumpkin and watermelon.

Main Parts And Functions

  • Roots absorb water and minerals and hold soil, reducing erosion.
  • Stem bears branches, leaves, flowers, and fruits; it conducts water and food.
  • Leaves prepare food by photosynthesis using sunlight, carbon dioxide, water, and chlorophyll.
  • Flowers help reproduction by forming fruits and seeds.
  • Fruits protect seeds and help seed dispersal.

Usefulness Of Plants And Animals

  • Plants give food, oxygen, medicines, fibres, timber, shade, and habitat.
  • Animals help pollination, seed dispersal, manure formation, transport, and food chains.
  • Plants and animals depend on each other in ecosystems.
  • Human beings must conserve forests and avoid unnecessary cutting of plants.

Concepts And Differences

ConceptMeaning / use
Tap rootOne main root with side branches; seen in mustard and pea.
Fibrous rootMany thin roots from stem base; seen in wheat and grass.
Parallel venationVeins run almost parallel; common in grass-like leaves.
Reticulate venationVeins form network; common in mango and peepal leaves.

Classroom Connection

Make a school herbarium with fallen leaves only. Children group leaves by shape, margin, venation, smell, and use. They label local names and discuss why plucking flowers or uprooting small plants should be avoided.

One-minute Revision

  • Plant parts: root, stem, leaf, flower, fruit, seed.
  • Leaves make food; roots absorb; stems support and transport.
  • Herbs, shrubs, trees, climbers, and creepers are common groups.
  • Modifications help storage, support, protection, and reproduction.
  • Plants and animals are interdependent.

Common Exam Lines

  • The plant body has root and shoot systems, and each part performs a specific function.
  • Photosynthesis is the process by which green leaves prepare food.
  • Modification of plant parts is an adaptation for special functions such as storage, support, protection, or reproduction.
