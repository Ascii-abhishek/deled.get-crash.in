Listening and Correct Pronunciation

This chapter prepares a primary teacher to build careful listening, clear speech, and correct Hindi pronunciation through practice-rich classroom work.

Core Ideas

  • Listening is the first language skill; without accurate listening, correct speaking and reading become weak.
  • Correct pronunciation depends on sound awareness, position of tongue/lips, breath control, rhythm, and repeated practice.
  • The teacher should model standard pronunciation but respect the child's home language and avoid shaming dialect differences.
  • Difficult Hindi sounds include aspirated/unaspirated pairs, retroflex sounds, nasal sounds, and the distinction among श, ष, स.
  • Good listening activities include storytelling, sound games, following instructions, picture description, songs, poems, and dialogue practice.
  • Correction is best done by recasting: child says a word, teacher repeats it correctly in a sentence, and the class practices naturally.
  • Pronunciation practice should move from sound to syllable, syllable to word, word to sentence, and sentence to expressive reading.
  • Teachers should observe clarity, volume, pace, stress, pause, and confidence during oral language activities.
  • Assessment may include oral repetition, reading aloud, listening comprehension, rhyming, and identifying beginning/ending sounds.
  • A supportive environment makes children willing to speak; fear reduces both listening attention and oral fluency.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with a two-minute oral warm-up connected to the child's home and school experience.
  • Model once, practise together, then let pairs practise while the teacher listens and supports.
  • Use blackboard, cards, pictures, real objects, short poems, and quick games instead of long lecture.
  • Keep correction respectful: show the correct pattern and ask the child to try again in a meaningful sentence.
  • End with a tiny exit task: one word, one sentence, one oral response, or one corrected example.

Quick Teaching Flow

Warm-up -> Model -> Guided practice -> Pair work -> Notebook/oral task -> Feedback
