Word Recognition

This chapter builds quick recognition of Hindi words through meaning, sound, form, context, and vocabulary relationships.

Core Ideas

  • Word recognition is the ability to identify written or spoken words quickly and attach meaning to them.
  • It grows through phonemic awareness, letter-sound knowledge, repeated reading, vocabulary, and context use.
  • Children first recognize familiar names, classroom labels, picture words, and high-frequency words.
  • Synonyms enrich vocabulary; antonyms sharpen meaning boundaries.
  • Rhyming words support sound awareness and are useful in poems and early reading.
  • Similar-sounding words require careful listening and sentence context to avoid confusion.
  • Word families, prefixes/suffixes, and compound words help children decode new words.
  • Flash cards should be used briefly; meaningful sentences and stories are more powerful than isolated drilling.
  • A teacher should connect word recognition with speaking, reading aloud, writing, and picture discussion.
  • Assessment can include matching, sorting, odd-one-out, word-picture pairing, and reading short sentences.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with a two-minute oral warm-up connected to the child's home and school experience.
  • Model once, practise together, then let pairs practise while the teacher listens and supports.
  • Use blackboard, cards, pictures, real objects, short poems, and quick games instead of long lecture.
  • Keep correction respectful: show the correct pattern and ask the child to try again in a meaningful sentence.
  • End with a tiny exit task: one word, one sentence, one oral response, or one corrected example.

Quick Teaching Flow

Warm-up -> Model -> Guided practice -> Pair work -> Notebook/oral task -> Feedback
