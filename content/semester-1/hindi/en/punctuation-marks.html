Punctuation Marks

A scoring chapter on the meaning, use, and teaching of Hindi punctuation marks in reading and writing.

Core Ideas

  • Punctuation marks guide pause, tone, meaning, and sentence boundaries.
  • In Hindi, danda (।) is commonly used as full stop; modern texts also use period in some contexts.
  • Comma shows a small pause, separates list items, and can clarify meaning.
  • Question mark is used after direct questions, not after indirect question-like statements.
  • Exclamation mark expresses surprise, joy, pain, command, or strong emotion; overuse weakens writing.
  • Quotation marks enclose exact words spoken or quoted from a text.
  • Semicolon separates closely related independent clauses or complex list items; it is less common in primary writing but useful for teacher knowledge.
  • Wrong punctuation can change meaning and reading expression.
  • Teaching should connect punctuation with oral reading: pause at comma, stop at full stop, raise tone for question.
  • Assessment should include placing marks in sentences, reading with expression, and correcting faulty paragraphs.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with a two-minute oral warm-up connected to the child's home and school experience.
  • Model once, practise together, then let pairs practise while the teacher listens and supports.
  • Use blackboard, cards, pictures, real objects, short poems, and quick games instead of long lecture.
  • Keep correction respectful: show the correct pattern and ask the child to try again in a meaningful sentence.
  • End with a tiny exit task: one word, one sentence, one oral response, or one corrected example.

Quick Teaching Flow

Warm-up -> Model -> Guided practice -> Pair work -> Notebook/oral task -> Feedback
