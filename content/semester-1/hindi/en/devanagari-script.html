Devanagari Script

A compact revision of Devanagari signs, vowels, consonants, matras, conjuncts, and common script errors for primary language teaching.

Core Ideas

  • Devanagari is an akshara-based script: each consonant normally carries an inherent अ sound unless changed by matra or halant.
  • Independent vowels are used when a syllable begins with a vowel; matras are used when a vowel sound follows a consonant.
  • Children should learn sound-letter relation, stroke direction, spacing, shirorekha, matra position, and word formation together.
  • Common confusions include ब-व, श-ष-स, ड़-ढ़, short/long vowel matras, and placement of ि before the consonant shape.
  • Halant removes the inherent vowel and helps in conjunct formation, as in क् + त = क्त.
  • Teaching should use sand tray writing, air writing, letter cards, matra charts, picture-word matching, and dictation.
  • Copying alone is insufficient; children need to read, say, trace, write, and use letters in meaningful words.
  • Correct handwriting includes proportion, line discipline, spacing between words, and complete matras.
  • Assessment should check recognition, formation, pronunciation, and use of letters in words.
  • The teacher should connect script learning with stories, names, classroom labels, and environmental print.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with a two-minute oral warm-up connected to the child's home and school experience.
  • Model once, practise together, then let pairs practise while the teacher listens and supports.
  • Use blackboard, cards, pictures, real objects, short poems, and quick games instead of long lecture.
  • Keep correction respectful: show the correct pattern and ask the child to try again in a meaningful sentence.
  • End with a tiny exit task: one word, one sentence, one oral response, or one corrected example.

Quick Teaching Flow

Warm-up -> Model -> Guided practice -> Pair work -> Notebook/oral task -> Feedback
