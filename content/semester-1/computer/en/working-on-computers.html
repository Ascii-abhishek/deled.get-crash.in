Working on Computers

Learn operating system basics, Windows desktop, file management, control panel, safe start/shutdown, and common applications.

Key Points

  • The operating system is the bridge between user and computer hardware.
  • Start the computer by checking power, switching on UPS/CPU/monitor, logging in, and waiting until desktop loads.
  • Shut down through the operating system menu; avoid direct power-off because files may be damaged.
  • Create clear folders for subjects, classes, tests, and records before saving files.
  • Use copy, move, rename, delete, restore, search, and shortcut operations carefully.
  • Common windows have title bar, menu/ribbon, scroll bars, minimize, maximize, and close buttons.
  • Calculator, Notepad, Paint, browser, and PDF reader are useful for daily school work.
  • Good habits include strong passwords, regular backup, antivirus update, and not installing unknown software.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with learners own experience, then place the key vocabulary on the board in a small table.
  • Use pair or group activity so children learn by doing and explaining.
  • Connect the topic with school records, local resources, attendance, water saving, lesson planning, or community life.
  • Support weak readers with pictures, oral instructions, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute oral recap and one short written question.
