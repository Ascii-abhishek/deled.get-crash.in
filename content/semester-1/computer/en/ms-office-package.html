MS Office Package

Revise the basic use of MS Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Access, and office-package skills needed by a teacher.

Key Points

  • MS Office is an office package: several applications used together for documentation, calculation, presentation, and records.
  • Word skills include typing, font, paragraph, page setup, table, header-footer, spell check, print, and saving as PDF.
  • Excel skills include rows, columns, cell references, formulas, auto-fill, sorting, filtering, charts, and functions such as SUM and AVERAGE.
  • PowerPoint skills include slide layout, readable text, images, charts, transitions, speaker notes, and slideshow presentation.
  • Access stores structured records in tables and can use forms, queries, and reports.
  • A teacher can prepare annual plans in Word, assessment analysis in Excel, teaching slides in PowerPoint, and student records in Access.
  • Files should be named clearly, saved in correct folders, and backed up.
  • Good office work is accurate, readable, consistent, printable, and easy for others to understand.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with learners own experience, then place the key vocabulary on the board in a small table.
  • Use pair or group activity so children learn by doing and explaining.
  • Connect the topic with school records, local resources, attendance, water saving, lesson planning, or community life.
  • Support weak readers with pictures, oral instructions, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute oral recap and one short written question.
