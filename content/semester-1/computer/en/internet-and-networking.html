Internet and Networking

Understand networks, internet components, online services, educational websites, downloading, uploading, software updates, and data security.

Key Points

  • Networks help share printers, files, internet connection, and learning resources.
  • Common network types are PAN, LAN, MAN, and WAN; the internet is the largest WAN.
  • A website contains linked web pages stored on a server and opened through a browser.
  • Hyperlinks connect one page, file, or location to another.
  • Online services include WWW, email, chat, video calling, cloud storage, e-governance, and educational portals.
  • Teachers should prefer authentic educational sources such as government, DIET, SCERT, NCERT, and official school portals.
  • Downloading means receiving a file from the internet; uploading means sending a file to the internet.
  • Security habits include strong passwords, antivirus updates, safe browsing, backups, and avoiding suspicious links.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with learners own experience, then place the key vocabulary on the board in a small table.
  • Use pair or group activity so children learn by doing and explaining.
  • Connect the topic with school records, local resources, attendance, water saving, lesson planning, or community life.
  • Support weak readers with pictures, oral instructions, and small steps.
  • Close with a two-minute oral recap and one short written question.
