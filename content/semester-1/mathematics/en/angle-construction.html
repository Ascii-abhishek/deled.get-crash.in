Angle Construction

This chapter revises how to construct standard angles, especially 60, 90 and 120 degrees, using ruler and compass with clear steps.

Before Construction

  • Keep pencil sharp and compass tight so arcs are clear.
  • Draw a base ray first and mark the vertex clearly.
  • Do not change compass width unless the step says so.
  • Label points in order and join them with a ruler.
  • Check the constructed angle with a protractor after completion if available.

Constructing 60 Degrees

  1. Draw ray OA.
  2. With O as centre and any radius, draw an arc cutting OA at B.
  3. With B as centre and same radius, cut the arc at C.
  4. Join OC. Angle AOC is 60 degrees.

Constructing 90 Degrees

  1. Draw ray OA and mark vertex O.
  2. With O as centre, draw an arc cutting OA at B.
  3. With B as centre and same radius, mark C on the arc; with C as centre, mark D on the arc.
  4. With C and D as centres and equal radius, draw arcs intersecting at P.
  5. Join OP. Angle AOP is 90 degrees.

Constructing 120 Degrees

  1. Construct a 60-degree angle on base ray OA.
  2. Using the same arc, step off one more equal arc from the 60-degree point.
  3. Mark the new point D on the arc.
  4. Join OD. Angle AOD is 120 degrees.

Common Errors

  • Changing compass width during equal-arc steps changes the angle.
  • Not keeping the vertex fixed leads to inaccurate construction.
  • Thick or unclear arcs make the intersection hard to identify.
  • Students may draw by guessing instead of following construction logic.

Classroom Connection

Let children first estimate angles with paper folding and clock hands, then use ruler and compass. This links visual sense with formal construction.

One-minute Revision

  • Construction means accurate drawing with instruments.
  • 60 degrees is made by equal arcs from the vertex and first arc point.
  • 90 degrees is a perpendicular or right angle.
  • 120 degrees is two 60-degree steps on the same arc.
  • Same compass radius is the key to accuracy.

Common Exam Lines

  • Compass construction depends on equal distances shown through arcs.
  • Students should write construction steps and draw a neat labelled figure.
  • Angle construction develops accuracy, spatial thinking and instrument handling.
