Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define fraction.

A fraction represents one or more equal parts of a whole or group.

2. What are numerator and denominator?

The numerator tells selected parts; the denominator tells total equal parts.

3. What are equivalent fractions?

Equivalent fractions are different fractions with the same value, such as 1/2 and 2/4.

4. How do we add unlike fractions?

We make the denominators same using LCM, then add the numerators.

5. Name two TLMs for teaching fractions.

Paper folding, fraction strips, number lines, cut-outs and food-sharing activities are useful.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and types of fractions.
  • A fraction represents equal parts of a whole or group.
  • Proper fractions have numerator smaller than denominator.
  • Improper fractions have numerator equal to or greater than denominator.
  • Mixed fractions contain a whole number and a proper fraction.
  • Like fractions have same denominators; unlike fractions have different denominators.
2. Describe equivalent fractions and simplest form.
  • Equivalent fractions have the same value.
  • They are made by multiplying or dividing numerator and denominator by the same non-zero number.
  • Example: 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6.
  • Simplest form is obtained by dividing by the HCF.
  • Pictures and fraction strips make the idea clear.
3. Explain operations on fractions.
  • For like fractions, add or subtract numerators and keep denominator.
  • For unlike fractions, first make common denominator.
  • For multiplication, multiply numerators and denominators.
  • For division, multiply by reciprocal of the second fraction.
  • Use diagrams before symbolic rules in primary classes.
4. How should fractions be taught in primary classes?
  • Begin with concrete sharing and folding activities.
  • Stress that parts must be equal.
  • Use fraction strips, number lines and local examples.
  • Discuss common errors such as adding denominators.
  • Assess through drawing, shading, comparison and story problems.
