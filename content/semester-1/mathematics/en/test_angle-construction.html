Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is geometrical construction?

It is accurate drawing of figures using instruments such as ruler and compass.

2. Name two instruments used in angle construction.

Ruler and compass are the main instruments; a protractor can be used for checking.

3. What is a perpendicular?

Two lines or rays meeting at 90 degrees are perpendicular.

4. How is 120 degrees related to 60 degrees?

120 degrees is made of two 60-degree angles.

5. Why should compass width remain same in some steps?

Equal compass width keeps distances equal and makes construction accurate.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Write steps to construct a 60-degree angle.
  • Draw ray OA.
  • With O as centre, draw an arc cutting OA at B.
  • With B as centre and same radius, cut the arc at C.
  • Join OC.
  • Angle AOC is 60 degrees.
2. Write steps to construct a 90-degree angle.
  • Draw ray OA.
  • Make equal arc steps from the base point.
  • Use intersecting arcs above the base ray.
  • Join the vertex to the intersection point.
  • The new ray forms a right angle of 90 degrees.
3. Explain common precautions in angle construction.
  • Use a sharp pencil.
  • Keep compass width unchanged where required.
  • Mark points clearly.
  • Draw thin arcs and straight rays.
  • Label the final angle neatly.
4. How will you teach angle construction in elementary classes?
  • Begin with angle examples from clock hands and door openings.
  • Demonstrate instrument handling.
  • Show each arc step slowly on the board.
  • Let learners practise with peer checking.
  • Use protractor only to verify after construction.
