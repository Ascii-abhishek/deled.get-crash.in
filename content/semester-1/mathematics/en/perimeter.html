Perimeter

Perimeter means the total distance around the boundary of a closed figure. This chapter is useful for daily-life problems such as fencing, borders, running tracks, and classroom measurement activities.

Core Formulae

FigureFormulaExample
TriangleSum of three sides4 + 5 + 6 = 15 cm
Equilateral triangle3 x side3 x 7 = 21 cm
Rectangle2 x (length + breadth)2 x (8 + 5) = 26 m
Square4 x side4 x 6 = 24 cm
Regular polygonNumber of sides x side length5 x 4 = 20 cm
Circle2 x pi x r or pi x dIf r = 7 cm, circumference = 44 cm approximately.

Steps to Solve Perimeter Questions

  1. Check whether the figure is closed.
  2. Identify the shape and all side lengths.
  3. Use the direct formula if the figure is standard.
  4. For irregular figures, add each boundary segment carefully.
  5. Write the final answer with the correct length unit.

Worked Examples

  • Rectangle: A classroom blackboard is 3 m long and 1 m broad. Perimeter = 2 x (3 + 1) = 8 m.
  • Square: A square tile has side 25 cm. Perimeter = 4 x 25 = 100 cm.
  • Triangle: Sides are 5 cm, 7 cm, and 8 cm. Perimeter = 20 cm.
  • Regular hexagon: Side is 6 cm. Perimeter = 6 x 6 = 36 cm.

Teaching Points

  • Begin with walking around the boundary of a desk, mat, or playground.
  • Use thread to measure curved boundaries and then compare with a ruler.
  • Ask children to make shapes with sticks and find total boundary length.
  • Discuss why same perimeter can sometimes enclose different areas.
  • Connect perimeter with real tasks such as ribbon around a chart or fencing a garden.

One-minute Revision

  • Perimeter is one round along the boundary.
  • Rectangle: 2 x (l + b); square: 4 x side.
  • Equilateral triangle: 3 x side.
  • Circumference is perimeter of a circle.
  • Perimeter uses length units, not square units.

Common Exam Lines

  • Define perimeter with an example.
  • Write formulae for perimeter of square, rectangle, triangle, and circle.
  • Differentiate between perimeter and area.
  • Suggest activities to teach perimeter at primary level.
