Data Representation

This chapter revises how ungrouped data is collected, organized and shown through tables, pictographs, bar graphs and pie graphs for quick interpretation.

Exam Snapshot

TermMeaningExample
DataCollected facts or numbers.Favourite fruits of 30 children.
Ungrouped dataRaw data not divided into class intervals.6, 8, 5, 6, 7.

Steps In Data Handling

  1. Collect data through counting, observation, survey or records.
  2. Arrange data in a table with clear headings.
  3. Use tally marks or frequency to count repeated values.
  4. Choose a suitable representation: pictograph for young children, bar graph for comparison, pie graph for parts of a whole.
  5. Interpret the graph by asking highest, lowest, total, difference and reason-based questions.

Reading Graphs

  • Always read the title first to know what the graph is about.
  • Check labels, scale, units and symbol key before answering.
  • In bar graphs, compare bar heights.
  • In pictographs, multiply number of symbols by the value of one symbol.
  • In pie graphs, compare sector size or percentage.

Common Errors

  • Forgetting the pictograph key gives wrong totals.
  • Unequal scales on a bar graph mislead readers.
  • Bars should have equal width; only height or length shows value.
  • Pie graph sectors must add to the whole, not more or less.

Classroom Connection

Ask children to vote for favourite games, then convert the tally into a table, pictograph and bar graph. This connects mathematics with observation, language and classroom decision-making.

One-minute Revision

  • Data means collected information.
  • Tally marks help count repeated data quickly.
  • Pictograph needs a clear key.
  • Bar graph needs title, scale, labels and equal-width bars.
  • Pie graph shows parts of a whole circle.

Common Exam Lines

  • Data representation makes information easier to compare and interpret.
  • A suitable graph depends on the nature and purpose of the data.
  • Students should learn to both draw and interpret graphs from real classroom data.
