Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define variable.

A variable is a letter or symbol that can take different numerical values.

2. What are like terms?

Like terms have the same variable part and same powers, such as 2x and 7x.

3. Simplify 8a - 3a.

8a - 3a = 5a.

4. Write "5 more than n" algebraically.

It is written as n + 5.

5. Why cannot 3x and 3y be added as 6xy?

They are unlike terms because their variable parts are different.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the parts of an algebraic expression.
  • Variables represent changing or unknown numbers.
  • Constants are fixed numbers.
  • Coefficients multiply variables.
  • Terms are separated by plus or minus signs.
  • An expression combines these parts without an equality sign.
2. Explain addition and subtraction of algebraic expressions.
  • Identify like and unlike terms.
  • Group like terms together.
  • Add or subtract coefficients only.
  • Keep variable parts unchanged.
  • Do not combine unlike terms into one term.
3. Describe how algebra can be taught through patterns.
  • Start with concrete patterns such as sticks or tiles.
  • Ask learners to count first few cases.
  • Record numbers in a table.
  • Find the rule using n.
  • Use language such as "three times a number" before symbols.
4. Distinguish expression and equation.
  • An expression is a combination of terms.
  • An equation states equality between two expressions.
  • Expression: 2x + 5.
  • Equation: 2x + 5 = 15.
  • Equations can be solved; expressions can be simplified or evaluated.
