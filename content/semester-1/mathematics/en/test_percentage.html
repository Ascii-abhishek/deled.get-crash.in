Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define percentage.

Percentage is a fraction or ratio expressed with denominator 100. It means per hundred.

2. Convert 3/4 into percentage.

3/4 = 0.75, and 0.75 x 100 = 75%.

3. Find 15% of 200.

15/100 x 200 = 30.

4. Why is percentage useful for comparing marks?

It converts scores with different total marks to the same base of 100, so comparison becomes fair.

5. Name two classroom contexts for percentage.

Marks, attendance, shop discounts, survey data and shaded grids can be used.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and importance of percentage.
  • Percentage means per hundred.
  • It writes a quantity as a part of 100.
  • It connects with fractions and decimals.
  • It helps compare marks, attendance and data with different totals.
  • It is useful in daily life for discount, profit, loss and survey results.
2. Describe methods of finding percentage.
  • Use p/100 x whole to find p% of a number.
  • Use part/whole x 100 to find percentage of a part.
  • Use unitary method by first finding 1%.
  • Use decimal conversion, such as 0.25 = 25%.
  • Check the answer by estimation.
3. How will you teach percentage in primary classes?
  • Begin with a 100-square grid.
  • Shade simple values such as 10%, 25% and 50%.
  • Connect these with fractions and decimals.
  • Use marks, attendance and shop bills.
  • Ask children to explain which quantity is whole and which is part.
4. Write common mistakes in percentage and remedies.
  • Students may forget that percent means out of 100.
  • They may compare raw marks with different totals.
  • They may confuse percentage and number of students.
  • Use grids, ratio tables and real classroom data.
  • Ask students to state the whole before calculating.
