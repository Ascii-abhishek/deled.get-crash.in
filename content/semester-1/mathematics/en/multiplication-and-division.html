Multiplication and Division

This chapter revises multiplication and division as connected operations so that primary teachers can teach computation with meaning, not only memorised procedures.

Meaning Of Multiplication

  • Multiplication begins with equal groups: 5 plates with 4 laddus each means 5 x 4.
  • It can be shown on a number line as repeated jumps of equal size.
  • Arrays connect multiplication with area: 3 rows and 5 columns show 15 objects.
  • Skip counting helps children move from concrete groups to multiplication facts.

Meaning Of Division

  • Sharing model: 20 pencils shared equally among 5 children gives 4 pencils each.
  • Grouping model: 20 pencils packed in groups of 5 makes 4 groups.
  • Division is the inverse of multiplication: if 5 x 4 = 20, then 20 ÷ 5 = 4 and 20 ÷ 4 = 5.
  • Remainders should be interpreted according to the situation, not ignored mechanically.

Algorithms And Mental Strategies

  • Before long multiplication, children should know place value and expanded form.
  • Example: 23 x 14 = 23 x (10 + 4) = 230 + 92 = 322.
  • Long division should be linked with repeated subtraction, grouping and multiplication facts.
  • Estimation checks reasonableness: 398 ÷ 4 is close to 400 ÷ 4 = 100.

Classroom Connection

Use bottle caps or seeds. Ask groups to arrange 24 caps as equal rows, then write all related multiplication and division facts: 4 x 6 = 24, 6 x 4 = 24, 24 ÷ 4 = 6 and 24 ÷ 6 = 4.

One-minute Revision

  • Multiplication means equal groups, repeated addition, arrays and scaling.
  • Division means equal sharing or equal grouping.
  • Multiplication and division are inverse operations.
  • Place value supports written algorithms.
  • Concrete material and story problems prevent mechanical mistakes.

Common Exam Lines

  • Multiplication should be introduced through equal groups and arrays before formal algorithms.
  • Division has two meanings: sharing and grouping.
  • A good primary teacher checks the answer of division using multiplication.
