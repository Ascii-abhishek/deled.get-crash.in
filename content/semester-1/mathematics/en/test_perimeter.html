Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define perimeter.

Perimeter is the total length around the boundary of a closed figure.

2. Write the formula for perimeter of a rectangle.

Perimeter of rectangle = 2 x (length + breadth).

3. What is circumference?

Circumference is the perimeter or boundary length of a circle.

4. Why is perimeter measured in length units?

Perimeter measures boundary length, so it is written in units such as cm, m, or km.

5. Give two daily uses of perimeter.

Perimeter is used for fencing a field and putting ribbon or border around a chart.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Write perimeter formulae for common figures.
  • Triangle: sum of three sides.
  • Equilateral triangle: 3 x side.
  • Rectangle: 2 x (length + breadth).
  • Square: 4 x side.
  • Regular polygon: number of sides x side length.
  • Circle: 2 x pi x radius or pi x diameter.
2. Differentiate between perimeter and area.

Perimeter is the total length of the boundary and is measured in length units. Area is the measure of the surface enclosed inside a figure and is measured in square units. Perimeter helps in fencing, while area helps in tiling or painting.

3. Solve: A rectangular garden is 12 m long and 8 m broad. Find its perimeter.
  • Formula: Perimeter = 2 x (length + breadth).
  • Perimeter = 2 x (12 + 8).
  • Perimeter = 2 x 20 = 40 m.
  • The garden boundary is 40 m long.
4. How will you teach perimeter through classroom activities?
  • Ask children to walk around the boundary of a desk or mat.
  • Use thread to measure curved boundaries.
  • Make shapes with sticks and add their side lengths.
  • Let children find the border length of notebooks and charts.
  • Connect perimeter with fencing, ribbon, and playground tracks.
