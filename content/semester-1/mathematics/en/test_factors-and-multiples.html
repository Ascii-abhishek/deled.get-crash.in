Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define factor.

A factor is a number that divides another number exactly without leaving a remainder.

2. Define multiple.

A multiple is a product obtained by multiplying a number by whole numbers.

3. List factors of 18.

The factors of 18 are 1, 2, 3, 6, 9 and 18.

4. Write the divisibility rule for 9.

A number is divisible by 9 if the sum of its digits is divisible by 9.

5. What are common multiples?

Common multiples are multiples shared by two or more numbers.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain factors with examples.
  • A factor divides a number exactly.
  • Every number has 1 and itself as factors.
  • Factors of 18 are 1, 2, 3, 6, 9 and 18.
  • Factor pairs help list factors systematically.
  • Arrays and equal grouping make factors visible.
2. Explain multiples with examples.
  • Multiples are products of a number and whole numbers.
  • Multiples of 7 are 7, 14, 21, 28 and so on.
  • Multiples are infinite.
  • Every number is a multiple of itself.
  • Skip counting is useful for teaching multiples.
3. Differentiate factors and multiples.
  • Factors divide a number; multiples are produced by multiplication.
  • Factors are finite; multiples are infinite.
  • Factors are generally smaller than or equal to the number.
  • Multiples are generally greater than or equal to the number, except zero.
  • Both ideas prepare students for HCF and LCM.
4. How should factors and multiples be taught?
  • Begin with concrete objects arranged in equal groups.
  • Use arrays for factors and skip counting for multiples.
  • Teach divisibility rules through examples.
  • Use number charts to find common factors and common multiples.
  • Assess with sorting, listing and word problems.
