Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define multiplication.

Multiplication is repeated addition of equal groups. It can also be shown through arrays and skip counting.

2. Write two meanings of division.

Division means equal sharing and equal grouping.

3. What is a remainder?

Remainder is the quantity left after equal division when the total cannot be divided exactly.

4. How are multiplication and division related?

They are inverse operations. If 6 x 7 = 42, then 42 ÷ 6 = 7 and 42 ÷ 7 = 6.

5. Name two teaching aids for multiplication.

Counters, seeds, bottle caps, arrays, number lines and multiplication charts can be used.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain multiplication with properties and classroom examples.
  • Multiplication means equal groups or repeated addition.
  • Arrays and number-line jumps make the idea visible.
  • Important properties are commutative, associative, distributive, identity and zero property.
  • Example: 4 rows of 5 seeds show 4 x 5 = 20.
  • Teachers should move from concrete objects to symbols and algorithms.
2. Explain division as sharing and grouping.
  • In sharing, the number of groups is known and each share is found.
  • In grouping, group size is known and the number of groups is found.
  • Example: 24 pencils shared among 6 children gives 4 each.
  • Example: 24 pencils packed in groups of 6 gives 4 groups.
  • Both meanings should be taught through stories and objects.
3. Describe common errors in multiplication and division.
  • Children may ignore place value in two-digit multiplication.
  • They may confuse divisor, dividend and quotient.
  • They may write a remainder larger than the divisor.
  • They may memorise tables without understanding equal groups.
  • Teachers can use expanded form, arrays, estimation and multiplication checks.
4. How should a primary teacher teach multiplication and division?
  • Begin with daily-life situations such as sharing food or arranging seats.
  • Use concrete material, drawings, arrays and number lines.
  • Connect multiplication facts with division facts.
  • Introduce algorithms only after concept understanding.
  • Assess through oral questions, small tasks and word problems.
