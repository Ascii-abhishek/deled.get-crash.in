Fractions

This chapter revises the meaning, types, comparison and operations of fractions with classroom methods suitable for primary learners.

Meaning Of Fraction

  • A fraction is meaningful only when the whole is divided into equal parts.
  • It can show part-whole, part of a collection, measurement, division or ratio-like comparison.
  • The denominator names the size of parts; the numerator counts how many such parts are taken.
  • Use paper folding, food sharing, fraction strips and number lines before symbols.

Types Of Fractions

TypeMeaningExample
Proper fractionNumerator is smaller than denominator.3/8.
Improper fractionNumerator is equal to or greater than denominator.9/4.
Mixed fractionWhole and fraction together.2 1/4.
Like fractionsSame denominator.2/7 and 5/7.
Unlike fractionsDifferent denominators.1/3 and 2/5.

Equivalent And Simplest Form

  • Multiplying or dividing numerator and denominator by the same non-zero number gives an equivalent fraction.
  • Example: 3/5 = 6/10 because both numerator and denominator are multiplied by 2.
  • Simplest form is obtained by dividing numerator and denominator by their HCF.
  • Example: 12/18 = 2/3 after dividing by 6.

Operations With Fractions

OperationRuleExample
Add like fractionsAdd numerators; keep denominator.2/9 + 4/9 = 6/9.
Subtract like fractionsSubtract numerators; keep denominator.7/10 - 3/10 = 4/10.
Add unlike fractionsMake denominators same using LCM.1/2 + 1/3 = 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6.
Multiply fractionsMultiply numerator by numerator and denominator by denominator.2/3 x 4/5 = 8/15.
Divide fractionsMultiply by reciprocal of the second fraction.3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 3/4 x 5/2.

Comparing Fractions

  • For like fractions, compare numerators: 5/8 is greater than 3/8.
  • For same numerator, the smaller denominator gives the larger fraction: 1/3 is greater than 1/5.
  • For unlike fractions, use common denominator, cross multiplication or a number line.
  • Benchmark fractions such as 1/2 help children estimate quickly.

Common Errors

ErrorCorrection
Adding denominators: 1/4 + 1/4 = 2/8Only numerators are added for like fractions: 2/4.
Thinking larger denominator always means larger fractionUse equal paper strips to show 1/8 is smaller than 1/4.
Ignoring equal partsMake learners divide the whole into equal parts before shading.
Confusing mixed and improper fractionsUse diagrams and conversion practice.

Classroom Connection

Fold paper circles or rectangles into equal parts. Ask children to shade 1/2, 2/4 and 4/8, then compare the shaded regions to discover equivalent fractions.

One-minute Revision

  • Fraction means selected equal parts of a whole or group.
  • Denominator tells total equal parts; numerator tells selected parts.
  • Equivalent fractions have equal value.
  • Unlike fractions need common denominators for addition and subtraction.
  • Concrete models are essential for primary fraction teaching.

Common Exam Lines

  • A fraction is a number representing one or more equal parts of a whole.
  • Equivalent fractions should be taught through pictures, strips and number lines.
  • Many fraction errors come from weak understanding of equal parts.
