Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Differentiate between number and numeral.

Number is the idea of quantity; numeral is the symbol or written form used to represent that number.

2. What is place value?

Place value is the value of a digit according to its position in a numeral.

3. Write the expanded form of 4,305.

4,305 = 4000 + 300 + 0 + 5.

4. Why is zero called a placeholder?

Zero holds an empty place so that other digits keep their correct place value, such as 105.

5. Name two TLMs for teaching place value.

Abacus, place-value chart, bundles of sticks, number cards and currency notes are useful TLMs.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain number, numeral and digit with examples.
  • Number is the idea of quantity, such as five objects.
  • Numeral is the written symbol, such as 5 or five.
  • Digit is a single symbol from 0 to 9.
  • Many digits combine to make numerals like 347.
  • Teachers should use objects before symbols.
2. Explain place value and face value.
  • Face value is the digit itself.
  • Place value depends on position.
  • In 6,482, face value of 6 is 6 but place value is 6000.
  • Place-value chart helps children see ones, tens, hundreds and thousands.
  • Expanded form makes place value clear.
3. Describe the base-ten place value system.
  • The system uses ten digits 0 to 9.
  • Each place to the left is ten times the place to its right.
  • Ten ones make one ten; ten tens make one hundred.
  • Zero works as placeholder.
  • Indian grouping uses ones, thousands, lakhs and crores.
4. How will you teach place value in primary class?
  • Begin with counting objects and making groups of ten.
  • Use sticks, beads, abacus and place-value chart.
  • Ask children to build numbers such as 236.
  • Exchange 10 ones for 1 ten and 10 tens for 1 hundred.
  • Move from concrete material to expanded form and symbols.
