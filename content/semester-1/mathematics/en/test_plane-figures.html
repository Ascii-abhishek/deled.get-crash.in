Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is a plane figure?

A plane figure is a flat two-dimensional shape with length and breadth.

2. Name the main parts of a circle.

The main parts are centre, radius, diameter, chord, arc, and circumference.

3. How is a square different from a rectangle?

A square has all four sides equal, while a rectangle has only opposite sides equal. Both have four right angles.

4. What is a polygon?

A polygon is a closed plane figure made of straight line segments.

5. Give two classroom materials for teaching plane figures.

Paper cutouts and matchsticks can be used to teach plane figures. Tiles, geoboards, and rangoli patterns are also useful.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Write the properties of triangle, rectangle, square, and circle.
  • Triangle has 3 sides, 3 vertices, and angle sum 180 degrees.
  • Rectangle has opposite sides equal and four right angles.
  • Square has all sides equal and four right angles.
  • Circle has a curved boundary and all points on it are equally distant from the centre.
2. Explain important circle terms.
  • Centre is the fixed point inside the circle.
  • Radius is the distance from centre to boundary.
  • Diameter passes through the centre and equals two radii.
  • Chord joins any two points on the circle.
  • Circumference is the circle boundary length.
3. Differentiate between perimeter and area.

Perimeter is the total boundary length of a closed figure and is measured in length units like cm or m. Area is the surface enclosed inside the figure and is measured in square units. Perimeter is used for fencing; area is used for tiling or painting.

4. How will you teach plane figures at primary level?
  • Use familiar objects such as books, plates, tiles, and windows.
  • Let children sort shapes by sides, corners, and curved or straight boundaries.
  • Use paper folding, cutouts, matchsticks, and geoboards.
  • Ask learners to draw figures and label parts.
  • Connect shapes with local art, rangoli, and classroom layout.
