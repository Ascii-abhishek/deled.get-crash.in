Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is data?

Data is collected information, facts or numbers used for study and comparison.

2. What is a pictograph?

A pictograph represents data using pictures or symbols with a clear key.

3. Write two rules for drawing a bar graph.

Use a title, labels, scale and bars of equal width with equal spacing.

4. When is a pie graph useful?

A pie graph is useful when parts of one whole are to be shown.

5. Why are tally marks used?

Tally marks help count repeated items quickly and accurately.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the steps of data representation.
  • Collect data through observation or survey.
  • Arrange it in a table.
  • Count frequency with tally marks.
  • Select a suitable graph.
  • Interpret the graph using questions on total, highest, lowest and difference.
2. Compare pictograph and bar graph.
  • Pictograph uses pictures; bar graph uses rectangular bars.
  • Pictograph is easier for young learners.
  • Bar graph shows category comparison more precisely.
  • Both need titles and labels.
  • Pictograph needs a symbol key; bar graph needs a scale.
3. What points should be checked while reading a graph?
  • Read the title.
  • Check labels and units.
  • Understand the scale or symbol key.
  • Compare values carefully.
  • Look for highest, lowest, total and difference.
4. How will you teach data handling in an elementary class?
  • Use real class data such as favourite fruits or games.
  • Let children collect and tally responses.
  • Convert the tally into a table.
  • Draw pictographs and bar graphs.
  • Ask interpretation questions in simple language.
