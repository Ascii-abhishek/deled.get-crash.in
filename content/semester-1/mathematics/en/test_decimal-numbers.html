Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define decimal number.

A decimal number uses a decimal point to show whole units and parts smaller than one.

2. What are tenths and hundredths?

Tenths are parts when one whole is divided into 10 equal parts; hundredths are parts when divided into 100 equal parts.

3. Convert 7/10 into decimal.

7/10 = 0.7.

4. Why do we align decimal points?

We align decimal points so that ones, tenths, hundredths and other place values are added or subtracted correctly.

5. Give two daily-life uses of decimals.

Decimals are used in money, length, mass, capacity and temperature.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain decimal place value.
  • Decimals extend the base-ten system to parts smaller than one.
  • Places to the right are tenths, hundredths and thousandths.
  • In 26.348, 3 is tenths, 4 is hundredths and 8 is thousandths.
  • Trailing zeroes do not change value, such as 2.5 = 2.50.
  • Place-value charts help children understand the concept.
2. Explain relation between fractions and decimals.
  • Decimals are another way to write fractions with denominators 10, 100, 1000 and so on.
  • 1/10 = 0.1 and 25/100 = 0.25.
  • Grids and money make this relation visible.
  • Some common fractions can be converted by making denominator 10 or 100.
  • This relation supports percentage learning later.
3. Describe operations on decimals.
  • Addition and subtraction require aligned decimal points.
  • Multiplication by 10, 100 and 1000 shifts place value left.
  • Division by 10, 100 and 1000 shifts place value right.
  • In decimal multiplication, count decimal places in factors.
  • Estimation should be used to check answers.
4. How should decimals be taught in primary classes?
  • Begin with money and measurement contexts.
  • Use place-value charts, grids and number lines.
  • Compare decimals by whole part, tenths and hundredths.
  • Discuss errors such as thinking 0.45 is greater than 0.5.
  • Give shopping, measuring and bill-making activities.
