Prime, Composite, LCM, and HCF

Prime And Composite Numbers

  • Prime numbers have exactly two factors: 1 and the number itself.
  • Composite numbers have more than two factors.
  • 1 is neither prime nor composite because it has only one factor.
  • 2 is the smallest prime number and the only even prime number.
  • Every composite number can be expressed as a product of prime factors.

Finding Prime Numbers

  • Use divisibility rules to test whether a number has factors other than 1 and itself.
  • Sieve of Eratosthenes: write numbers, circle 2, cut its multiples, then repeat with next uncrossed number.
  • For primary classes, use a 1 to 100 number chart and colour multiples.
  • Students should understand why 1 is not prime, not merely memorise it.

Methods Of LCM And HCF

MethodLCM useHCF use
ListingList multiples and choose smallest common one.List factors and choose greatest common one.
Prime factorisationTake highest powers of all prime factors.Take common prime factors with lowest powers.
Division methodDivide by common or individual prime divisors until all become 1.Divide only by common divisors; multiply them.

Worked Examples

  • LCM of 12 and 18: 12 = 2 x 2 x 3, 18 = 2 x 3 x 3. LCM = 2 x 2 x 3 x 3 = 36.
  • HCF of 12 and 18: common prime factors are 2 and 3. HCF = 6.
  • If two bells ring every 6 and 8 minutes, they ring together after LCM(6, 8) = 24 minutes.
  • If 24 red beads and 36 blue beads are packed equally in maximum identical packets, use HCF(24, 36) = 12.

LCM And HCF Difference

PointLCMHCF
Full formLeast common multiple.Highest common factor.
NatureA common multiple.A common factor.
Answer sizeUsually greater than or equal to given numbers.Less than or equal to given numbers.
Problem clueEvents repeating together, common time.Maximum equal grouping or sharing.

Common Errors

  • Calling 1 a prime number. Correct it by counting factors.
  • Confusing LCM with HCF in word problems. Look for smallest common repetition or greatest equal grouping.
  • Missing repeated prime factors in LCM. Use factor trees carefully.
  • Using only rules without meaning. Link each method to factors and multiples.

Classroom Connection

Use a number chart to mark multiples of 2, 3, 5 and 7. Children can discover prime numbers as numbers left with only two factors, then use factor trees for composite numbers.

One-minute Revision

  • Prime number has exactly two factors; composite number has more than two.
  • 1 is neither prime nor composite.
  • Prime factorisation breaks a number into prime factors.
  • LCM is the smallest common multiple; HCF is the greatest common factor.
  • LCM solves repeated-event problems; HCF solves maximum equal-grouping problems.

Common Exam Lines

  • Prime numbers should be taught by factor discovery and number charts.
  • LCM and HCF are applications of multiples and factors.
  • Word problems become easier when students identify whether the situation asks for common repetition or equal grouping.
