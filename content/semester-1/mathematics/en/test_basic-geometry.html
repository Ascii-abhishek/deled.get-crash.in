Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define point.

A point represents an exact position and has no length, breadth or thickness.

2. What is a line segment?

A line segment is a part of a line with two fixed end points.

3. Define ray.

A ray starts from one end point and extends endlessly in one direction.

4. What is an angle?

An angle is formed by two rays having a common end point.

5. Give two classroom examples of geometry.

Notebook corners show angles and blackboard edges show line segments.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain line, line segment and ray.
  • A line extends endlessly in both directions.
  • A line segment has two end points and measurable length.
  • A ray has one end point and extends in one direction.
  • These are related but not the same.
  • Use arrows and end points carefully while drawing.
2. Explain angle and its parts.
  • An angle is made by two rays.
  • The rays are called arms.
  • The common end point is called vertex.
  • In angle ABC, B is the vertex.
  • Clock hands and door openings show angles.
3. Compare open and closed curves.
  • Open curve has ends that do not meet.
  • Closed curve encloses a region.
  • A simple closed curve has interior, boundary and exterior.
  • Closed curves are useful for understanding shapes.
  • Children can trace both with thread or chalk.
4. How will you teach basic geometry in primary classes?
  • Begin with objects around the classroom.
  • Let children identify corners, edges and surfaces.
  • Use drawing, folding, sticks and thread.
  • Teach correct naming with capital letters.
  • Move gradually from concrete objects to diagrams.
