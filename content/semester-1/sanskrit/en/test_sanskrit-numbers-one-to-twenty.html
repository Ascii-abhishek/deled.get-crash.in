Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define Sanskrit Numbers One to Twenty as a chapter topic.

Sanskrit Numbers One to Twenty studies the key terms, examples, and classroom use needed for beginners to understand this Sanskrit topic accurately.

2. Why should examples be taught before rules?

Examples make the pattern visible. After children hear, read, and use examples, the formal rule becomes easier and less mechanical.

3. Write two classroom activities for this topic.

Use chart reading, word-card matching, oral repetition, substitution drills, fill in blanks, and sentence-making with familiar words.

4. Mention one common learner error and its remedy.

A common error is confusing similar forms or using a form without context. Remedy: compare examples, model the correct form, and give short repeated practice.

5. How will you assess this topic quickly?

Ask children to identify terms, match examples, fill blanks, read aloud, and make one correct sentence using the target form.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain Sanskrit Numbers One to Twenty with examples.

Explain the key terms one by one, show their examples, and connect them with simple Sanskrit sentences. The teacher should move from oral recognition to written practice, then to short application. Use the examples Basic count, Number chain, Teen pattern, Memory drill, पञ्च पुस्तकानि to make the chapter concrete.

2. Prepare a teaching plan for this chapter.

Start with oral examples, show a chart, explain the pattern, conduct choral repetition, ask pairs to practise, give a short written task, and close with correction plus one-minute revision. Include both recognition and use.

3. Discuss common difficulties and remedies.

Learners may confuse terms, forget endings, pronounce forms incorrectly, or memorise without meaning. Remedies include sound practice, colour-coded charts, comparison tables, sentence context, peer practice, and short daily revision.

4. Describe evaluation for this chapter.

Evaluation should include oral reading, meaning identification, matching terms with examples, fill in blanks, sentence formation, and short translation where appropriate. It should check both memory and actual use.
