Sanskrit Names of Surroundings

A vocabulary chapter for teaching names of classroom objects, family, animals, birds, plants, and daily surroundings in simple Sanskrit.

Core Ideas

  • Vocabulary from surroundings makes Sanskrit less frightening and more usable for beginners.
  • Start with real objects: book, pen, board, door, water, tree, flower, bird, animal, family members.
  • Use the pattern: इदम् पुस्तकम्, एषः गजः, एषा लेखनी, तत् गृहम्.
  • Teach nouns with gender indicators: masculine often -ः, neuter often -म्, feminine often -आ/-ई forms, but exceptions exist.
  • Picture cards, object labels, matching games, and classroom commands make vocabulary active.
  • Pronunciation should be practiced with short vowels, long vowels, visarga, and anusvara.
  • Children should make simple sentences, not only memorise word lists.
  • Connect Sanskrit words with Hindi cognates where helpful: पुष्प - पुष्प, नदी - नदी.
  • Assessment can include word-picture matching, oral naming, fill in blanks, and two-word sentences.
  • A teacher should repeat words in context across several days for retention.

Classroom Connection

  • Begin with a two-minute oral warm-up connected to the child's home and school experience.
  • Model once, practise together, then let pairs practise while the teacher listens and supports.
  • Use blackboard, cards, pictures, real objects, short poems, and quick games instead of long lecture.
  • Keep correction respectful: show the correct pattern and ask the child to try again in a meaningful sentence.
  • End with a tiny exit task: one word, one sentence, one oral response, or one corrected example.

Quick Teaching Flow

Warm-up -> Model -> Guided practice -> Pair work -> Notebook/oral task -> Feedback
