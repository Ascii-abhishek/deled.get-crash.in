Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is district administration?

District administration is the system of officers and offices that implement government work, maintain order, manage revenue, and provide public services in a district.

2. Write two functions of District Magistrate.

The District Magistrate coordinates law and order, revenue work, elections, disaster management, and general administration. Any two may be written.

3. What is the role of Superintendent of Police?

The Superintendent of Police manages district police, crime prevention, traffic, public safety, and law-and-order support.

4. What is a tehsil?

A tehsil is a revenue subdivision in a district, important for land records, certificates, and revenue cases.

5. How is district administration connected with schools?

It supports enrolment drives, scholarships, midday meal monitoring, health programmes, safety drills, and elementary education administration.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the structure of district-level administration.

District administration has the district headquarters, tehsils, blocks, and local bodies. The District Magistrate coordinates administration. The SP manages police, CDO manages development, CMO manages health, and BSA manages elementary education. Tehsils handle revenue, while blocks coordinate rural development.

2. Describe the functions of the District Magistrate.
  • Coordinates general administration.
  • Maintains law and order with police.
  • Supervises revenue and land-related work.
  • Conducts elections and public hearings.
  • Leads disaster management and relief work.
3. Differentiate between tehsil and block.

Tehsil is mainly a revenue unit dealing with land records, certificates, and revenue cases. Block is mainly a development unit coordinating rural schemes, panchayats, and field-level development workers.

4. How can a teacher teach district administration?

A teacher can use flow charts, role play of DM/SP/Tehsildar/BDO, local office mapping, examples of certificates and health services, and discussion of school links such as enrolment and safety drills.
