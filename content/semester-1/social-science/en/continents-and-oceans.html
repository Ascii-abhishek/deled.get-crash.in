Continents and Oceans

The earth's surface is made of large landmasses called continents and vast water bodies called oceans. This chapter builds world-map awareness and helps future teachers connect physical geography with human life.

TermFast meaningExample

Importance for Human Life

  • Continents provide land for settlement, agriculture, forests, minerals, and transport networks.
  • Oceans influence climate, rainfall, trade, fisheries, and global water cycle.
  • Coastal areas support ports, tourism, fishing, and cultural exchange.
  • World map knowledge helps children understand news, environment, and human diversity.

Classroom Connection

Use a wall map or floor map. Ask children to place name cards for continents and oceans, identify India in Asia, and trace the Indian Ocean. A simple blue-green globe activity helps them see that water covers more area than land.

One-minute Revision

  • There are seven continents and five oceans.
  • Asia is the largest continent; Australia is the smallest.
  • Pacific is the largest ocean; Arctic is the smallest major ocean.
  • India lies in Asia and is surrounded by the Indian Ocean region.
  • Oceans influence climate, trade, food, and rainfall.

Common Exam Lines

  • Name all continents and oceans.
  • Differentiate between continent and ocean.
  • Write the importance of oceans for human life.
