Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is rural life?

Rural life is life in villages and countryside areas, closely linked with land, water, seasons, community, and local resources.

2. Name four rural occupations.

Farming, agricultural labour, animal rearing, craft work, fishing, shopkeeping, teaching, and health work are rural occupations. Any four may be written.

3. What is Gram Panchayat?

Gram Panchayat is the elected local self-government body responsible for village-level civic and development work.

4. What is a self-help group?

A self-help group is a small group that encourages savings, credit, and mutual support, often with active participation of women.

5. Mention two problems of rural life.

Seasonal work, irregular rainfall, debt, limited health services, poor roads, and social inequality are rural problems. Any two may be written.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Describe the main features of rural life.

Rural life is connected with land, water, seasons, animals, local markets, and community relations. People may work in farming, labour, dairy, crafts, shops, transport, teaching, and health services. Festivals, fairs, folk culture, and cooperation are important parts of village life.

2. Explain different rural livelihoods.
  • Small farmers cultivate their own land.
  • Agricultural labourers work on others' fields for wages.
  • Animal rearing and dairy provide milk and income.
  • Artisans do weaving, pottery, carpentry, and repair work.
  • Local services include shops, teaching, health, and transport.
3. Explain the role of rural institutions.

Gram Sabha allows adult voters to discuss village issues. Gram Panchayat manages local development, sanitation, roads, water, and welfare work. Schools support education and child development. Self-help groups and cooperatives encourage savings, credit, marketing, and participation.

4. How can a teacher use rural surroundings in teaching?
  • Prepare a village resource map.
  • Discuss local crops, water sources, occupations, and festivals.
  • Invite community workers for interaction.
  • Use local examples in maths, EVS, language, and social science.
  • Teach dignity of labour and respect for local knowledge.
