Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define economics.

Economics is the study of how people use limited resources to satisfy their wants and improve welfare.

2. What is scarcity?

Scarcity means resources are limited in comparison to human wants, so choices must be made.

3. Name four factors of production.

Land, labour, capital, and entrepreneur are the four factors of production.

4. What is consumption?

Consumption is the use of goods and services to satisfy wants, such as eating food or using a notebook.

5. What is saving?

Saving is keeping part of income for future needs instead of spending it immediately.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and scope of economics.

Economics studies how individuals, families, communities, firms, and governments use limited resources. It includes production, consumption, exchange, distribution, saving, markets, welfare, and development. It helps people make wise choices because wants are many and resources are limited.

2. Explain basic economic activities.
  • Production: making goods or giving services.
  • Consumption: using goods and services.
  • Exchange: buying, selling, or barter.
  • Distribution: sharing income as wages, rent, interest, and profit.
  • Saving: keeping income for future use.
3. Differentiate between micro and macro economics.

Micro economics studies individual units such as a consumer, firm, or price of one good. Macro economics studies the whole economy, including national income, inflation, unemployment, and economic growth.

4. How can a teacher teach economics in primary classes?

A teacher can use class budget games, school shop examples, stories about needs and wants, local market visits, role play of buyer and seller, and saving activities to make economics concrete and child-friendly.
