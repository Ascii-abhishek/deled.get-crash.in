Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What are heat zones?

Heat zones are broad latitude belts of the earth based on the amount of solar heat received.

2. Name the major heat zones.

The major heat zones are Torrid Zone, North and South Temperate Zones, and North and South Frigid Zones.

3. Why is the Torrid Zone hot?

It receives direct or nearly vertical rays of the Sun for much of the year, so heat is concentrated.

4. Why are Frigid Zones cold?

They receive very slanting rays, which spread over a large area and pass through more atmosphere.

5. Where are Temperate Zones located?

They lie between the tropics and the polar circles in both hemispheres.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the three main heat zones.

The Torrid Zone lies between the two tropics and receives direct rays. Temperate Zones lie between tropics and polar circles and receive moderate heat. Frigid Zones lie between polar circles and poles and receive very slanting rays, so they are cold.

2. Why does the amount of heat differ on Earth?
  • Earth is spherical.
  • Sun rays are vertical in some areas and slanting in others.
  • Slanting rays spread over a larger area.
  • Earth's tilt and revolution shift the overhead Sun.
  • Atmosphere reduces the strength of slanting rays.
3. Differentiate between Torrid, Temperate, and Frigid Zones.

Torrid Zone is hottest and lies between the tropics. Temperate Zones have moderate heat and lie between tropics and polar circles. Frigid Zones are coldest and lie near the poles.

4. How will you teach heat zones in primary classes?

Use a torch as the Sun and a ball as the earth. Show straight rays near the middle and slanting rays near the top and bottom. Then mark the tropics and polar circles on a simple diagram and connect them with heat zones.
