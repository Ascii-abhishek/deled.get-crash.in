Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is transport?

Transport is the movement of people and goods from one place to another.

2. Name five means of transport.

Road, rail, water, air, and pipeline are means of transport.

3. Write two road-safety rules for children.

Children should use zebra crossings, look right-left-right before crossing, wear helmets on two-wheelers, and never play on roads. Any two may be written.

4. Why is public transport useful?

Public transport carries many people together, reduces traffic pressure, saves fuel, and can reduce pollution when used well.

5. What does a yellow traffic light mean?

A yellow light means slow down, be alert, and get ready; it does not mean rush through the crossing.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the importance of transport.

Transport connects homes, schools, markets, hospitals, farms, factories, and offices. It supports agriculture, industry, trade, emergency services, tourism, education, and social contact. Without transport, goods and people cannot move efficiently.

2. Describe different means of transport.
  • Road transport: bus, car, truck, cycle.
  • Rail transport: train and metro for many passengers and goods.
  • Water transport: boats and ships.
  • Air transport: aeroplanes and helicopters.
  • Pipeline: oil, gas, and water.
3. Write road-safety rules for children.

Children should walk on footpaths, cross at zebra crossings, check both sides, wear helmets and seat belts, avoid playing on roads, board buses only after they stop, and stay away from railway tracks.

4. How can a teacher teach transport and safety?

A teacher can use road-sign charts, role play, school safety walks, local map making, traffic light games, bus-safety discussion, and data collection on vehicles to connect transport with everyday safety.
