Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What do the Upanishads mainly discuss?

They discuss self, ultimate reality, knowledge, karma, rebirth, and liberation.

2. Name two principles of Jainism.

Ahimsa and truth are two major principles. Non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possession are also important.

3. What are the Four Noble Truths about?

They explain suffering, its cause, its ending, and the path that leads to the end of suffering.

4. What is the Eightfold Path?

It is the Buddhist path of right view, thought, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, and concentration.

5. Why did new religious ideas grow in this period?

Complex rituals, social change, urban growth, trade, and questions about suffering and liberation encouraged new ideas.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the main ideas of the Upanishads.
  • They discuss Atman, Brahman, karma, rebirth, and liberation.
  • They value knowledge and reflection.
  • They use teacher-student dialogue.
  • They shift attention from external ritual to inner understanding.
  • They influenced later Indian philosophical thought.
2. Describe the main teachings of Jainism.

Jainism teaches ahimsa, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, and non-possession. It gives importance to self-control, austerity, karma, and liberation of the soul. Mahavira is remembered as the twenty-fourth Tirthankara.

3. Describe the main teachings of Buddhism.
  • Buddha taught the Middle Path.
  • The Four Noble Truths explain suffering and its end.
  • The Eightfold Path gives practical discipline.
  • Buddhism emphasizes compassion, ethical conduct, meditation, and wisdom.
  • The Sangha helped spread Buddhist teachings.
4. Compare Jainism and Buddhism.

Both questioned ritualism and stressed ethical conduct, self-discipline, and liberation. Jainism gives central importance to strict ahimsa, soul, and austerity. Buddhism emphasizes the Middle Path, Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path, compassion, and meditation.
