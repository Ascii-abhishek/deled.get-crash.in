Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define map.

A map is a flat representation of the earth or a part of the earth drawn with scale, direction, symbols, and colours.

2. What is a map scale?

Scale is the ratio between distance on a map and the actual distance on the ground, such as 1 cm representing 1 km.

3. Name three types of maps.

Physical maps, political maps, and thematic maps are three important types of maps.

4. What is a legend?

A legend or key explains the symbols, colours, and signs used on a map.

5. Why are symbols used in maps?

Symbols save space, make maps easy to read, and show many features clearly in a small area.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the main elements of a map.
  • Direction tells north, south, east, and west.
  • Scale connects map distance with real distance.
  • Symbols show features such as roads, rivers, towns, and forests.
  • Colours help separate landforms and water bodies.
  • Legend explains the symbols and colours.
2. Differentiate between map, sketch, and plan.

A map is a scaled flat representation of a large or small area. A sketch is a rough drawing without exact scale, often used for simple routes. A plan is a large-scale detailed drawing of a small place such as a room or building.

3. Describe the types of maps with examples.

Physical maps show natural features like mountains and rivers. Political maps show countries, states, districts, and capitals. Thematic maps show one subject such as rainfall, crops, population, or minerals.

4. How will you teach mapping to primary children?
  • Start with the classroom and school route.
  • Use direction games for north, south, east, and west.
  • Let children create a simple legend.
  • Move from local map to district, state, India, and world map.
  • Use questioning and observation instead of only memorisation.
