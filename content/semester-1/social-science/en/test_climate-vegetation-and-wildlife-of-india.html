Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Differentiate between weather and climate.

Weather is the daily condition of air. Climate is the average weather condition of a place over a long period.

2. Name the main seasons of India.

Cold weather, hot weather, south-west monsoon, and retreating monsoon are the main seasons.

3. What is natural vegetation?

Natural vegetation means plants that grow naturally in an area without being planted by people.

4. Name any three types of natural vegetation in India.

Evergreen forests, deciduous forests, thorn forests, mountain vegetation, and mangroves are examples. Any three may be written.

5. Why are protected areas important?

Protected areas conserve wildlife, habitats, forests, and biodiversity by reducing hunting and habitat destruction.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Describe the seasons of India.
  • Cold weather season lasts roughly from December to February.
  • Hot weather season lasts from March to May.
  • South-west monsoon from June to September brings most rainfall.
  • Retreating monsoon from October to November marks withdrawal and some coastal rainfall.
2. Explain the types of natural vegetation in India.

India has evergreen forests in heavy rainfall areas, deciduous forests in monsoon regions, thorn vegetation in dry areas, mountain vegetation in the Himalayas, and mangrove vegetation in tidal deltaic coasts such as the Sundarbans.

3. Why is wildlife conservation necessary?

Wildlife conservation protects animals, habitats, food chains, ecological balance, and biodiversity. It also prevents extinction, supports forests, and teaches respect for nature. Sanctuaries, national parks, biosphere reserves, and community awareness are important methods.

4. How can this chapter be taught in an elementary classroom?
  • Use weather charts and season calendars.
  • Show a vegetation map of India.
  • Use picture cards of trees and animals.
  • Discuss local rainfall, crops, birds, and trees.
  • Organise a poster activity on forest and wildlife conservation.
