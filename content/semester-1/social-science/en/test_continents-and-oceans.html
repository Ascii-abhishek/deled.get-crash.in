Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define continent.

A continent is a very large continuous landmass, such as Asia, Africa, or Europe.

2. Define ocean.

An ocean is a very large body of salty water, such as the Pacific Ocean or Indian Ocean.

3. Name the seven continents.

Asia, Africa, North America, South America, Antarctica, Europe, and Australia.

4. Name the five oceans.

Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic Oceans.

5. What is a peninsula?

A peninsula is land surrounded by water on three sides. The southern part of India forms a peninsula.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Describe the seven continents briefly.
  • Asia is largest and includes India.
  • Africa is second largest and crossed by the Equator.
  • North America and South America lie mainly in the Western Hemisphere.
  • Antarctica is ice-covered near the South Pole.
  • Europe is west of Asia.
  • Australia is the smallest continent.
2. Describe the five oceans briefly.

Pacific is the largest and deepest. Atlantic is second largest. Indian Ocean is south of Asia and touches India. Southern Ocean surrounds Antarctica. Arctic Ocean lies around the North Pole and is the smallest major ocean.

3. Differentiate between continents and oceans.

Continents are large landmasses where people live, farm, build settlements, and use resources. Oceans are vast salt-water bodies that influence climate, rainfall, trade, fisheries, and the water cycle.

4. How will you teach continents and oceans?
  • Use a globe and world map.
  • Ask children to place labels on continents and oceans.
  • Locate India in Asia and Indian Ocean nearby.
  • Use colour coding: green or brown for land and blue for water.
  • Connect with news, travel, climate, and food examples.
