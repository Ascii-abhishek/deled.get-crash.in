Introduction to Economics

This chapter introduces economics as the study of human needs, limited resources, choice, production, consumption, exchange, distribution, and simple economic life around children.

Exam Snapshot

Meaning And Scope

  • Economics studies how individuals, families, communities, firms, and governments make decisions about resources.
  • Human wants are many, but money, time, land, labour, and natural resources are limited.
  • Because resources are scarce, every person and society must make choices and set priorities.
  • Economics is not only about money; it includes work, production, consumption, savings, welfare, markets, and development.

Basic Economic Activities

ActivityMeaningExample
ProductionMaking goods or providing services.Farmer grows wheat; teacher teaches.
ConsumptionUsing goods and services to satisfy wants.Eating food, using a notebook.
ExchangeBuying, selling, or barter of goods and services.Buying vegetables from market.
DistributionSharing income among factors of production.Wages, rent, interest, profit.
SavingKeeping part of income for future use.Bank deposit, self-help group saving.

Factors Of Production

  • Land: Natural resources such as soil, water, forest, minerals, and location.
  • Labour: Human physical and mental effort used in work.
  • Capital: Tools, machines, buildings, money, and materials used for production.
  • Entrepreneur: Person who organises land, labour, and capital, takes risk, and starts production.

BasisMicro economicsMacro economics
FocusIndividual units.Whole economy.

Needs, Wants, And Luxury

  • Need: Essential for life and dignity, such as food, shelter, health, and education.
  • Want: Desire that may vary by person, place, income, and time.
  • Luxury: Costly item or service beyond basic need, often linked with comfort or status.
  • Good economic education helps children understand responsible use, sharing, saving, and avoiding waste.

Classroom Connection

Use a simple class budget activity: give groups a fixed imaginary amount for a school picnic and ask them to choose food, transport, first-aid, and learning material. Children learn scarcity, choice, priority, and opportunity cost through discussion.

One-minute Revision

  • Economics studies the use of limited resources for unlimited wants.
  • Scarcity forces choice; every choice has an opportunity cost.
  • Production, consumption, exchange, distribution, and saving are basic economic activities.
  • Land, labour, capital, and entrepreneur are factors of production.
  • Economics connects strongly with everyday family, school, market, and community life.

Common Exam Lines

  • Define economics in simple words.
  • Explain scarcity and choice with example.
  • Write the basic economic activities.
  • Differentiate between micro and macro economics.
