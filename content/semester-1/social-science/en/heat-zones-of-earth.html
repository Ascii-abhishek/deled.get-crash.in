Heat Zones of Earth

Heat zones are broad belts of the earth based on the amount of solar heat received. The unequal heating happens because the earth is spherical, tilted, and receives vertical or slanting rays at different latitudes.

Why Heat Differs on Earth

  • The earth is round, so sunlight does not fall equally everywhere.
  • Vertical rays heat a smaller area strongly; slanting rays spread over a larger area and give less heat.
  • The atmosphere filters and scatters sunlight more where rays are slanting.
  • Earth's tilted axis and revolution shift the overhead sun between the tropics during the year.

Common Confusions

  • The hottest belt is not exactly one line; it is a broad zone between the two tropics.
  • The Temperate Zone is not always equally pleasant; it can have hot summers and cold winters.
  • Frigid regions are cold mainly because sun rays are very slanting, not because they are always farther from the Sun.

Classroom Connection

Use a torch and a ball. Shine the torch straight near the middle and slanting near the top or bottom. Children can observe that the same light spreads over different areas, making heat zones easier to understand.

One-minute Revision

  • Heat zones are based on solar heat received at different latitudes.
  • Torrid Zone lies between 23.5 degree N and 23.5 degree S.
  • Temperate Zones lie between tropics and polar circles.
  • Frigid Zones lie between polar circles and poles.
  • Vertical rays give more heat; slanting rays give less heat.
