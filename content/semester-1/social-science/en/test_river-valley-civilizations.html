Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is a river valley civilization?

It is an early settled civilization that developed near a river because the river supported farming, water supply, transport, and trade.

2. Name four major river valley civilizations.

Mesopotamian, Egyptian, Indus or Harappan, and Chinese civilizations.

3. Write two features of Indus cities.

Indus cities had planned streets and covered drainage. Many houses used baked bricks, wells, and bathrooms.

4. Why were rivers useful for early farming?

Rivers provided water and fertile alluvial soil, making regular farming and surplus food production possible.

5. Why are seals important historical evidence?

Seals suggest trade, identity marks, symbols, animals, and aspects of Harappan economic and cultural life.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain why river valleys became centres of early civilizations.
  • Rivers supplied water for people, animals, and crops.
  • Floods deposited fertile soil.
  • Surplus food supported crafts, trade, and administration.
  • River transport connected settlements.
  • Irrigation and flood control encouraged cooperation and organization.
2. Describe the main features of the Indus Valley Civilization.
  • Important sites include Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, Dholavira, Kalibangan, Lothal, and Rakhigarhi.
  • Cities had planned streets, baked bricks, wells, bathrooms, and drainage.
  • People practised agriculture, crafts, animal rearing, and trade.
  • Seals, weights, ornaments, pottery, and tools are important sources.
  • The script is not fully deciphered, so archaeology is very important.
3. Compare the major river valley civilizations.

Mesopotamia grew near the Tigris-Euphrates, Egypt near the Nile, Indus near the Indus system, and Chinese civilization near the Huang He and Yangtze. All depended on river water, fertile soil, settled farming, trade, and organized public life, but their scripts, rulers, buildings, and cultural practices differed.

4. How will you teach this chapter in primary classes?
  • Show major rivers and sites on a map.
  • Use clay seals, brick patterns, or model drains.
  • Compare local water sources with ancient river settlements.
  • Ask children to sort objects as archaeological evidence.
  • Use a timeline and picture cards for revision.
