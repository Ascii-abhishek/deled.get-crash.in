Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Name the four Vedas.

Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, and Atharvaveda.

2. What were Sabha and Samiti?

They were assemblies connected with discussion, advice, and decision-making in Vedic society.

3. Mention two changes in the Later Vedic period.

Agriculture expanded with iron tools, and kingship became stronger. Varna divisions and rituals also became more formal.

4. What was a gurukul?

A gurukul was a residential learning system in which students lived with the guru and learnt through discipline, recitation, and practice.

5. Name two Vedic deities.

Indra and Agni are two important Vedic deities. Varuna is another example.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Differentiate between Early Vedic and Later Vedic periods.
  • Early Vedic life was more pastoral; Later Vedic life was more agricultural.
  • Cattle were central earlier; land and crops became more important later.
  • Assemblies were influential earlier; kingship became stronger later.
  • Varna was less rigid earlier; it became clearer and more formal later.
  • Religion moved from simpler nature worship to more elaborate rituals.
2. Describe social and economic life in the Vedic period.

The family was the basic unit, and society was linked with clan and tribe. Cattle rearing was important in the early phase, while agriculture expanded later with iron tools. Crafts such as carpentry, weaving, pottery, and metal work developed. Varna divisions became clearer in the later period.

3. Explain religion and education in the Vedic period.
  • People worshipped deities related to natural forces.
  • Yajnas and rituals became more elaborate in the later period.
  • Philosophical questioning also grew later.
  • Education was oral, residential, disciplined, and value-oriented.
  • Students learnt recitation, conduct, duties, and practical knowledge.
4. How will you teach Vedic life in elementary classes?
  • Use a comparison chart for Early and Later Vedic life.
  • Use role-play of Sabha or Samiti.
  • Compare gurukul with present school.
  • Use picture cards for occupations, food, dress, and rituals.
  • Discuss oral tradition through memory games and recitation.
