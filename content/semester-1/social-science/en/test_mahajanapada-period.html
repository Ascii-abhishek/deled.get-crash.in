Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What is a mahajanapada?

A mahajanapada was a large and powerful territorial state that developed from smaller janapadas.

2. Name four mahajanapadas.

Magadha, Kosala, Vatsa, and Avanti. Other examples include Kashi, Vajji, Kuru, and Panchala.

3. What was a gana-sangha?

A gana-sangha was a political organization in which power was shared by a group of chiefs or clan leaders.

4. Give two reasons for the rise of Magadha.

Magadha had fertile land and access to iron and forest resources. It also controlled important routes and had strong rulers.

5. What were punch-marked coins?

They were early metal coins marked with symbols and used in trade and exchange.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the causes of the rise of mahajanapadas.
  • Agriculture expanded through iron tools.
  • Surplus production supported towns, armies, and officials.
  • Trade routes increased wealth and contact.
  • Population grew and tribes settled into larger territories.
  • Taxation and administration became more organized.
2. Differentiate between monarchy and gana-sangha.

In monarchy, power was held by a king and was often hereditary. In a gana-sangha, power was shared among ruling families, chiefs, or assemblies. Magadha and Kosala were monarchies, while Vajji and Malla are examples of gana-sanghas.

3. Why did Magadha become powerful?
  • It had fertile Ganga plain land and water supply.
  • Iron ore and forests helped tools, weapons, and elephants.
  • Its location controlled land and river routes.
  • Rulers used conquest, diplomacy, alliances, and forts.
  • Rajagriha and Pataliputra became strategic centres.
4. Why is the Mahajanapada period historically important?

It marks the growth of large states, stronger administration, towns, trade, coins, and political forms such as monarchy and gana-sangha. It also created the social background in which Jainism and Buddhism developed.
