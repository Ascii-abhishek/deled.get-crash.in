Movements of Earth

The earth has two main movements: rotation on its axis and revolution around the Sun. These movements explain day and night, year, seasons, solstices, and equinoxes.

Rotation

  • Earth rotates from west to east once in about 24 hours.
  • The side facing the Sun has day; the opposite side has night.
  • Rotation makes the Sun appear to rise in the east and set in the west.
  • The circle separating day and night is called the circle of illumination.

Revolution and Seasons

  • Earth revolves around the Sun in about 365 days and 6 hours.
  • The extra 6 hours make one extra day after about four years; that year is called a leap year.
  • Seasons happen because the earth's axis is tilted and the earth revolves around the Sun.
  • When the Northern Hemisphere is tilted towards the Sun, it has summer and the Southern Hemisphere has winter.

Common Confusions

  • Day and night are caused by rotation, not revolution.
  • Seasons are caused mainly by tilt plus revolution, not by the earth simply moving closer or farther from the Sun.
  • A leap year has 366 days because the extra 6 hours add up to about one day in four years.

Classroom Connection

Use a globe and torch. Rotate the globe to show day and night. Then move the tilted globe around the torch to show seasons. Ask children to predict which hemisphere receives more direct light.

One-minute Revision

  • Rotation takes about 24 hours and causes day and night.
  • Revolution takes about 365 days 6 hours and forms a year.
  • Earth's axis is tilted about 23.5 degrees.
  • Seasons occur due to tilt and revolution together.
  • Equinox means nearly equal day and night.
