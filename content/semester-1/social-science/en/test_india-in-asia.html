Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Where is India located in Asia?

India is located in South Asia and forms a major part of the Indian subcontinent.

2. Name any four neighbours of India.

Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Sri Lanka, and Maldives are India's neighbours. Any four may be written.

3. Name the three main water bodies around India.

Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean are the main water bodies around India.

4. What is the Indian subcontinent?

It is the South Asian region around India, separated by the Himalayas and seas, with shared physical and cultural features.

5. Why is India's location important for trade?

India has a long coastline and a central position in the northern Indian Ocean, linking West Asia, Southeast Asia, Africa, and Europe through sea routes.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Describe India's location in Asia.

India lies in South Asia. The Himalayas are to its north and the Indian Ocean is to its south. The Arabian Sea is on the west and the Bay of Bengal is on the east. This position makes India a major country of the Indian subcontinent and gives it contact with many Asian regions.

2. Explain the importance of India's geographical position.
  • It affects monsoon climate and rainfall.
  • It supports sea trade through the Indian Ocean.
  • It links India with West, Central, and Southeast Asia.
  • It encourages cultural exchange through land and sea routes.
  • It shapes defence and foreign relations.
3. Write about India's neighbours and boundaries.

India shares land boundaries with Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. Sri Lanka and Maldives are nearby island neighbours. Natural boundaries include the Himalayas in the north, the Arabian Sea in the west, the Bay of Bengal in the east, and the Indian Ocean in the south.

4. How will you teach India's location in a primary classroom?
  • Show Asia on a wall map or globe.
  • Ask children to locate India and its neighbours.
  • Use colours for Himalayas, Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean.
  • Let learners label directions and water bodies.
  • Connect the map with travel, climate, food, and news examples.
