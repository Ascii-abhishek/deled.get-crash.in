Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define national income.

National income is the total income earned by normal residents of a country from production of final goods and services in one year.

2. What is GDP?

GDP is the value of final goods and services produced within the domestic territory of a country in one year.

3. What is per capita income?

Per capita income is average income per person, calculated by dividing national income by population.

4. Why should double counting be avoided?

Double counting repeats the same value at different stages of production and makes national income appear larger than it really is.

5. Name three methods of measuring national income.

Production or value added method, income method, and expenditure method are three methods.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and importance of national income.

National income measures a country's yearly income from final goods and services. It shows the size and growth of the economy, helps government planning, supports budget and welfare decisions, and shows the contribution of agriculture, industry, and services.

2. Explain methods of measuring national income.
  • Production method counts value added in producing sectors.
  • Income method counts wages, rent, interest, profit, and mixed income.
  • Expenditure method counts final spending on goods and services.
3. Differentiate between GDP and GNP.

GDP measures final goods and services produced within domestic territory in a year. GNP measures income of residents by adding net factor income from abroad to GDP. GDP is territory-based; GNP is resident/income-based.

4. Write limitations of national income.

National income may undercount unpaid household work and informal work. Per capita income hides inequality. It does not fully show pollution, resource loss, quality of life, or real welfare. Price rise can also make income look higher.
