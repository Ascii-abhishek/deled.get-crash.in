Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define rotation.

Rotation is the spinning of Earth on its axis. It takes about 24 hours and causes day and night.

2. Define revolution.

Revolution is Earth's movement around the Sun in its orbit. It takes about 365 days and 6 hours.

3. What is a leap year?

A leap year has 366 days. It occurs because the extra 6 hours of revolution add up to about one day in four years.

4. What is an equinox?

An equinox is a time when day and night are almost equal, around March 21 and September 23.

5. What causes seasons?

Seasons are caused by Earth's tilted axis and its revolution around the Sun.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Differentiate between rotation and revolution.

Rotation is Earth's spin on its axis and takes about 24 hours, causing day and night. Revolution is Earth's movement around the Sun and takes about 365 days 6 hours, causing a year and, with axial tilt, seasons.

2. Explain the causes of day and night.

Earth rotates from west to east. The half facing the Sun receives light and has day. The other half remains in darkness and has night. As Earth rotates, places move from light to darkness and darkness to light.

3. Explain the causes of seasons.
  • Earth's axis is tilted about 23.5 degrees.
  • Earth revolves around the Sun.
  • Different hemispheres receive direct rays at different times.
  • The tilted hemisphere towards the Sun has summer.
  • The opposite hemisphere has winter.
4. How will you teach Earth's movements in primary classes?

Use a globe and torch. Rotate the globe to show day and night. Move the tilted globe around the torch to show revolution and seasons. Ask children to observe lighted and dark parts and record what they see.
