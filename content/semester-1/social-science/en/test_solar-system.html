Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define solar system.

The solar system is the Sun and all bodies that revolve around it, including planets, satellites, asteroids, comets, dwarf planets, and meteoroids.

2. Write the order of planets from the Sun.

Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

3. What is rotation?

Rotation is the spinning of a body on its axis. Earth's rotation causes day and night.

4. What is revolution?

Revolution is movement around another body in an orbit. Earth revolves around the Sun in about 365 days.

5. Difference between planet and satellite?

A planet revolves around the Sun, while a satellite revolves around a planet. Earth is a planet; the Moon is Earth's satellite.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Describe the main members of the solar system.
  • The Sun is the central star.
  • Eight planets revolve around the Sun.
  • Satellites revolve around planets.
  • Asteroids, comets, dwarf planets, meteoroids, and dust are also present.
  • Gravity keeps the system organized.
2. Differentiate between rotation and revolution.

Rotation is spinning on an axis and causes day and night on Earth. Revolution is movement around the Sun in an orbit and is linked with the year and, with Earth's tilted axis, seasons.

3. Explain inner and outer planets.

Inner planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars. They are rocky, smaller, and closer to the Sun. Outer planets are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. They are large, farther away, and have many moons; some have ring systems.

4. How will you teach the solar system in primary classes?
  • Use balls or circles to show planet order.
  • Use a lamp as the Sun to show light and shadow.
  • Use role-play for revolution and rotation.
  • Make a planet-order memory chart.
  • Clarify common confusions: Sun is a star, Moon is a satellite.
