Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define teaching technique.

A teaching technique is a specific classroom procedure used by the teacher to explain, question, demonstrate, discuss, guide practice, or evaluate learning.

2. Name five teaching techniques.

Question-answer, explanation, demonstration, discussion, drill, storytelling, role play, assignment, illustration, and blackboard work are common techniques.

3. When is demonstration useful?

Demonstration is useful when a process or skill must be shown, such as an experiment, handwriting stroke, craft activity, drawing, or mathematical operation.

4. Write two precautions for question-answer technique.

Questions should be clear, purposeful, and graded. The teacher should give wait time, involve many learners, and avoid insulting wrong answers.

5. Difference between method and technique.

A method is a broad organized way of teaching a lesson, while a technique is a smaller classroom procedure used inside a method.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain teaching techniques and their importance.

Teaching techniques are specific classroom procedures used to make teaching effective. They help the teacher explain, ask, demonstrate, guide practice, use materials, involve learners, check understanding, and give feedback. They are important because they make the class active, reduce confusion, support different objectives, and help children learn through varied experiences.

2. Describe question-answer, explanation, and demonstration techniques.
  • Question-answer: uses planned questions to check previous knowledge and develop thinking.
  • Explanation: presents concepts clearly through simple language, examples, diagrams, and steps.
  • Demonstration: shows how something is done, then allows learners to observe and practice.
3. Discuss drill, discussion, and role play as teaching techniques.

Drill gives repeated practice for mastery of facts or skills. Discussion allows exchange of ideas and is useful for social and value topics. Role play lets children act situations and understand language, values, and social behavior. All three need clear objectives and feedback.

4. How should a teacher select teaching techniques?

The teacher should select techniques according to learning objective, subject matter, learner age, class size, time, available material, and type of learning. Concepts may need explanation and illustration; skills need demonstration and practice; values need story, role play, and discussion; revision may need quiz and drill.

5. Explain good blackboard work and assignment as teaching techniques.

Good blackboard work is neat, organized, readable, and purposeful. It uses headings, diagrams, key words, and correct spacing. Assignment is purposeful classwork or homework with clear instructions and feedback. It should reinforce learning and not become meaningless copying.
