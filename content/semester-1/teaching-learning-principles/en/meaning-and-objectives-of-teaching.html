Meaning and Objectives of Teaching

This chapter explains teaching as a purposeful, interactive, and learner-centered process. For D.El.Ed revision, focus on the meaning of teaching, its wider and narrower meanings, the relation between teaching and learning, characteristics of good teaching, and major objectives of classroom teaching.

Exam Snapshot

Term Last-minute meaning Classroom example
Teaching A planned process that creates conditions, guidance, and activities so learners can learn meaningfully. A teacher uses questions, pictures, examples, and practice to help children understand place value.
Tripolar process Teaching connects teacher, learner, and curriculum/content. The teacher selects poem activities according to the class level and lesson objective.

Meaning Of Teaching

  • Teaching is not only telling facts. It is arranging experiences that help learners understand, practice, think, and apply knowledge.
  • It establishes a relationship among teacher, learner, and curriculum or subject matter.
  • Teaching includes planning, motivating, explaining, questioning, demonstrating, guiding practice, using TLM, evaluating, and giving feedback.
  • Successful teaching is seen in learning: if learners do not understand or use the lesson, teaching remains incomplete.
  • Modern teaching is child-centered, active, democratic, practical, and connected with life.

Teaching As A Tripolar Process

Teaching is often called a tripolar process because three centers work together:

Teacher  <---- interaction ---->  Learner
   \                              /
    \                            /
       Curriculum / Content
  • Teacher: Plans, guides, motivates, explains, observes, and evaluates.
  • Learner: Participates, asks questions, practices, reflects, and applies learning.
  • Curriculum/content: Provides the knowledge, skills, values, and experiences to be learned.
  • The teacher's work is to connect the content with the learner's level, interest, language, and life situation.

Wider And Narrower Meaning Of Teaching

Point Wider meaning Narrower meaning
Scope All influences that help a person learn from birth to death. Formal school or classroom teaching for a planned lesson.
Agencies Family, society, environment, media, peer group, school, and experiences. Mainly teacher, school, textbook, timetable, and classroom activities.
Planning May be direct or indirect, planned or unplanned. Usually planned, organized, and controlled.

Teaching And Learning

  • Teaching is the teacher's organized effort; learning is the learner's achievement or change.
  • Teaching aims to make learning effective, joyful, practical, and meaningful.
  • Teaching is complete only when learners show desirable change in knowledge, behavior, skill, habit, attitude, or value.
  • A teacher may teach by discussion, activity, story, question-answer, experiment, demonstration, play, project, worksheet, or local example.
  • Good teaching therefore asks: What did children learn? not only What did the teacher say?

Characteristics Of Good Teaching

Characteristic Meaning Teacher action
Purposeful It has clear objectives. Write learning outcomes before teaching.
Child-centered It respects age, level, interest, pace, and individual differences. Use simple language, varied tasks, and support for slow learners.
Active Learners participate rather than only listen. Use activities, questions, pair work, and practice.
Democratic Every learner gets respect and opportunity. Encourage questions and avoid fear-based discipline.
Motivating It creates interest and desire to learn. Begin with a problem, story, object, picture, or local example.
Remedial It identifies and removes learning difficulties. Use quick checks, correction, extra examples, and practice.
Practical It links knowledge with life and use. Connect math, language, EVS, and values with daily situations.
Evaluative It checks progress and improves teaching. Use oral questions, observation, worksheets, and feedback.

Main Objectives Of Teaching

  • Knowledge objective: To give useful, life-related knowledge and correct information.
  • Understanding objective: To develop clear concepts, relationships, causes, and reasons.
  • Skill objective: To develop reading, writing, speaking, calculation, observation, drawing, experiment, and problem-solving skills.
  • Interest and motivation: To create curiosity and willingness to learn.
  • Personality development: To develop confidence, expression, creativity, discipline, cooperation, and responsibility.
  • Attitude and value formation: To develop healthy attitudes, moral values, social values, democratic behavior, and respect for others.
  • Vocational and self-reliance objective: To prepare learners for useful work, dignity of labour, and future livelihood skills.
  • Adjustment objective: To help learners adjust with family, school, society, environment, and changing situations.
  • Guidance objective: To guide learners according to their needs, abilities, interests, and difficulties.
  • Citizenship objective: To develop responsible citizens who contribute to society, nation, and world welfare.

Objectives In Three Domains

Domain Focus Example objective
Cognitive Knowledge, understanding, thinking, reasoning, application. Students explain why plants need sunlight.
Affective Interest, attitude, values, appreciation, cooperation. Students show care for classroom plants.
Psychomotor Physical and practical skills, drawing, writing, measuring, handling materials. Students measure classroom objects using a ruler.

Classroom Activities Used In Teaching

  • Subject-based activities, projects, worksheets, and practice tasks.
  • Small-group and large-group work so children learn through cooperation.
  • Educational games, storytelling, poem recitation, role play, and discussion.
  • Question-answer, explanation, demonstration, observation, experiment, and field visit.
  • Writing, oral expression, picture-based description, and local environment study.
  • Use of blackboard, charts, real objects, flash cards, models, and digital aids where available.
  • Evaluation, feedback, self-study, reflection, and remedial teaching.

Teaching Objective Vs Learning Outcome

Teaching objective Learning outcome
Written from the teacher's planning side. Written from what the learner will be able to do.
Example: To teach the meaning of noun. Example: Students identify nouns in five given sentences.
Guides selection of method, material, and activity. Guides assessment of actual learning.
May be broad if not written carefully. Should be observable and measurable as far as possible.

Classroom Connection

In an elementary classroom, the objective should decide the method. If the objective is memorizing a poem, repetition and rhythm may help. If the objective is understanding water conservation, discussion, local observation, drawing, and action tasks will be better. A teacher should ask: What do I want children to know, feel, and do after this lesson?

One-minute Revision

  • Teaching connects teacher, learner, and curriculum/content.
  • Wider meaning: lifelong learning influence from all agencies; narrower meaning: formal planned classroom instruction.
  • Teaching is successful only when meaningful learning and desirable behavioral change occur.
  • Good teaching is purposeful, child-centered, active, democratic, motivating, remedial, practical, and evaluative.
  • Main objectives: knowledge, understanding, skill, interest, personality, values, self-reliance, adjustment, guidance, and citizenship.
  • Objectives should cover cognitive, affective, and psychomotor domains.

Common Exam Lines

  • Teaching is a purposeful interaction among teacher, learner, and curriculum.
  • The main aim of teaching is not mere memorization but development of knowledge, skills, attitudes, and behavior.
  • Teaching and learning are two sides of the same process; teaching becomes meaningful when learning occurs.
  • Good teaching keeps individual differences, learner activity, motivation, and evaluation in mind.
  • Objectives make teaching planned, focused, measurable, and useful for learner development.
