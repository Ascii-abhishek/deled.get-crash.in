Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define communication.

Communication is the process of sending and receiving ideas, facts, feelings, or messages so that the receiver understands the intended meaning.

2. Name the main elements of communication.

The main elements are sender, message, encoding, channel, receiver, decoding, feedback, and noise or barriers.

3. Why is feedback important in classroom communication?

Feedback tells the teacher whether the learner has understood the message. It helps remove doubts, correct mistakes, improve teaching, and make communication two-way.

4. Mention four barriers to communication.

Physical noise, difficult language, unclear message, fear, hesitation, poor listening, wrong medium, and lack of feedback are common barriers to classroom communication.

5. Write four qualities of effective classroom communication.

Effective classroom communication is clear, simple, purposeful, timely, respectful, learner-centered, supported by examples, and followed by feedback.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and importance of communication in teaching.

Communication is the exchange of messages with shared understanding. In teaching, it connects teacher, learner, and subject matter. Without communication, explanation, questioning, motivation, feedback, and evaluation cannot work effectively.

  • It clarifies new concepts and instructions.
  • It creates attention and interest.
  • It encourages learner participation.
  • It removes doubts through feedback.
  • It builds trust and a positive classroom climate.
  • It helps the teacher evaluate understanding and improve teaching.
2. Describe the process and elements of communication.

The communication process begins when the sender has an idea or message. The sender encodes it into words, symbols, gestures, pictures, or writing. The message travels through a channel such as voice, blackboard, chart, or worksheet. The receiver decodes it and gives feedback. Noise or barriers may disturb the process at any stage.

  • Sender: source of message.
  • Message: content to be communicated.
  • Encoding: converting idea into message form.
  • Channel: medium of communication.
  • Receiver: person receiving the message.
  • Decoding: interpreting the message.
  • Feedback: response of receiver.

4. What are the barriers to classroom communication? Suggest remedies.
  • Noise: arrange seating, use clear voice, and reduce disturbance.
  • Difficult language: use simple, age-appropriate language and examples.
  • Unclear message: give stepwise instructions and write key points.
  • Fear and hesitation: create a respectful and democratic classroom.
  • Poor listening: listen actively and respond patiently.
  • Lack of feedback: ask questions, observe work, and use short checks.
5. How can a teacher make classroom communication effective?
  • Plan the message before teaching.
  • Use simple, correct, and age-appropriate language.
  • Connect new content with previous knowledge and local examples.
  • Use voice modulation, pause, eye contact, gestures, and blackboard work.
  • Use charts, pictures, real objects, activities, and examples.
  • Ask questions and invite children's responses.
  • Listen carefully to doubts and answers.
  • Give immediate and respectful feedback.
  • Adapt language for learners with different needs and backgrounds.
