Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define maxims of teaching.

Maxims of teaching are short practical rules that guide the teacher in arranging content and classroom explanation in a psychological and learner-friendly order.

2. Write four maxims of teaching.

Known to unknown, simple to complex, concrete to abstract, easy to difficult, particular to general, general to particular, whole to part, and part to whole are common maxims.

3. Why is concrete to abstract important?

Primary children understand real objects and activities before symbols and abstract ideas. Concrete examples make concepts clear and reduce rote learning.

4. What is the difference between particular to general and general to particular?

Particular to general moves from examples to rule. General to particular starts with a rule and applies it to examples.

5. Give one classroom use of known to unknown.

Before teaching the water cycle, the teacher may begin with children's known experiences of rain, wells, handpumps, ponds, and drying clothes.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and importance of maxims of teaching.

Maxims of teaching are practical rules that help organize teaching in a natural sequence. They are important because they use previous knowledge, reduce difficulty, build confidence, support child psychology, and make teaching systematic. They help the teacher decide where to begin, how to proceed, and how to connect new knowledge with experience.

2. Explain known to unknown, simple to complex, and concrete to abstract.
  • Known to unknown: begin with familiar experience and move to new content.
  • Simple to complex: teach easy ideas before difficult combinations.
  • Concrete to abstract: use real objects, pictures, and activities before symbols, definitions, and rules.
3. Compare whole to part and part to whole.

Whole to part presents the complete object, story, poem, map, or model first and then studies details. It is useful when overall meaning is important. Part to whole teaches small units first and combines them later, such as letters to words or number facts to problem solving.

4. How can maxims be used in a primary lesson?

In primary classes, the teacher should start with children's known experiences, use concrete objects, arrange content from simple to difficult, give examples before definitions, and use activities before abstract symbols. For example, while teaching triangle, the teacher shows triangular objects, lets children count sides and corners, then gives the definition.

5. Are maxims rigid rules? Explain.

Maxims are not rigid laws. They are flexible guidelines. A teacher should choose them according to topic, age, class level, available materials, and learner needs. Often several maxims work together in one lesson, such as known to unknown, concrete to abstract, and particular to general.
