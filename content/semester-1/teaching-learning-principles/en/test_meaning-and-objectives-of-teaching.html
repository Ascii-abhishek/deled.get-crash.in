Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define teaching.

Teaching is a purposeful and planned process in which the teacher organizes learning experiences, gives guidance, uses suitable methods and materials, and helps learners acquire knowledge, skills, attitudes, and desirable behavior.

2. Why is teaching called a tripolar process?

Teaching is called a tripolar process because it has three main poles: teacher, learner, and curriculum or content. The teacher connects the learner with the content through suitable methods, activities, guidance, and evaluation.

3. Write two differences between wider and narrower meaning of teaching.

In the wider meaning, teaching is a lifelong process influenced by family, society, environment, school, media, and experiences. In the narrower meaning, teaching is formal, planned, and organized classroom instruction for a fixed purpose and duration.

4. Mention four characteristics of good teaching.

Good teaching is purposeful, child-centered, active, democratic, motivating, practical, remedial, and evaluative. It respects individual differences and connects learning with life.

5. Name the three domains of teaching objectives with one example each.
  • Cognitive: Students explain causes of seasons.
  • Affective: Students show respect for classmates.
  • Psychomotor: Students measure length using a ruler.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the meaning and nature of teaching.

Teaching is a purposeful process that helps learners learn meaningfully. It is not limited to giving information. A teacher plans experiences, explains concepts, asks questions, motivates children, uses teaching-learning materials, gives practice, checks progress, and provides feedback.

  • It is an interactive process between teacher and learner.
  • It connects the learner with curriculum and life situations.
  • It aims at desirable change in knowledge, skills, attitudes, habits, and behavior.
  • It is child-centered and considers age, ability, interest, and individual differences.
  • It becomes successful only when learning actually takes place.

3. Describe the main objectives of teaching.

The main objective of teaching is to make learning useful, meaningful, and development-oriented. Important objectives are:

  • Knowledge and understanding: To develop correct information, concepts, reasoning, and application.
  • Skill development: To develop reading, writing, speaking, calculating, observing, experimenting, and problem-solving skills.
  • Interest and motivation: To create curiosity and willingness to learn.
  • Personality development: To develop confidence, expression, creativity, discipline, and responsibility.
  • Values and attitudes: To develop cooperation, democratic behavior, respect, health habits, and moral-social values.
  • Adjustment and citizenship: To help children adjust with society and become responsible citizens.
4. Explain the characteristics of good teaching with classroom examples.
  • Purposeful: The teacher starts with clear objectives, such as "students will identify even numbers."
  • Child-centered: The teacher uses examples suitable to children's age and local life.
  • Active: Children solve, draw, discuss, read, ask questions, and work in pairs or groups.
  • Democratic: Every child is respected and gets a chance to participate.
  • Motivating: The teacher begins with a story, problem, object, or picture to create interest.
  • Remedial: The teacher identifies errors and gives extra practice or examples.
  • Evaluative: The teacher checks learning through questions, observation, worksheets, and feedback.
5. Distinguish between teaching objectives and learning outcomes. Why are objectives important?

Teaching objectives are written from the teacher's planning side, such as "to teach nouns." Learning outcomes are written from the learner's side, such as "students identify nouns in five sentences."

Objectives are important because they:

  • make teaching planned and purposeful;
  • help the teacher choose suitable methods, activities, and materials;
  • guide evaluation and feedback;
  • help balance cognitive, affective, and psychomotor development;
  • make learning useful, measurable, and connected with learner development.
