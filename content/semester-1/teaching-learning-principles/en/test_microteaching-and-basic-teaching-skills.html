Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define microteaching.

Microteaching is a scaled-down teaching practice in which a trainee teaches small content to a small group for a short time while practicing one teaching skill and receiving feedback.

2. Write the microteaching cycle.

The cycle is: plan, teach, feedback, re-plan, re-teach, and re-feedback.

3. Name five basic teaching skills.

Set induction, questioning, explanation, stimulus variation, reinforcement, illustration with examples, blackboard writing, and closure are basic teaching skills.

4. What is stimulus variation?

Stimulus variation is the skill of changing voice, movement, gesture, pause, media, and learner activity to avoid monotony and maintain attention.

5. Write two advantages of microteaching.

Microteaching builds confidence and allows focused practice of one skill with immediate feedback and improvement.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain microteaching and its main features.

Microteaching is a teacher-training technique used to practice teaching skills under controlled conditions. It reduces class size, lesson length, content, and skill load. A trainee practices one skill for a short time, receives feedback, re-plans, and teaches again. Its features include focused practice, immediate feedback, peer observation, skill improvement, and safe preparation for real teaching.

2. Describe the microteaching cycle.
  • Plan: prepare a small lesson for one skill.
  • Teach: teach a small group for a short time.
  • Feedback: supervisor and peers identify strengths and improvements.
  • Re-plan: revise according to feedback.
  • Re-teach: teach again with improvement.
  • Re-feedback: discuss improvement and skill mastery.
3. Explain set induction, questioning, and explanation skills.

Set induction introduces the lesson, links previous knowledge, and creates curiosity. Questioning uses clear, graded, distributed, and thought-provoking questions. Explanation presents a concept in simple language, sequence, examples, comparison, and recap. These skills help learners enter, think about, and understand the lesson.

4. Discuss reinforcement, stimulus variation, and blackboard writing skills.

Reinforcement strengthens correct responses through praise, nodding, repetition, and encouraging cues. Stimulus variation maintains attention through voice change, movement, gestures, pause, media, and activity. Blackboard writing organizes key points, diagrams, spellings, headings, and summaries in readable form.

5. Write advantages and limitations of microteaching.

Advantages: builds confidence, gives focused skill practice, provides immediate feedback, reduces fear, and develops reflective teaching. Limitations: it is artificial, peer learners may not act like real children, it needs trained supervisors, and skills may become mechanical unless later integrated in real classroom teaching.
