Teaching Techniques

Teaching techniques are classroom procedures used by a teacher to explain, question, demonstrate, discuss, drill, guide practice, and evaluate learning. A technique is smaller than a method and is used inside a method to make teaching effective.

Exam Snapshot

TechniqueMeaningBest use
Question-answerTeaching through planned questions and learner responses.Checking previous knowledge and developing thinking.
ExplanationClear verbal presentation with examples and sequence.Clarifying a concept, rule, or process.
DemonstrationShowing how something is done.Science activity, drawing, handwriting, craft, math operation.
DiscussionExchange of ideas among teacher and learners.Social issues, story, EVS, value education.
Drill and practiceRepeated guided practice for mastery.Tables, spellings, reading fluency, pronunciation.

Meaning And Features

  • A teaching technique is a specific classroom action used to achieve a learning objective.
  • A method is broader, while a technique is a smaller tool inside the method.
  • Techniques should be selected according to objective, subject, age, learner level, time, and available material.
  • Good techniques increase attention, participation, understanding, practice, and feedback.

Common Teaching Techniques

TechniqueHow to usePrecaution
Question-answerAsk clear, purposeful, graded questions; give wait time; involve many learners.Do not ask only memory questions or shame wrong answers.
ExplanationUse simple language, examples, diagrams, steps, and summary.Avoid long lecture and difficult words.
DemonstrationShow material to all, perform steps slowly, explain while doing, let learners repeat.Do not demonstrate where children cannot see.
DiscussionGive a topic or problem, guide turns, record points, conclude clearly.Do not allow a few children to dominate.
StorytellingUse voice, expression, sequence, and moral or concept link.Keep story relevant and age-appropriate.
IllustrationUse examples, pictures, charts, maps, real objects, and analogies.Examples must be accurate and local when possible.
AssignmentGive purposeful classwork/homework with clear instructions and feedback.Do not give excessive mechanical copying.
DrillUse short repeated practice with correction and variety.Avoid meaningless repetition without understanding.
Role playLet learners act a situation, then discuss the learning point.Keep it respectful and linked to objective.
Blackboard workWrite neatly, organize headings, draw simple diagrams, highlight key terms.Avoid overcrowded or unreadable writing.

Choosing The Right Technique

  • For concepts: explanation, illustration, examples, concept map, questioning.
  • For skills: demonstration, imitation, guided practice, drill, feedback.
  • For values and attitudes: story, role play, discussion, reflection.
  • For problem solving: heuristic questions, group work, worksheet, peer explanation.
  • For revision: quiz, recap, drill, summary chart, one-minute writing.

Classroom Connection

In a Class 3 handwriting lesson, the teacher may demonstrate letter formation on the board, explain strokes, let children imitate in air, give guided notebook practice, observe errors, and give feedback. One lesson may therefore use demonstration, explanation, drill, observation, and feedback together.

One-minute Revision

  • Technique is a specific classroom procedure; method is broader.
  • Common techniques include questioning, explanation, demonstration, discussion, drill, storytelling, role play, assignment, illustration, and blackboard work.
  • Technique choice depends on objective, content, learner level, time, and material.
  • Good techniques make learners active and give feedback to the teacher.
  • No single technique is best for every lesson.

Common Exam Lines

  • Teaching techniques are practical classroom procedures used to make teaching effective.
  • Questioning is useful for checking previous knowledge, attention, understanding, and thinking.
  • Demonstration is most useful when a process or skill has to be shown.
  • Drill is useful for mastery, but it should not become meaningless repetition.
  • A good teacher combines techniques according to objective and learner needs.
