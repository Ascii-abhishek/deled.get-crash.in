Teaching Learning Materials

Teaching-learning materials, or TLMs, are objects, models, charts, cards, digital resources, local materials, and classroom tools that make learning concrete, active, and memorable. Good TLMs help children see, touch, discuss, compare, and practice concepts.

Importance Of TLM

  • Makes abstract ideas concrete, especially in mathematics, language, EVS, and science.
  • Increases attention, curiosity, participation, and retention.
  • Supports inclusive classrooms because different children learn through different senses.
  • Connects school knowledge with local life and community resources.
  • Reduces rote learning by encouraging observation, manipulation, and discussion.

Types Of TLM

  • Real objects: seeds, soil, leaves, utensils, coins, measuring tools.
  • Visual aids: charts, flash cards, maps, timelines, diagrams, picture cards.
  • Models and working aids: clock, abacus, place-value chart, water filter model.
  • Print material: worksheets, story cards, word strips, newspapers, textbooks.
  • Digital and audio aids: short video, recorded story, projector, radio, offline images.

Principles Of Selection And Use

  • Match the TLM with the objective and class level.
  • Use simple, clear, safe, durable, and culturally familiar material.
  • Let children handle the material instead of only watching the teacher.
  • Prepare questions and activities before showing the material.
  • Store and label materials so they can be reused by other teachers.

Concepts And Differences

Digital TLMScreen, audio, or interactive media used with clear learning purpose.

Classroom Connection

For Class 4 fractions, the teacher gives paper circles and asks children to fold them into halves, quarters, and thirds. Children compare pieces, paste them in notebooks, and write fraction names. The TLM becomes useful because it is handled by learners and linked with questions.

One-minute Revision

  • TLM makes learning concrete and active.
  • Best TLM is objective-based, child-safe, clear, low-cost, and reusable.
  • Children should manipulate materials, not only watch them.
  • Local materials make learning familiar and affordable.
  • Store, label, repair, and evaluate TLM after use.

Common Exam Lines

  • Teaching-learning material is any planned resource that supports achievement of learning objectives.
  • Low-cost TLM encourages activity-based learning even in resource-poor schools.
  • A TLM is effective only when it is linked with questioning, learner activity, and assessment.
