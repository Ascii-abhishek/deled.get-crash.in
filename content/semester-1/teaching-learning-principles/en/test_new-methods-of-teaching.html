Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. What are new methods of teaching?

New methods are child-centered, activity-based, participatory, inquiry-oriented, and life-connected ways of teaching that reduce rote learning and promote understanding.

2. Name five new methods of teaching.

Activity-based learning, project method, play-way method, problem-solving method, heuristic method, cooperative learning, brainstorming, and ICT-supported learning are common new methods.

3. Why is play-way method useful in primary classes?

Play-way method is useful because young children learn naturally through games, songs, rhythm, movement, imitation, and joyful activities.

4. Write two features of project method.

Project method is based on real-life problems and learner activity. It includes planning, information collection, group work, presentation, and evaluation.

5. What is the teacher's role in new methods?

The teacher acts as facilitator, guide, planner, observer, questioner, evaluator, and inclusive organizer.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the need and importance of new methods of teaching.

New methods are needed because lecture and rote learning alone do not develop understanding, creativity, cooperation, and problem solving. They make learners active, connect learning with life, support individual differences, encourage inquiry, and provide continuous feedback. They are especially useful at the primary level where children learn through concrete experience and activity.

2. Describe activity-based learning and project method.

Activity-based learning teaches through meaningful tasks such as sorting, measuring, drawing, experimenting, or role work. Project method asks learners to investigate a real problem, plan work, collect information, create a product or report, present it, and evaluate learning. Both make learning practical and participatory.

3. Explain play-way, problem-solving, and heuristic methods.
  • Play-way: uses games, stories, songs, movement, and joyful tasks.
  • Problem-solving: begins with a problem, suggests and tests solutions, then concludes.
  • Heuristic: learners discover through observation, questions, inquiry, and guided exploration.
4. Compare traditional and new methods of teaching.

Traditional methods often emphasize teacher talk, textbook completion, same task for all, and memory-based answers. New methods emphasize learner activity, understanding, flexible grouping, creativity, inquiry, problem solving, cooperation, and continuous feedback. The teacher becomes a guide rather than only a speaker.

5. How can cooperative learning and ICT support elementary teaching?

Cooperative learning develops academic understanding and social skills through small groups, peer help, shared roles, and group presentation. ICT can support learning through pictures, audio, video, digital quizzes, and simulations. Both should be planned according to objectives and should include participation, feedback, and teacher guidance.
