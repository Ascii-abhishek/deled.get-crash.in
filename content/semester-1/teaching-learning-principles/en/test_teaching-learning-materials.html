1. Define teaching-learning material.

Teaching-learning material is any planned resource that supports achievement of learning objectives. TLM makes learning concrete and active.

2. Write four qualities of good TLM.

Low-cost TLM encourages activity-based learning even in resource-poor schools. Best TLM is objective-based, child-safe, clear, low-cost, and reusable.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (4 Questions)

1. Explain the importance of TLM in primary teaching.

Teaching Learning Materials: Teaching-learning materials, or TLMs, are objects, models, charts, cards, digital resources, local materials, and classroom tools that make learning concrete, active, and memorable. Good TLMs help children see, touch, discuss, compare, and practice concepts.

  • Makes abstract ideas concrete, especially in mathematics, language, EVS, and science.
  • Increases attention, curiosity, participation, and retention.
  • Supports inclusive classrooms because different children learn through different senses.
  • Connects school knowledge with local life and community resources.

For Class 4 fractions, the teacher gives paper circles and asks children to fold them into halves, quarters, and thirds. Children compare pieces, paste them in notebooks, and write fraction names. The TLM becomes useful because it is handled by learners and linked with questions.

2. Classify TLM with examples from language, mathematics, EVS, and science.

Teaching Learning Materials: Teaching-learning materials, or TLMs, are objects, models, charts, cards, digital resources, local materials, and classroom tools that make learning concrete, active, and memorable. Good TLMs help children see, touch, discuss, compare, and practice concepts.

  • Real objects: seeds, soil, leaves, utensils, coins, measuring tools.
  • Visual aids: charts, flash cards, maps, timelines, diagrams, picture cards.
  • Models and working aids: clock, abacus, place-value chart, water filter model.
  • Print material: worksheets, story cards, word strips, newspapers, textbooks.

3. Describe principles for selecting, preparing, using, and storing TLM.

Teaching Learning Materials: Teaching-learning materials, or TLMs, are objects, models, charts, cards, digital resources, local materials, and classroom tools that make learning concrete, active, and memorable. Good TLMs help children see, touch, discuss, compare, and practice concepts.

  • Match the TLM with the objective and class level.
  • Use simple, clear, safe, durable, and culturally familiar material.
  • Let children handle the material instead of only watching the teacher.
  • Prepare questions and activities before showing the material.
