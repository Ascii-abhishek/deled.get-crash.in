Guidance and Counselling

Guidance helps learners make suitable educational, personal, and social choices; counselling is a deeper face-to-face helping process for adjustment and problem solving. Primary teachers use basic supportive counselling and refer serious cases.

Core Points

  • Guidance is for all learners; counselling is usually for learners with a specific concern.
  • A teacher should listen more than lecture, avoid blaming, and protect child dignity.
  • Common primary issues include fear, absenteeism, peer conflict, slow learning, bullying, low confidence, and family stress.
  • Counselling steps: rapport, problem understanding, goal setting, alternatives, action plan, follow-up.
  • Teachers must not promise secrecy if the child is unsafe; safety concerns require referral.
  • Parent meetings should be supportive, not accusatory.

Quick Memory Hook

Guidance shows direction; counselling helps the child walk with confidence.

Classroom Connection

A child suddenly stops participating. The teacher speaks privately, listens without scolding, checks peer relations and home factors, gives small achievable roles, informs parents sensitively, and follows up weekly.

One-minute Revision

  • Counselling: Personal helping relationship for understanding and solving problems.
