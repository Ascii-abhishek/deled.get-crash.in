Teaching Learning Material and Methods

Inclusive classrooms need flexible methods and accessible teaching-learning materials. The teacher adapts content, process, product, and environment so different learners can participate meaningfully.

Core Points

  • Choose TLM according to objective, age, safety, cost, and accessibility.
  • Use local materials: seeds, leaves, sticks, bottle caps, cloth pieces, newspapers, and picture cards.
  • Methods should include demonstration, activity, story, role play, project, group work, and individual practice.
  • For CWSN, adapt size, contrast, texture, sound, time, and response mode.
  • Group work needs clear roles so one child does not dominate and another does not disappear.
  • TLM is useful only when children handle, discuss, classify, compare, and apply it.

Quick Memory Hook

Same concept, many doors: see, hear, touch, move, say, draw.

Classroom Connection

To teach place value, use bundled sticks, number cards, and a place-value mat. A child with fine-motor difficulty can point or arrange large cards instead of tying bundles.

One-minute Revision

  • TLM: Material used to make learning concrete and active.
  • Multisensory teaching: Uses sight, sound, touch, movement, and speech.
  • Universal Design for Learning: Planning multiple ways to engage, present, and express learning.
  • Peer tutoring: Learners support one another under teacher guidance.
  • Cooperative learning: Small groups work with shared responsibility.
  • Differentiation: Adapting task level, pace, material, or response mode.
