Children With Special Needs

Children with special needs are learners who need additional support because of disability, difficulty, giftedness, health condition, language barrier, or social disadvantage. The focus is not charity but right, participation, dignity, and learning.

Core Points

  • Use person-first respectful language and avoid labels that lower dignity.
  • Needs may be physical, visual, hearing, intellectual, learning, emotional, behavioural, or socio-cultural.
  • Early identification reduces secondary frustration and dropout.
  • Collaboration with parents, special educators, health workers, and peers is essential.
  • Inclusive classrooms use flexible grouping, multisensory teaching, accessible materials, and supportive assessment.
  • The aim is maximum participation in common classroom life.

Quick Memory Hook

See the barrier, not the child, as the problem.

Classroom Connection

Seat a low-vision learner near the board, provide large-print worksheets, read instructions aloud, pair with a supportive buddy, and evaluate orally when writing speed blocks expression.

One-minute Revision

  • CWSN: Learners needing additional educational support.
  • Disability: Long-term physical, sensory, intellectual, or mental condition causing barriers.
  • Impairment: Problem in body function or structure.
  • Inclusive education: All children learn together with support.
  • Individualised support: Support planned according to learner need.
  • Screening: Early process of noticing possible difficulties.

Common Exam Lines

  • Children With Special Needs should be explained with definition, example, classroom use, and learner-friendly activity.

  • Assessment should check understanding, application, and expression, not recall alone.
