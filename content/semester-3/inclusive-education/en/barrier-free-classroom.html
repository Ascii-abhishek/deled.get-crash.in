Barrier-free Classroom

A barrier-free classroom helps every child participate. Barriers may be physical, social, linguistic, emotional, or related to teaching methods.

Practical Adjustments

  • Seat children where they can see and hear clearly.
  • Use pictures, actions, examples, and local language.
  • Give extra time where needed.
  • Encourage peer support without labelling children.

Teacher Reflection

Ask: Which child is silent or left out during activities? What small change can help that child join?
