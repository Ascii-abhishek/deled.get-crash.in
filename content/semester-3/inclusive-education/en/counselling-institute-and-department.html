Counselling Institute and Department

Counselling support in education works through school staff, resource centres, DIETs, special educators, health departments, child protection systems, and community agencies. A teacher should know when and where to refer.

Core Points

  • The class teacher is often the first observer of emotional, learning, attendance, or safety concerns.
  • School-level support should begin with listening, observation, parent contact, and small classroom adjustments.
  • Refer when the problem is beyond teacher competence: abuse, severe distress, disability assessment, medical issue, addiction, or repeated self-harm talk.
  • Maintain a referral diary with date, concern, action taken, agency contacted, and follow-up.
  • Never publicly disclose a child’s sensitive case.
  • Coordination among teacher, head teacher, parents, health worker, and specialist improves outcomes.

Quick Memory Hook

Observe early, support locally, refer responsibly, follow up quietly.

Classroom Connection

If a child has repeated hearing difficulty, the teacher records observations, informs the head teacher, speaks with parents, refers to the nearest health service for screening, and adapts seating and instructions meanwhile.
