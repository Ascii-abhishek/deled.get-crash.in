Sentence Construction Based on Dialogue Lessons

This chapter explains how dialogue lessons help learners construct simple Sanskrit sentences for real communication.

Core Points

  • Start from sound, meaning, and use before giving heavy grammar terminology.
  • Use tables, cards, chanting, substitution drills, and short dialogues for repeated practice.
  • Connect Sanskrit forms with Hindi meaning carefully, but avoid word-by-word confusion.
  • Give examples first, then guide learners to state the rule.
  • Include oral, written, pair, and board-work activities in every lesson.
  • Evaluate through pronunciation, form recognition, sentence use, and short explanation.

Classroom Connection

Let pairs perform a two-line Sanskrit dialogue and then replace names, objects, and verbs to make new sentences.
