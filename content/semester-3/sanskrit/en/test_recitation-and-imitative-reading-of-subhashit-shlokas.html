Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Recitation and Imitative Reading of Subhashit Shlokas.

This chapter develops correct, rhythmic, meaningful recitation and imitative reading of Sanskrit subhashit shlokas.

2. Define Subhashit and Shloka.

Subhashit: A good saying or verse carrying moral or practical wisdom. Shloka: A Sanskrit verse usually arranged in metrical lines.

3. Write two classroom uses of Recitation and Imitative Reading of Subhashit Shlokas.

Teacher recites one line, children repeat with pause, then discuss one value from the shloka. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.
