Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Sanskrit Letters and Pronunciation.

This chapter revises Sanskrit vowels, consonants, sound production, pronunciation places, and classroom pronunciation practice.

2. Define Vowel and Consonant.

Vowel: A sound pronounced without obstruction of air. Consonant: A sound pronounced with obstruction or contact in the mouth.

3. Write two classroom uses of Sanskrit Letters and Pronunciation.

Use mirror practice, clap rhythm, and mouth-position demonstration for vowels and consonants. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

3. Explain the key terms and concepts with examples.
  • Vowel: A sound pronounced without obstruction of air.
  • Consonant: A sound pronounced with obstruction or contact in the mouth.
  • Place of articulation: The mouth position where sound is produced.
  • Effort: The manner of producing a sound such as touch or friction.
  • Short vowel: A vowel with brief duration.
  • Long vowel: A vowel with longer duration.
