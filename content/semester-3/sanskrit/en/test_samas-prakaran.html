Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Samas Prakaran.

This chapter explains Sanskrit compounds, their vigraha, types, and classroom practice.

2. Define Samas and Vigraha.

Samas: A compact compound made by joining related words. Vigraha: Expansion that explains the meaning of a compound.

3. Write two classroom uses of Samas Prakaran.

Use two-word cards and ask learners to form compounds and explain them in simple Hindi. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

3. Explain the key terms and concepts with examples.
  • Samas: A compact compound made by joining related words.
  • Vigraha: Expansion that explains the meaning of a compound.
  • Samasta pada: The compound word formed after samas.
  • Tatpurusha: Compound where one word depends on another.
  • Karmadharaya: Compound of noun and adjective or similar relation.
  • Dvandva: Compound with equal importance of both words.
