Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Suggested Teaching Methods.

This chapter revises practical Sanskrit teaching methods, activities, teaching aids, practice, and evaluation.

2. Define Teaching method and Direct method.

Teaching method: A planned way of helping learners understand and practise. Direct method: Teaching through target language, action, and objects.

3. Write two classroom uses of Suggested Teaching Methods.

Plan a 35-minute lesson with recitation, word cards, pair dialogue, board table, and quick oral check. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

3. Explain the key terms and concepts with examples.
  • Teaching method: A planned way of helping learners understand and practise.
  • Direct method: Teaching through target language, action, and objects.
  • Translation method: Teaching by comparing Sanskrit and mother tongue meaning.
  • Recitation method: Teaching through model reading, rhythm, and repetition.
  • Activity method: Learning through games, cards, role play, and tasks.
  • Teaching aid: Material that supports understanding.
