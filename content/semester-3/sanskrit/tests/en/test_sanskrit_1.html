Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Write a short note on Sanskrit Letters and Pronunciation.
  • Introduction: This chapter revises Sanskrit vowels, consonants, sound production, pronunciation places, and classroom pronunciation practice.
  • Key terms: Vowel, Consonant, Place of articulation, Effort.
  • Classroom use: Use mirror practice, clap rhythm, and mouth-position demonstration for vowels and consonants.
  • Conclusion: The chapter connects knowledge, language use, and teaching skill.
2. Write a short note on Sandhi Prakaran.
  • Introduction: This chapter revises Sanskrit sandhi as the joining of sounds with rules, types, and examples.
  • Key terms: Sandhi, Sandhi-vicheda, Swar sandhi, Vyanjan sandhi.
  • Classroom use: Give word cards and let children join vowels or split simple joined forms aloud.
  • Conclusion: The chapter connects knowledge, language use, and teaching skill.
3. Write a short note on Samas Prakaran.
  • Introduction: This chapter explains Sanskrit compounds, their vigraha, types, and classroom practice.
  • Key terms: Samas, Vigraha, Samasta pada, Tatpurusha.
  • Classroom use: Use two-word cards and ask learners to form compounds and explain them in simple Hindi.
  • Conclusion: The chapter connects knowledge, language use, and teaching skill.
4. Write a short note on Sanskrit Word Forms, Word Types, Number and Cases.
  • Introduction: This chapter revises Sanskrit nouns, pronouns, gender, number, cases, and common shabda-rupa patterns.
  • Key terms: Noun, Pronoun, Gender, Singular.
  • Classroom use: Use a रूप तालिका for राम, फल, लता and ask learners to identify number and case.
  • Conclusion: The chapter connects knowledge, language use, and teaching skill.
5. Write a short note on Sanskrit Verb Forms.
  • Introduction: This chapter revises Sanskrit roots, lakara, person, number, and common verb-form patterns.
  • Key terms: Dhatu, Lakar, Lat lakar, Lang lakar.
  • Classroom use: Chant पठ् धातु forms in ताल and then use them in three simple sentences.
  • Conclusion: The chapter connects knowledge, language use, and teaching skill.
