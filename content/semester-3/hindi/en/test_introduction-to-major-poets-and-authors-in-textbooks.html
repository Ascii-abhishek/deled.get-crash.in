Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Introduction to Major Poets and Authors in Textbooks.

This chapter helps trainee teachers introduce textbook writers with their life context, style, themes, and child-friendly classroom use.

2. Define Poet and Author.

Poet: A creator who expresses feelings, imagination, and ideas in verse. Author: A creator who writes prose, stories, essays, plays, or other literary forms.

3. Write two classroom uses of Introduction to Major Poets and Authors in Textbooks.

Ask groups to prepare a five-line author card: name, period, one work, main theme, and one question for children. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

  • Poet: A creator who expresses feelings, imagination, and ideas in verse.

  • Language style: The special way of using words, sentences, images, and tone.
  • Theme: The central idea or feeling of a poem or prose piece.
