Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Introduction to Verb and Sentence Forms.

This chapter revises verbs, sentence formation, tatsam-tadbhav-deshaj words, and basic sentence transformation for Hindi teaching.

2. Define Verb and Transitive verb.

Verb: A word that shows action, state, or occurrence. Transitive verb: A verb that needs an object to complete meaning.

3. Write two classroom uses of Introduction to Verb and Sentence Forms.

Let children act a verb, say it in present, past, and future, then make simple, negative, and interrogative sentences. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

3. Explain the key terms and concepts with examples.
  • Verb: A word that shows action, state, or occurrence.
  • Transitive verb: A verb that needs an object to complete meaning.
  • Intransitive verb: A verb that does not need an object.
  • Tense: Time of action: present, past, or future.
  • Sentence: A meaningful group of words with complete sense.
  • Subject: The doer or topic of a sentence.
