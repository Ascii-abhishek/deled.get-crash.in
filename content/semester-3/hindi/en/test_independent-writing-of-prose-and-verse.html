Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Independent Writing of Prose and Verse.

This chapter develops independent expression through paragraphs, stories, poems, descriptions, and festival writing.

2. Define Independent writing and Prose.

Independent writing: Writing one’s own ideas without copying a model. Prose: Ordinary sentence-based writing such as paragraph, story, or essay.

3. Write two classroom uses of Independent Writing of Prose and Verse.

Use a picture or school event and let children write first freely, then revise for sequence, vocabulary, and punctuation. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

  • Prose: Ordinary sentence-based writing such as paragraph, story, or essay.
  • Verse: Rhythmic or poetic expression using lines, images, and feelings.
  • Paragraph: A group of connected sentences on one idea.
  • Theme: The topic or central idea of writing.
  • Draft: The first written version before correction.
