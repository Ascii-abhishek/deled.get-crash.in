Core Points

  • Textbooks give planned lessons, but extra material gives real language situations.
  • Choose age-appropriate, clear, inclusive, and culturally respectful material.
  • Use pre-reading, during-reading, and post-reading activities.
  • Encourage silent reading, paired reading, read aloud, and story sharing.
  • Connect reading with writing: summary, poster, diary, letter, and question making.
  • Assess reading through fluency, comprehension, vocabulary, and response.

One-minute Revision

  • Supplementary reading: Reading material used beyond the textbook for wider language exposure.
  • Extensive reading: Reading many simple texts for pleasure and fluency.
  • Intensive reading: Close reading for details, vocabulary, and comprehension.
  • Reading fluency: Accurate, smooth, and meaningful reading.
  • Comprehension: Understanding meaning, sequence, and message of a text.
  • Library corner: A classroom space where children access varied reading material.
