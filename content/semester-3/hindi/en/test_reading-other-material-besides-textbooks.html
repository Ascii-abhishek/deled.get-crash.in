Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Reading Other Material Besides Textbooks.

This chapter explains how newspapers, stories, notices, letters, charts, poems, and digital texts enrich Hindi reading beyond the textbook.

2. Define Supplementary reading and Extensive reading.

Supplementary reading: Reading material used beyond the textbook for wider language exposure. Extensive reading: Reading many simple texts for pleasure and fluency.

3. Write two classroom uses of Reading Other Material Besides Textbooks.

Create a class reading corner with labels: story, poem, notice, news, letter, chart, and child-made booklet. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

3. Explain the key terms and concepts with examples.
  • Supplementary reading: Reading material used beyond the textbook for wider language exposure.
  • Extensive reading: Reading many simple texts for pleasure and fluency.
  • Intensive reading: Close reading for details, vocabulary, and comprehension.
  • Reading fluency: Accurate, smooth, and meaningful reading.
  • Comprehension: Understanding meaning, sequence, and message of a text.
  • Library corner: A classroom space where children access varied reading material.
