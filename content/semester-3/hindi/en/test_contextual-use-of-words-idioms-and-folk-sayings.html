Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Contextual Use of Words, Idioms and Folk Sayings.

This chapter explains how words, idioms, and folk sayings gain meaning from context and how children can use them naturally.

2. Define Context and Word meaning.

Context: The situation that gives a word or expression its exact meaning. Word meaning: The basic sense of a word used in a sentence.

3. Write two classroom uses of Contextual Use of Words, Idioms and Folk Sayings.

Give children a short story and ask them to replace plain sentences with suitable idioms or folk sayings. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

3. Explain the key terms and concepts with examples.
  • Context: The situation that gives a word or expression its exact meaning.
  • Word meaning: The basic sense of a word used in a sentence.
  • Idiomatic meaning: A special meaning different from the literal meaning of words.
  • Idiom: A fixed expression used for force, beauty, or sharpness in language.
  • Folk saying: A popular saying that expresses common experience or practical wisdom.
  • Synonym: A word with similar meaning.
