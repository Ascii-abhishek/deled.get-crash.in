Core Points

  • Do not make author introduction a list of dates only; connect it with the lesson children will read.
  • For primary classes, highlight simple facts, major work, main feeling, and one inspiring line.
  • Use pictures, timelines, author cards, role play, and recitation for active learning.
  • Discuss writers from different regions, genders, genres, and social concerns to widen children’s view.
  • Explain difficult words and cultural references before reading the text.
  • Evaluation can include oral introduction, matching author-work, and short appreciation notes.
