Contextual Use of Words, Idioms and Folk Sayings

This chapter explains how words, idioms, and folk sayings gain meaning from context and how children can use them naturally.

Core Points

  • Teach idioms through situations, not isolated memorisation.
  • A folk saying usually carries a complete life lesson, while an idiom often works inside a sentence.
  • Children understand expressions faster through stories, role play, pictures, and local speech.
  • Correct use requires meaning, grammar, tone, and social appropriateness.
  • Collect local sayings respectfully and compare standard Hindi forms where needed.
  • Assessment should test use in new sentences, not only definitions.
