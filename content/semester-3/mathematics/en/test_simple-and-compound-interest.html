Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define Principal with an example.

Original money borrowed or invested.

2. Write the difference between Interest and Rate.

Simple interest: Interest on original principal only. Compound interest: Interest on amount after each period.

3. Why is Time important in this chapter?

Time organises the core idea and helps solve or explain questions correctly.

4. Write two common mistakes learners make in this chapter.

Two common mistakes are memorising definitions without examples and confusing similar terms.

5. Suggest one primary-classroom activity for this chapter.

Run a class savings-bank activity: groups calculate simple interest first, then compound interest for the same data and discuss why amounts differ.
