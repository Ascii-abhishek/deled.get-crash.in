Factorisation and Division of Algebraic Expressions

This chapter revises algebraic expressions, common factors, identities, factorisation methods, and division of polynomials by monomials or simple expressions.

Core Points

  • First look for a common numerical and variable factor in all terms.
  • Use identities such as a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b) and (a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2.
  • Group terms when pairs have common factors.
  • For division by a monomial, divide each term separately.
  • Check factorisation by multiplying factors back.
  • Use area models to show why factorisation is reverse multiplication.
