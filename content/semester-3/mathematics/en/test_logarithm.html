Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define Logarithm with an example.

Power to which a base must be raised to get a number.

2. Write the difference between Base and Common logarithm.

Exponent: Gives value from base and power. Logarithm: Gives power from base and value.

3. Why is Natural logarithm important in this chapter?

Natural logarithm organises the core idea and helps solve or explain questions correctly.

4. Write two common mistakes learners make in this chapter.

Two common mistakes are memorising definitions without examples and confusing similar terms.

5. Suggest one primary-classroom activity for this chapter.

Use a table of powers of 10 and ask learners to read it both ways: exponential statement and logarithmic statement.
