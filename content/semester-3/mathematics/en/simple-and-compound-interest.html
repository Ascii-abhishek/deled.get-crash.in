Simple and Compound Interest

This chapter covers principal, rate, time, simple interest, compound interest, amount, and comparison of interest methods used in money problems.

Core Points

  • Simple interest formula: SI = (P x R x T) / 100.
  • Amount in simple interest: A = P + SI.
  • Compound amount for annual compounding: A = P(1 + R/100)^T.
  • For half-yearly compounding, halve the rate and double the number of periods.
  • Read word problems carefully: months must be converted into years if rate is per annum.
  • Connect with savings accounts, loans, classroom bank, and fair borrowing discussions.

Classroom Connection

Run a class savings-bank activity: groups calculate simple interest first, then compound interest for the same data and discuss why amounts differ.

One-minute Revision

  • Principal: Original money borrowed or invested.
  • Interest: Extra money paid for use of principal.
  • Rate: Interest for one year on every hundred rupees.
  • Time: Period for which money is borrowed or invested.
  • Amount: Principal plus interest.
  • Simple interest: Interest calculated only on original principal.
