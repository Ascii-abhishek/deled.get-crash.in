Core Points

  • Banking mathematics uses percentage, interest, balance, deposits, withdrawals, and service charges.
  • Savings accounts encourage financial discipline; current accounts suit frequent business transactions.
  • A passbook or statement must be read date-wise: opening balance, deposit, withdrawal, closing balance.
  • Cheques require date, payee, amount in words and figures, and signature.
  • Digital banking needs privacy: never share PIN, OTP, or password.
  • Primary teaching can link banking with classroom shop, savings chart, and household budgeting.

One-minute Revision

  • Bank: Institution that accepts deposits, lends money, and supports payments.
  • Savings account: Account for regular saving with limited interest.
  • Current account: Account mainly for business transactions.
  • Fixed deposit: Money deposited for a fixed period at a fixed rate.
  • Recurring deposit: Regular monthly deposit for a chosen period.
  • Cheque: Written order to bank to pay a stated amount.
