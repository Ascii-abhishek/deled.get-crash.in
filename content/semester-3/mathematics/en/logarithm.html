Logarithm

This chapter explains logarithm as the inverse of exponentiation, its laws, common logarithms, and use in simplifying multiplication, division, powers, and roots.

Core Points

  • Logarithm answers: what power gives this number?
  • If 10^3 = 1000, then log10 1000 = 3.
  • Product turns into addition; quotient turns into subtraction.
  • Power comes in front as multiplier, so roots can also be simplified.
  • Common logarithms were historically used for long numerical calculations.
  • Teach through exponent tables first; then show the inverse relation with logarithms.
