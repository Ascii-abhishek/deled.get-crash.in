Shares and Dividend

This chapter introduces shares, face value, market value, premium, discount, dividend, yield, and simple investment calculations.

Core Points

  • Dividend is calculated on face value, not market value.
  • Investment is calculated on market value plus possible brokerage.
  • If market value is above face value, share is at premium; if below, at discount.
  • Yield = annual income / investment x 100.
  • Read numerical problems carefully: dividend rate and market price refer to different bases.
  • Use simple classroom examples before discussing real stock-market risk.

Classroom Connection

Create paper shares with face value and market value. Learners compute dividend and compare yield for different purchase prices.

One-minute Revision

  • Share: One unit of ownership capital of a company.
  • Face value: Value printed on the share.
  • Market value: Current buying or selling price of the share.
  • Premium: Market value greater than face value.
  • Discount: Market value less than face value.
  • Dividend: Profit distributed by company to shareholders.
