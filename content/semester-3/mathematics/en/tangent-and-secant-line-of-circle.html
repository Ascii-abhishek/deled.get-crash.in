Tangent and Secant Line of Circle

This chapter explains tangent, secant, point of contact, tangent-radius relation, construction ideas, and common circle-line properties.

Core Points

  • A tangent has exactly one common point with the circle.
  • A secant has two common points with the circle.
  • The radius at the point of contact is perpendicular to the tangent.
  • From one external point, the lengths of two tangents to the circle are equal.
  • A line outside the circle has no common point.
  • Use compass drawing and a cardboard circle to let learners test line positions.

Classroom Connection

Place a ruler around a drawn circle: outside, touching, and cutting. Learners record the number of common points in each case.

One-minute Revision

  • Tangent: Line touching a circle at exactly one point.
  • Point of contact: Point where tangent touches the circle.
  • Secant: Line cutting a circle at two points.
  • External point: Point outside the circle from which tangent may be drawn.
  • Radius to tangent: Radius drawn to point of contact is perpendicular to tangent.
  • Chord: Segment joining two points on circle.
