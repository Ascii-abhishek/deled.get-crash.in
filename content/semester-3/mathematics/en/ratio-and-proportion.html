Ratio and Proportion

This chapter builds quick command over comparing quantities, writing equivalent ratios, solving proportions, and applying the unitary method in daily-life and classroom situations.

Core Points

  • Compare only like quantities; convert units before forming a ratio.
  • Simplify a ratio by dividing both terms by their HCF.
  • In a:b = c:d, product of extremes equals product of means, so a x d = b x c.
  • Use direct proportion for cost, distance at fixed speed, and work done by equal workers.
  • Use inverse proportion for time and number of workers when total work is fixed.
  • In teaching, begin with concrete comparisons: boys:girls, red beads:blue beads, rupees:paise after conversion.

Classroom Connection

Give groups of bottle caps in two colours. Learners form ratios, simplify them, then predict missing caps using proportion before writing symbolic solutions.

One-minute Revision

  • Ratio: Comparison of two quantities of the same kind by division, written a:b or a/b.
  • Antecedent: The first term of a ratio.
  • Consequent: The second term of a ratio.
  • Proportion: Equality of two ratios, such as a:b = c:d.
  • Extremes: First and fourth terms in a proportion.
  • Means: Second and third terms in a proportion.
