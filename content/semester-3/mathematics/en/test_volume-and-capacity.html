Section 2: Short Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Define Volume with an example.

Space occupied by a solid object.

2. Write the difference between Capacity and Cubic unit.

Volume: Space occupied. Capacity: How much a container can hold.

3. Why is Litre important in this chapter?

Litre organises the core idea and helps solve or explain questions correctly.

4. Write two common mistakes learners make in this chapter.

Two common mistakes are memorising definitions without examples and confusing similar terms.

5. Suggest one primary-classroom activity for this chapter.

Fill cuboid boxes with centimetre cubes, then pour water into measuring jars to connect cubic units with litres and millilitres.
