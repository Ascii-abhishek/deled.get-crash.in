Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Use of I.C.T. for Students.

Students use ICT for self-learning, projects, practice tests, e-content, career information, and responsible communication. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Self-learning and E-content.

Learning with ICT is active when students solve, create, discuss, and reflect; it is passive when they only watch.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

ICT supports revision through videos, quizzes, dictionaries, maps, and simulations. Students can collect local data, take photos with permission, and present findings creatively.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

Ask groups to record household water use for one day, enter totals in a table, and discuss conservation habits.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

An example connects the abstract concept with child experience and makes the answer clear.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Use of I.C.T. for Students with examples.
  • Students use ICT for self-learning, projects, practice tests, e-content, career information, and responsible communication.
  • ICT supports revision through videos, quizzes, dictionaries, maps, and simulations.
  • Students can collect local data, take photos with permission, and present findings creatively.
  • Online tests give immediate practice but must not replace written expression and oral discussion.
  • Classroom example: Ask groups to record household water use for one day, enter totals in a table, and discuss conservation habits.
2. Explain Self-learning, E-content, and Online test.
  • Self-learning: Learning at one's own pace with resources and feedback. Example: Watching a lesson again before homework
  • E-content: Digital learning content in text, audio, video, or interactive form. Example: Interactive multiplication game
  • Online test: Assessment attempted through a digital device or platform. Example: Chapter MCQ quiz
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • Ask groups to record household water use for one day, enter totals in a table, and discuss conservation habits.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Learning with ICT is active when students solve, create, discuss, and reflect; it is passive when they only watch.
  • Online information becomes knowledge only after selection, understanding, and application.
