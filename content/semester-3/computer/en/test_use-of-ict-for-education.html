Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the short meaning of Use of I.C.T. for Education.

ICT expands education through multimedia, smart classes, distance learning, e-labs, web portals, and teleconferencing. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write one difference related to Smart class and Multimedia.

Smart class enriches face-to-face teaching; distance education supports learning away from the school building.

3. Write two key points from the chapter.

ICT helps reach learners who cannot attend regular classes due to distance, illness, or emergencies. Multimedia supports difficult concepts by making invisible processes visible.

4. Write one primary-classroom use of this topic.

For a water cycle lesson, use a short animation, ask learners to draw the cycle, and end with local rainfall observations.

5. Why is an example important in a good answer?

An example connects the abstract concept with child experience and makes the answer clear.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions

1. Explain the main concepts of Use of I.C.T. for Education with examples.
  • ICT expands education through multimedia, smart classes, distance learning, e-labs, web portals, and teleconferencing.
  • ICT helps reach learners who cannot attend regular classes due to distance, illness, or emergencies.
  • Multimedia supports difficult concepts by making invisible processes visible.
  • Teleconferencing and video lessons allow expert interaction beyond the local school.
  • Classroom example: For a water cycle lesson, use a short animation, ask learners to draw the cycle, and end with local rainfall observations.
2. Explain Smart class, Multimedia, and E-lab.
  • Smart class: Classroom where digital resources support teacher-led learning. Example: Showing a map animation in social science
  • Multimedia: Combined use of text, image, sound, animation, and video. Example: Video plus captions plus diagram
  • E-lab: Digital or simulated laboratory for practice and demonstration. Example: Virtual circuit building
3. Write an answer connecting this chapter with the primary classroom.
  • For a water cycle lesson, use a short animation, ask learners to draw the cycle, and end with local rainfall observations.
  • The teacher should use local examples, simple language, activity, and revision.
  • Assessment should observe both understanding and expression.
4. Explain two differences or links from the chapter.
  • Smart class enriches face-to-face teaching; distance education supports learning away from the school building.
  • E-content is material; e-learning is the whole process of using that material with guidance and feedback.
