Role of Information and Communication Technology

ICT connects information, communication tools, digital devices, and classroom learning.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Data Raw facts such as numbers, names, symbols, or observations. Attendance numbers before analysis
Information Processed data that becomes meaningful for decision making. Monthly attendance percentage
Communication Sharing ideas, feelings, or information between two or more persons. Teacher explaining homework through a message
ICT Use of computers, networks, media, and communication tools for information work. Digital lesson with video and quiz
Educational technology Planned use of tools and methods to improve teaching and learning. Projector, audio clip, worksheet, and feedback

Key Points

  • ICT makes learning more visual, interactive, and accessible.
  • It supports storing, processing, presenting, and sharing information quickly.
  • In schools it helps teaching, assessment, communication, record keeping, and inclusive learning.
  • A teacher must select ICT according to age, objective, language level, and available resources.
  • ICT is useful only when it supports clear pedagogy, not when it becomes mere display.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Data is unprocessed fact; information is processed and useful meaning.
Point 2 Technology is the tool; pedagogy is the learning plan that gives the tool educational value.
