Use of I.C.T. for Students

Students use ICT for self-learning, projects, practice tests, e-content, career information, and responsible communication.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Self-learning Learning at one's own pace with resources and feedback. Watching a lesson again before homework
E-content Digital learning content in text, audio, video, or interactive form. Interactive multiplication game
Online test Assessment attempted through a digital device or platform. Chapter MCQ quiz
Digital project Assignment prepared with digital research, media, and presentation. Local plants slideshow
Cyber safety Safe and ethical behaviour while using digital tools. Not sharing passwords

Key Points

  • ICT supports revision through videos, quizzes, dictionaries, maps, and simulations.
  • Students can collect local data, take photos with permission, and present findings creatively.
  • Online tests give immediate practice but must not replace written expression and oral discussion.
  • Students need guidance on reliable sources, screen time, privacy, and respectful communication.
  • ICT helps learners with disabilities through audio, captions, magnification, and alternative input.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Learning with ICT is active when students solve, create, discuss, and reflect; it is passive when they only watch.
Point 2 Online information becomes knowledge only after selection, understanding, and application.
