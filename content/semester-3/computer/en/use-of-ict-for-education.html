Use of I.C.T. for Education

ICT expands education through multimedia, smart classes, distance learning, e-labs, web portals, and teleconferencing.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Smart class Classroom where digital resources support teacher-led learning. Showing a map animation in social science
Multimedia Combined use of text, image, sound, animation, and video. Video plus captions plus diagram
E-lab Digital or simulated laboratory for practice and demonstration. Virtual circuit building
Distance education Learning where teacher and learner are separated by place or time. Recorded lesson with assignment
Web portal Organised website for resources, notices, learning material, or services. School learning portal

Key Points

  • ICT helps reach learners who cannot attend regular classes due to distance, illness, or emergencies.
  • Multimedia supports difficult concepts by making invisible processes visible.
  • Teleconferencing and video lessons allow expert interaction beyond the local school.
  • Educational portals organise content, assignments, notices, and progress information.
  • Equity issues such as electricity, device access, language, and disability support must be planned.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 Smart class enriches face-to-face teaching; distance education supports learning away from the school building.
Point 2 E-content is material; e-learning is the whole process of using that material with guidance and feedback.
