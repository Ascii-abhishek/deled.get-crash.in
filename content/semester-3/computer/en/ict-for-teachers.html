I.C.T. for Teachers

ICT helps teachers plan lessons, update knowledge, communicate, assess, and create learning material.

Exam Snapshot

Key term Meaning Example
Digital content Text, audio, video, animation, worksheet, or quiz used for learning. A recorded poem with worksheet
Search skill Finding reliable information using suitable keywords and source checking. Searching NCERT activity on evaporation
Online collaboration Working with experts, peers, or students through digital platforms. Teacher group sharing TLM ideas
Assessment tool Digital method for checking learning and giving feedback. Online quiz with instant score
Professional development Continuous learning by teachers through courses, webinars, and reading. DIKSHA module or webinar

Key Points

  • Teachers can prepare lesson plans, worksheets, presentations, rubrics, and remedial material.
  • ICT gives access to updated policies, textbooks, open resources, and expert lectures.
  • It improves parent communication and student feedback when used responsibly.
  • Teachers must verify sources, protect student privacy, and avoid overdependence on ready-made material.
  • Low-resource ICT can include mobile audio, offline videos, printed screenshots, and shared devices.

Concepts And Differences

Difference or link Exam-ready line
Point 1 ICT-literate teacher operates tools; ICT-integrating teacher connects tools with objectives, activities, and assessment.
Point 2 Open resources are free to access, but they still need classroom adaptation.
