Section 2: Short Answer Questions (10 Questions)

1. Write the short meaning of Role of Information and Communication Technology.

ICT connects information, communication tools, digital devices, and classroom learning. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

2. Write the short meaning of I.C.T. for Teachers.

3. Write the short meaning of Use of I.C.T. for Education.

4. Write the short meaning of Use of I.C.T. for Students.

Students use ICT for self-learning, projects, practice tests, e-content, career information, and responsible communication. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

5. Write the short meaning of Use of I.C.T. in Office Work.

Office ICT improves documentation, calculation, presentation, databases, communication, storage, and cyber safety. It can be explained through definition, purpose, and classroom use.

6. Write one difference related to Data and Information.

Data is unprocessed fact; information is processed and useful meaning.

7. Write one difference related to Digital content and Search skill.

ICT-literate teacher operates tools; ICT-integrating teacher connects tools with objectives, activities, and assessment.

8. Write one difference related to Smart class and Multimedia.

Smart class enriches face-to-face teaching; distance education supports learning away from the school building.

9. Write one difference related to Self-learning and E-content.

Learning with ICT is active when students solve, create, discuss, and reflect; it is passive when they only watch.

10. Write one difference related to Word processor and Spreadsheet.

Word is best for text documents; Excel is best for calculations and tables; PowerPoint is best for visual presentation.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the main concepts of Role of Information and Communication Technology with examples.
  • ICT connects information, communication tools, digital devices, and classroom learning.
  • ICT makes learning more visual, interactive, and accessible.
  • It supports storing, processing, presenting, and sharing information quickly.
  • In schools it helps teaching, assessment, communication, record keeping, and inclusive learning.
  • Classroom example: Use a short picture, an audio clip, and a five-question quiz to teach traffic signs; then ask children to identify signs near school.
2. Explain the main concepts of I.C.T. for Teachers with examples.
  • ICT helps teachers plan lessons, update knowledge, communicate, assess, and create learning material.
  • Teachers can prepare lesson plans, worksheets, presentations, rubrics, and remedial material.
  • ICT gives access to updated policies, textbooks, open resources, and expert lectures.
  • It improves parent communication and student feedback when used responsibly.
  • Classroom example: Before a fractions lesson, the teacher downloads a local-language activity, prepares paper fraction strips, and uses a quick phone quiz for revision.
3. Explain the main concepts of Use of I.C.T. for Education with examples.
  • ICT expands education through multimedia, smart classes, distance learning, e-labs, web portals, and teleconferencing.
  • ICT helps reach learners who cannot attend regular classes due to distance, illness, or emergencies.
  • Multimedia supports difficult concepts by making invisible processes visible.
  • Teleconferencing and video lessons allow expert interaction beyond the local school.
  • Classroom example: For a water cycle lesson, use a short animation, ask learners to draw the cycle, and end with local rainfall observations.
4. Explain the main concepts of Use of I.C.T. for Students with examples.
  • Students use ICT for self-learning, projects, practice tests, e-content, career information, and responsible communication.
  • ICT supports revision through videos, quizzes, dictionaries, maps, and simulations.
  • Students can collect local data, take photos with permission, and present findings creatively.
  • Online tests give immediate practice but must not replace written expression and oral discussion.
  • Classroom example: Ask groups to record household water use for one day, enter totals in a table, and discuss conservation habits.
5. Explain the main concepts of Use of I.C.T. in Office Work with examples.
  • Office ICT improves documentation, calculation, presentation, databases, communication, storage, and cyber safety.
  • Word processing supports notices, letters, certificates, minutes, and reports.
  • Spreadsheets simplify marks, attendance, budgets, inventories, and charts.
  • Databases help maintain student, library, scholarship, and stock records.
  • Classroom example: Maintain a class reading log in a spreadsheet and show children a simple bar chart of books read by the class.
