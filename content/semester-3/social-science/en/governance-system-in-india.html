Governance System in India

This chapter explains the Constitution-based governance structure: legislature, executive, judiciary, parliament, law-making, and accountability.

Core Points

  • Indian governance is democratic, federal, parliamentary, and constitutional.
  • Separation of powers prevents concentration of authority in one organ.
  • Parliament makes laws, controls finance, and questions the executive.
  • The executive runs administration through ministers, departments, and civil services.
  • Judiciary protects rights and checks unconstitutional actions.
  • Teach through mock parliament, role cards, and local governance examples.

Classroom Connection

Conduct a mock parliament on a school rule. Learners become speaker, ministers, opposition, and members, then track how a bill becomes a rule.

One-minute Revision

  • Constitution: Supreme law that defines government and citizens’ rights.
  • Democracy: Government by elected representatives of the people.
  • Legislature: Organ that makes laws.
  • Executive: Organ that implements laws and policies.
  • Judiciary: Organ that interprets laws and gives justice.
  • Parliament: Union legislature: President, Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha.
