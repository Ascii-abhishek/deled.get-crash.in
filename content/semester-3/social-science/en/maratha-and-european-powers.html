Maratha and European Powers

This chapter explains the rise of Maratha power, entry of European trading companies, and the gradual establishment of British power in India.

Core Points

  • Maratha power grew through mobile warfare, local support, forts, and revenue claims.
  • After Shivaji, the Peshwas expanded influence but confederacy weaknesses increased.
  • European companies first came for trade, then used forts, armies, diplomacy, and wars.
  • British success came through naval strength, finance, diplomacy, and control of Bengal revenue.
  • Indian powers often fought each other, giving companies chances to intervene.
  • Teach with trade-route maps and comparison of Indian and European power strategies.
