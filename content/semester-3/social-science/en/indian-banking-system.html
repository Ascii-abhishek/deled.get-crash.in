Core Points

  • Banks encourage saving and provide secure payment services.
  • RBI supervises banks, controls credit policy, and protects monetary stability.
  • SBI and other public sector banks support welfare schemes, rural access, and financial inclusion.
  • Development banks and specialised institutions support agriculture, industry, housing, and infrastructure.
  • E-banking makes transactions faster, but students must learn password, OTP, and fraud-safety rules.
  • Banking is linked with employment, entrepreneurship, government schemes, and household budgeting.
