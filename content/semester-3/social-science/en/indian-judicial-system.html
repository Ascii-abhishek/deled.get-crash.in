Indian Judicial System

This chapter explains the structure, independence, functions, and access mechanisms of the Indian judiciary from Supreme Court to local courts.

Core Points

  • The judiciary protects rule of law, fundamental rights, and constitutional limits.
  • India has an integrated judicial system with Supreme Court at the top.
  • High Courts supervise subordinate courts in their jurisdiction.
  • Civil and criminal cases follow different procedures and remedies.
  • PIL and Lok Adalat improve access to justice for common people.
  • Teach with court hierarchy charts and simple dispute-resolution role play.
