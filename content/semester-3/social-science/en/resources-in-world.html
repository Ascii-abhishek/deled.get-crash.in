Resources in World

This chapter explains resource meaning, types, conservation, and world patterns of natural, mineral, energy, transport, and communication resources.

Core Points

  • A thing becomes a resource when humans know its use and can access it.
  • Resources are unevenly distributed because geology, climate, relief, and technology differ.
  • Coal, petroleum, and minerals are non-renewable, so conservation is necessary.
  • Solar, wind, water, and biomass are renewable but still need planned use.
  • Transport and communication connect resources, markets, people, and ideas.
  • Teach through local resource mapping and reduce-reuse-recycle activities.
