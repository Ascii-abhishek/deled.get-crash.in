Section 2: Short Answer Questions

1. Write the main purpose of Indian Banking System.

This chapter revises the role of banks, RBI, public sector banks, development banks, rural banking, and digital banking in India.

2. Define Bank and RBI.

Bank: An institution that accepts deposits, gives loans, and provides payment services. RBI: The central bank that regulates money, credit, banks, and currency issue in India.

3. Write two classroom uses of Indian Banking System.

Use a class saving-box activity: children list deposits, withdrawals, interest, safety rules, and why a bank is more reliable than keeping money at home. Oral practice, tables, pair work, and quick feedback can also be added.

4. Why is a comparison table useful in this chapter?

It separates similar concepts clearly, so learners understand definition, example, and use without confusion.

5. Write two suitable assessment methods for this chapter.

Oral questioning, short written exercises, matching tasks, form or word identification, classroom performance, and homework review are useful methods.

3. Explain the key terms and concepts with examples.
  • Bank: An institution that accepts deposits, gives loans, and provides payment services.
  • RBI: The central bank that regulates money, credit, banks, and currency issue in India.
  • SBI: A major public sector bank with wide urban and rural reach.
  • Commercial bank: A bank that mobilises deposits and gives loans for trade, industry, and personal needs.
  • Cooperative bank: A bank organised on cooperation, often serving local members and small borrowers.
  • Regional rural bank: A bank created to expand banking and credit in rural areas.
