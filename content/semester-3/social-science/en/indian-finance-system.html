Core Points

  • Indian finance system links households, banks, markets, government, and development needs.
  • Budget shows priorities: education, health, defence, infrastructure, welfare, and debt service.
  • Taxes finance public goods and services, but fairness and efficiency matter.
  • Deficit may support development, but excessive deficit creates pressure.
  • Financial institutions mobilise savings and provide credit for production and welfare.
  • Teach with a classroom budget: income sources, spending heads, saving, and deficit.
