Section 2: Short Answer Questions (10 Questions)

1. Define Babur with an example.

Founder of Mughal rule in India after the First Battle of Panipat.

2. Define Shivaji with an example.

Founder of strong Maratha power in western India.

3. Define Biosphere with an example.

Zone of Earth where life exists.

4. Define Resource with an example.

Anything useful to humans and usable with knowledge and technology.

5. Define Location with an example.

India lies in South Asia, mostly in the Northern and Eastern hemispheres.

6. Define Constitution with an example.

Supreme law that defines government and citizens’ rights.

7. Define Judiciary with an example.

Organ of government that gives justice and interprets law.

8. Define Public finance with an example.

Study of government income, expenditure, debt, and budgeting.

9. Write the main purpose of Indian Banking System.

This chapter revises the role of banks, RBI, public sector banks, development banks, rural banking, and digital banking in India.

10. Write the difference between Humayun and Akbar.

Akbar: Policy of broad alliance and religious tolerance. Aurangzeb: More orthodox policy and long Deccan wars.

Section 3: Long Answer Questions (5 Questions)

1. Explain the main concepts of Mughal Empire in India with examples.

A good answer should include definition, example, process, and classroom use.

  • The Mughal Empire began after Babur defeated Ibrahim Lodi in 1526.
  • Akbar’s policies of alliance, tolerance, and administration gave stability to the empire.
  • Mughal study = rulers + administration + culture + decline.
  • Ask learners to build a ruler timeline and place one administrative, cultural, and political feature under each major Mughal ruler.
2. Explain the main concepts of Maratha and European Powers with examples.

A good answer should include definition, example, process, and classroom use.

  • Maratha power grew through mobile warfare, local support, forts, and revenue claims.
  • After Shivaji, the Peshwas expanded influence but confederacy weaknesses increased.
  • Trade plus army plus revenue slowly became power.
  • Use a map of India to mark Maratha centres and European trading posts, then discuss how trade routes became political routes.
3. Explain the main concepts of Biosphere and Life Styles with examples.

A good answer should include definition, example, process, and classroom use.

  • Biosphere includes land, water, and air portions that support life.
  • Natural regions are shaped mainly by latitude, altitude, rainfall, temperature, and soil.
  • Environment shapes life, and people adapt to environment.
  • Let groups design a natural-region poster showing climate, vegetation, animals, clothing, housing, and occupation.
4. Explain the main concepts of Resources in World with examples.

A good answer should include definition, example, process, and classroom use.

  • A thing becomes a resource when humans know its use and can access it.
  • Resources are unevenly distributed because geology, climate, relief, and technology differ.
  • Usefulness + knowledge + access = resource.
  • Students prepare a village or neighbourhood resource map and classify each item as natural, human-made, renewable, or non-renewable.
5. Explain the main concepts of India At A Glance with examples.

A good answer should include definition, example, process, and classroom use.

  • India’s location gives it strategic importance in the Indian Ocean region.
  • The Himalayas influence climate, rivers, forests, and security.
  • Map practice turns India study into memory.
  • Use blank India maps repeatedly: one day physical divisions, next day rivers, then crops, minerals, industries, and trade routes.
