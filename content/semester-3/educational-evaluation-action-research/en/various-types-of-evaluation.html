Various Types of Evaluation

Evaluation is classified by purpose, time, reference point, and method. D.El.Ed exams often ask diagnostic, formative, summative, norm-referenced, criterion-referenced, oral, written, and practical evaluation.

Exam Snapshot

TermFast meaningExam point
Diagnostic evaluationFinds learning difficulties and causes before or during teaching.Used for remedial teaching.
Formative evaluationChecks learning during teaching for improvement.Low-stakes and frequent.
Summative evaluationJudges achievement at the end of unit or term.Used for grading/reporting.
Placement evaluationFinds entry level before instruction.Helps grouping and starting point.
Norm-referenced evaluationCompares learner with group performance.Rank and percentile are examples.
Criterion-referenced evaluationCompares learner with fixed learning criteria.Useful for mastery learning.
Oral evaluationUses spoken questions and responses.Good for language, confidence, quick checks.
Written evaluationUses written answers, worksheets, tests.Needs clear questions and marking scheme.
Practical evaluationAssesses skills through actual performance.Science experiment or map drawing.
Self evaluationLearner judges own work using criteria.Builds responsibility and reflection.

Core Points

  • Different types are not rivals; a good teacher combines them according to need.
  • Diagnostic evaluation should lead to specific remedial action.
  • Formative evaluation is most useful when feedback comes quickly.
  • Summative evaluation should not be the only basis of understanding a child.
  • Criterion-referenced evaluation is especially helpful in foundational literacy and numeracy.
  • Practical and oral evaluation make assessment inclusive for children who struggle with long writing.

Quick Memory Hook

Purpose decides type: diagnose, improve, certify, compare, or perform.

Classroom Connection

Before division, ask diagnostic questions on multiplication tables; during teaching use slate answers for formative evaluation; at the end use a short written test and one practical sharing activity.
