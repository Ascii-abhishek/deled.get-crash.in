Research and Action Research

Research is systematic inquiry for knowledge; action research is a teacher-led inquiry to solve an immediate classroom problem. For D.El.Ed, focus on problem identification, plan, action, observation, reflection, and improvement.

Core Points

  • Action research is local, practical, flexible, and improvement-oriented.
  • It does not require large samples or complex statistics, but it needs honesty and evidence.
  • The cycle is: identify problem -> plan action -> implement -> observe -> reflect -> revise.
  • A good classroom problem is specific: not children are weak, but ten Class 3 children read two-letter words slowly.
  • Data can include test scores, notebook work, attendance, observation notes, and child interviews.
  • The final report should mention limitations and next steps.

Quick Memory Hook

Action research is a teacher asking: What is the problem, what will I try, what changed?

Classroom Connection

Problem: Class 2 learners confuse b and d. Action: 15 days of multisensory letter tracing, mirror reading, and picture-word cards. Evidence: pre-post reading list, error tally, and teacher diary.

One-minute Revision

  • Research: Systematic and objective search for knowledge.
  • Action research: Small-scale inquiry by practitioner to improve own practice.
  • Problem identification: Selecting a real, specific, solvable classroom issue.
  • Hypothesis: Tentative idea about possible solution or relation.
  • Action plan: Steps, resources, time, and expected outcomes for intervention.
  • Observation: Careful recording of events during action.

Common Exam Lines
