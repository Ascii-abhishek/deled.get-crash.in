Measurement and Evaluation

This chapter explains how teachers collect evidence of learning and use it to improve instruction. The central distinction is measurement gives numbers, evaluation gives value judgement and decisions.

Exam Snapshot

TermFast meaningExam point
MeasurementQuantitative description of achievement or trait.Example: 18 out of 25 marks.
EvaluationJudging worth of learning using evidence and criteria.It supports promotion, remediation, and planning.
AssessmentContinuous process of gathering information about learning.Can be oral, written, practical, or observational.
TestTool or situation used to measure learning.Examples: unit test, oral quiz, worksheet.
CriterionStandard used to judge performance.Rubrics make criteria visible.
ReliabilityConsistency of scores or results.A reliable test gives similar results in similar conditions.
ValidityTest measures what it claims to measure.A reading test should test reading, not memory alone.
NormReference point for comparing performance.Can be class average or expected level.
FeedbackInformation that helps learner improve.Specific feedback is better than only marks.
RemediationSupport given after diagnosing learning gaps.It connects evaluation with teaching improvement.

Core Points

  • Measurement is narrow and numerical; evaluation is broad and decision-oriented.
  • Evaluation should be linked with learning objectives and classroom teaching.
  • A teacher uses assessment before teaching, during teaching, and after teaching.
  • Good evaluation covers knowledge, understanding, skills, attitudes, and application.
  • Marks without feedback do not improve learning; descriptive feedback tells the next step.
  • Evaluation should reduce fear and guide improvement, especially in elementary classes.

Quick Memory Hook

Measure to know the level; evaluate to decide the next step.

Classroom Connection

After a fraction lesson, give five quick problems, observe who uses concrete strips correctly, ask one oral question, and note common errors. Group children for reteaching equivalent fractions the next day.
