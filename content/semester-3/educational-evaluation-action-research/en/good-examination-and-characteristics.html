Good Examination and Its Characteristics

A good examination measures intended learning fairly and usefully. It is valid, reliable, objective, balanced, practical, and helpful for improving teaching-learning.

Core Points

  • The examination should match learning objectives, not surprise children with unrelated questions.
  • Question paper should include knowledge, understanding, application, and skill-based items.
  • Instructions, marks, time, and expected answer length must be clear.
  • A good test has a balanced mix of easy, average, and challenging questions.
  • Language should be age-appropriate and free from ambiguity.
  • After the exam, analysis should guide remedial teaching.

Quick Memory Hook

Good exam = right content + fair scoring + useful feedback.

Classroom Connection

For Class 5 EVS, prepare a blueprint: 30 percent recall, 40 percent understanding, 20 percent application, 10 percent activity/diagram. Include local examples and a marking scheme for descriptive answers.

One-minute Revision

  • Validity: Degree to which a test measures intended learning.
  • Reliability: Consistency of results across occasions or scorers.
  • Objectivity: Scoring does not depend on personal bias.
  • Comprehensiveness: Covers all important objectives and content areas.
  • Discrimination: Ability to distinguish high and low achievers.
  • Practicability: Test is possible within time, resources, and class conditions.
