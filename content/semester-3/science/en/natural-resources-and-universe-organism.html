Natural Resources and Universe Organism

This chapter connects natural resources, their conservation, and the larger universe that supports life on Earth. Study renewable and non-renewable resources, solar system basics, and the responsibility of sustainable living.

Core Points

  • Natural resources are the base of food, shelter, energy, transport, and industry.
  • Overuse causes depletion, pollution, deforestation, soil erosion, and climate stress.
  • Conservation methods include rainwater harvesting, afforestation, recycling, energy saving, and controlled mining.
  • Earth is suitable for life because of water, oxygen, atmosphere, sunlight, and moderate temperature.
  • Universe study builds curiosity but should be connected with Earth care.
  • Primary teaching should use local resources: school garden, soil samples, water sources, sky observation.

Quick Memory Hook

From soil under feet to stars overhead, responsible use is the lesson.

Classroom Connection

Make a resource audit of the school: water taps, electricity use, paper use, trees, waste. Children suggest one conservation action and observe the night sky for Moon phases over a week.

One-minute Revision

  • Natural resource: Material or energy from nature useful to humans.
  • Renewable resource: Resource replenished naturally in short time if used wisely.
  • Non-renewable resource: Resource formed over very long time and limited in stock.
  • Conservation: Careful protection and wise use of resources.
  • Biodiversity: Variety of plants, animals, and microorganisms.
  • Solar system: Sun and objects moving around it.
