Heat, Light and Sound

Heat, light, and sound are forms of energy experienced every day. This chapter covers transfer of heat, reflection and refraction of light, sound production, propagation, pitch, loudness, and safe use.

Core Points

  • Heat and temperature are related but not the same: heat is energy, temperature is degree of hotness.
  • Dark and rough surfaces absorb more heat; shiny surfaces reflect more heat.
  • Light travels in straight lines in a uniform medium and forms shadows when blocked.
  • Sound is produced by vibration and cannot travel through vacuum.
  • Noise pollution harms hearing, concentration, sleep, and health.
  • Teach through safe activities: shadow play, mirror reflection, spoon conduction, tuning fork or rubber band vibration.

Quick Memory Hook

Heat flows, light shows, sound vibrates.

Classroom Connection

Set up three stations: metal and wooden spoons in warm water for conduction, mirror and torch for reflection, rubber band box for sound vibration. Children record observation and safety rule at each station.

One-minute Revision
