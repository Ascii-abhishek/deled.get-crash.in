Non-Communicable Diseases

Non-communicable diseases do not spread from one person to another. They are often linked with lifestyle, heredity, environment, ageing, and long-term body changes. Prevention and healthy habits are central.

Core Points

  • NCDs are not caused by casual contact, sharing food, or sitting together.
  • Major risk factors are unhealthy diet, lack of exercise, tobacco, alcohol, stress, pollution, and heredity.
  • Healthy habits from childhood reduce future risk: balanced diet, play, sleep, hygiene, emotional expression.
  • Schools can promote mid-day meal awareness, physical activity, yoga, clean drinking water, and tobacco-free campus.
  • Some NCDs need lifelong management; avoid stigma and blame.
  • First aid and referral are important for asthma attack, fainting, severe allergy, or injury.

Quick Memory Hook

NCDs do not spread by touch, but healthy habits can spread by example.

Classroom Connection

Run a health habit chart for one week: water, fruit/vegetable, outdoor play, sleep, screen time. Children reflect without shame and set one improvement goal.
