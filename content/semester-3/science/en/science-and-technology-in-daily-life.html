Science and Technology in Daily Life

Science explains natural events through observation and evidence; technology applies scientific knowledge to solve practical problems. Daily life examples make science meaningful for primary learners.

Core Points

  • Daily science includes cooking, cleaning, transport, health, farming, electricity, weather, and communication.
  • Technology has benefits and risks; students should learn safe and responsible use.
  • Scientific method encourages questioning: What happened? Why? How can we test it?
  • Local examples such as handpump, pressure cooker, bicycle, mobile phone, and solar lamp make lessons concrete.
  • Teachers should connect science with health, environment, safety, and work education.
  • A scientific attitude fights superstition by asking for evidence respectfully.

Quick Memory Hook

Science asks why; technology asks how to use it wisely.

Classroom Connection

Ask children to list five technologies at home. Classify them into health, communication, transport, food, and energy. Discuss one safety rule and one environmental concern for each group.

One-minute Revision

  • Science: Systematic study of nature through observation, experiment, and reasoning.
  • Technology: Application of knowledge to design tools, processes, or systems.
  • Scientific attitude: Curiosity, objectivity, open-mindedness, and evidence-based thinking.
  • Observation: Careful use of senses or instruments to collect information.
  • Experiment: Planned activity to test an idea.
  • Energy use: Use of electricity, fuel, sunlight, or food energy in life.
