Pressure and Scientific Instruments

Pressure is force acting per unit area. Many daily tools and scientific instruments work by pressure, fluid pressure, atmospheric pressure, heat, or measurement principles.

Core Points

  • Pressure increases when force increases and decreases when area increases.
  • Sharp knives, nails, and needles have small area, so they produce large pressure.
  • Wide tyres, snow shoes, and school bag straps reduce pressure by increasing area.
  • Liquids exert pressure in all directions and pressure increases with depth.
  • Pascal’s law explains hydraulic lift and hydraulic brake.
  • Scientific instruments must be read at eye level to avoid parallax error.

Quick Memory Hook

Same force, smaller area, greater pressure.

Classroom Connection

Let children press a pencil from the sharp end and blunt end on clay. Compare marks and connect with P = F/A. Then show pictures of wide straps and sharp tools.
